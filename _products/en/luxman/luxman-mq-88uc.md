## Overview

The LUXMAN MQ-88uC is a stereo power amplifier using four KT88 vacuum tubes in a push-pull triode configuration, introduced around 2018–2019 as a revival of the company's 1969 MQ-60. It replicates the MQ-60's Mullard 5-20 compatible circuit topology and uses OY-15-type output transformers reproduced from a 1960s–1970s LUXMAN design. Rated at 25W + 25W with separate 4Ω, 8Ω, and 16Ω output taps, it is designed and manufactured in Japan. Current US market price is 5,995 USD [1].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

No independent third-party measurements for the MQ-88uC exist as of the review date. All performance data are manufacturer-published specifications [1].

Manufacturer specifications state frequency response of 20Hz–20kHz (+0/−0.5dB) and S/N ratio of 105dB (IHF-A weighted). THD is specified at 0.1% at 1kHz/1W — a distortion level at which audible degradation is a concern — and wideband THD across 20Hz–20kHz reaches 0.5% at 1W, substantially worse. Contemporary solid-state amplifiers routinely measure below 0.001% THD under equivalent conditions, representing a difference of more than a hundredfold. Damping factor and crosstalk are not documented in manufacturer-published specifications. Without independent measurement verification, additional uncertainty applies to all stated values.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The MQ-88uC uses the Mullard 5-20 push-pull topology, a publicly documented circuit from the 1950s–1960s, with JJ Electronic KT88 output tubes and OY-15-type output transformers reproduced from a 1960s–1970s LUXMAN design. No proprietary patents have been identified for any aspect of the amplifier. All core technologies are mature and freely replicable without licensing.

Two factors warrant credit: the amplifier is confirmed as an in-house design developed by a 40-year engineering veteran, and LUXMAN's continuous vacuum tube amplifier manufacturing represents a genuine accumulation of technical know-how. These are offset by multiple factors: all core technologies are outdated relative to current amplifier engineering; the product has no digital, software, or DSP integration of any kind; any competent manufacturer could replicate the circuit topology without meaningful technical barrier; and the transition from the predecessor MQ-88u to the MQ-88uC produced no improvement in rated output power, remaining at 25W + 25W, with changes limited to capacitor type and manufacturing location.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.0} $$

The MQ-88uC is priced at 5,995 USD [1]. The Fosi Audio ZA3 stereo amplifier (with 48V/5A power supply) is available at 149.99 USD [2] and provides equivalent or better performance on all documented measurement axes.

The ZA3 offers stereo 2-channel amplification, RCA line input, volume control, and direct input mode — functions equivalent to the MQ-88uC. Performance comparison on available data: frequency response deviation is ±0.1dB (20Hz–20kHz, manufacturer specification) versus the MQ-88uC's +0/−0.5dB; THD is below 0.006% versus 0.1% at 1kHz/1W; S/N ratio is specified at ≥106dB versus 105dB IHF-A. SINAD of 89dB for the ZA3 has been confirmed by independent third-party measurement [3]. The MQ-88uC's specifications are unverified by independent measurement, introducing provisional uncertainty into the S/N and frequency response comparisons, but the THD gap — more than sixteenfold in the ZA3's favor — is unambiguous under any reasonable reading of the available data.

CP = 149.99 USD / 5,995 USD = 0.025, rounded to 0.0.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

LUXMAN supports the MQ-88uC through a global authorized distributor network including North America (Rhythm Distribution) and Germany (LUXMAN Deutschland) [1]. The main-unit warranty in North America is estimated at approximately 3 years based on available documentation, with vacuum tubes covered for a shorter separate period.

Structural reliability considerations are inherent to the vacuum tube design. KT88 output tubes are consumable components with a typical service life of 2,000–5,000 hours, requiring periodic user replacement at additional cost. The amplifier incorporates no automatic tube-protection or shutdown circuit, meaning a failing output tube can cause cascading damage to internal components before the fault is detected by the user. The eight-tube complement and hand-wired construction represent a higher inherent complexity than solid-state designs. No statistical failure rate data is publicly available, and no product recalls or official service bulletins have been issued.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The MQ-88uC explicitly prioritizes heritage reproduction over measured performance: it replicates a 1969 circuit based on a 1950s topology, at a 5,995 USD price point, in a market where solid-state alternatives at a fraction of the price deliver superior measured performance across every documented axis [2][3].

Cost allocation is weighted toward non-performance elements: heritage chassis aesthetics, die-cast aluminum transformer housings, and "Made in Japan" positioning each contribute to the price without documented impact on measured audio performance. Multiple manufacturer claims assert audible effects for elements that have not been subjected to controlled testing — the "transparent timbre" attributed to triode connection mode, the "supple, natural tone" attributed to Nichicon film capacitors, and the audio quality improvement attributed to shortened internal wiring are presented as product facts rather than tested outcomes. The MQ-88u-to-MQ-88uC transition produced no measured output power gain, with changes limited to component substitution and manufacturing relocation. The design philosophy is intentionally conservative, oriented toward replicating a 60-year-old circuit rather than pursuing any modern audio engineering approach.

## Advice

Purchasers selecting primarily on measured audio performance will find that modern solid-state stereo amplifiers substantially outperform the MQ-88uC across all documented measurement axes at a fraction of the price. The Fosi Audio ZA3 at 149.99 USD [2], with SINAD independently verified at 89dB [3] and THD below 0.006%, is one such option.

The MQ-88uC is suited for users who specifically seek vacuum tube amplification — for the operational and physical characteristics of tube equipment, or for speaker systems specifically requiring a transformer-coupled output stage. For those users, LUXMAN's Japan manufacturing, global authorized distributor support, and estimated 3-year main-unit warranty are relevant advantages. Users selecting on the basis of measured fidelity should direct their budget elsewhere.
