## Overview

The Luxman L-507f integrated amplifier, released in October 2001 at ¥300,000, represents Luxman's engineering philosophy during the early 2000s era. This 130W-per-channel amplifier incorporates the company's proprietary ODNF (Optimally Designed Negative Feedback) circuit technology, designed to achieve ultra-wide bandwidth and low distortion through selective feedback of only distortion components. The amplifier features comprehensive connectivity including MM/MC phono stages, balanced XLR inputs, and power amplifier bypass capability. With its substantial 22.5kg construction utilizing custom Luxman components throughout, the L-507f exemplified the company's commitment to high-quality analog engineering during its production period.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The L-507f incorporates Luxman's proprietary ODNF feedback technology, representing significant circuit innovation for its 2001 release period. The ODNF circuit achieves ultra-wide bandwidth and high slew rate by feeding back only distortion components rather than the entire signal, eliminating phase correction requirements of conventional negative feedback designs. This implementation demonstrates sophisticated understanding of amplifier feedback theory and represents genuine technological advancement within analog design constraints. The amplifier utilizes extensive custom components including transformers, resistors, and capacitors specifically designed for Luxman applications, showing investment in proprietary development. However, the purely analog approach reflects 2001-era technology priorities rather than current innovation directions, resulting in a -0.1 adjustment for analog/mechanical-only implementation. Modern amplifier designs increasingly integrate digital processing, DSP capabilities, software control, and advanced connectivity features that the L-507f entirely lacks. While the ODNF technology provided competitive differentiation during its era and demonstrates technical sophistication, current technology leadership requires broader integration of digital technologies, software capabilities, and modern connectivity standards that would define contemporary innovation excellence.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Current used market price for the L-507f is approximately 3000 USD based on recent marketplace listings from HifiShark and Reverb platforms. The Arcam SA30 at approximately 950 USD provides equivalent-or-better functionality across key user-facing criteria [1]. Equipped with comprehensive MM/MC phono stage matching the L-507f's cartridge compatibility, the SA30 delivers identical 130W x 2 into 8Ω power output while incorporating extensive advanced functionality including streaming capabilities, Dirac Live room correction, MQA decoding, Roon endpoint support, Google Cast, and comprehensive digital connectivity options far exceeding the purely analog L-507f feature set. Both amplifiers provide equivalent analog power performance and phono stage capabilities, while the SA30 adds substantial digital functionality, software-based room correction, and modern streaming integration absent from the L-507f. The SA30's measured performance demonstrates superior specifications with THD of 0.002% at 1kHz versus the L-507f's 0.005%, and signal-to-noise ratio of 111.6dB versus 106dB. Cost-performance calculation: CP = 950 USD ÷ 3000 USD = 0.32.
