## Overview

The Groove 40th Anniversary Edition is a limited-production portable USB DAC and headphone amplifier released by Apogee Electronics in March 2024 to mark the company's 40th year [1]. Production is capped at 250 units worldwide. The device connects to a host via mini-USB with USB-A and USB-C adapter cables included, outputs to a single 3.5mm headphone jack, and operates entirely from USB bus power. Built from aluminum and assembled in the USA (95 × 30 × 16 mm), it upgrades the original 2015 Groove with a 32-bit ESS Sabre DAC (from 24-bit), higher rated output power, and marginally improved distortion and dynamic range specifications, while the fundamental headphone amplifier topology and physical device connector remain unchanged from the 2015 design.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Manufacturer specifications report THD+N of approximately 0.000355% at 600Ω and 0.000891% at 30Ω, a dynamic range of 119 dB A-weighted, and frequency response of ±0.1 dB from 10 Hz to 20 kHz [1]. These figures represent excellent distortion performance and dynamic range under the manufacturer's stated test conditions.

No independent third-party measurements of the 40th Anniversary Edition exist for verification. The device shares its headphone amplifier topology with the original 2015 Groove; Stereophile's measurements of that unit confirmed elevated output impedance across all audio frequencies [4], which causes frequency response deviation when driving typical low-impedance headphones — a limitation the manufacturer itself acknowledges by warning against use with multi-driver balanced armature in-ear monitors. A conservative evaluation applies to all manufacturer-sourced specifications given the absence of independent verification.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The 40th Anniversary Edition is an in-house Apogee design incorporating the company's patented Constant Current Drive headphone amplifier and accumulated professional audio engineering expertise. These represent genuine technical differentiation. However, the core CCD topology and physical mini-USB device connector are carried over from the 2015 original without architectural revision. The ESS Sabre 32-bit DAC is now standard across portable audio products at every price tier including those under 30 USD, and the CCD approach has seen no adoption by competing manufacturers in the nine years since the original Groove's introduction.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The Groove 40th Anniversary Edition is priced at 349 USD [1]. The FiiO KA11, available at 29.99 USD [3], is a bus-powered portable USB DAC and headphone amplifier with USB-C input, 3.5mm output, and Mac/Windows/iOS compatibility. Audio Science Review's formal measurement review [2] confirms the KA11 meets or exceeds the GROOVE's manufacturer-specified performance on all available metrics:

- THD+N: KA11 below 0.0006% at 32Ω vs. GROOVE 0.000891% at 30Ω (KA11 better)
- S/N Ratio: KA11 at least 125 dB A-weighted vs. GROOVE 119 dB A-weighted (KA11 better)
- Frequency Response deviation: ±0.1 dB for both products per respective manufacturer specifications (equivalent)

Volume control on the KA11 is host-side rather than via a dedicated hardware knob. This comparison is provisional as the review target's performance relies solely on manufacturer specifications without independent verification.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The Groove 40th Anniversary Edition carries a 1-year manufacturer warranty. Apogee provides manufacturer-direct global support via an online portal with typical 1–2 business day response times and weekday live chat availability. The device is structurally simple — an aluminum-encased USB DAC with no moving parts — making it inherently resistant to mechanical degradation. No user-updatable firmware exists for this hardware. No statistical failure data is available for this 250-unit limited production run. Some authorized retailers offer extended warranty coverage at point of sale.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The 40th Anniversary Edition is a conservative, incremental refresh of a nine-year-old design: the CCD amplifier topology and mini-USB device connector are unchanged from 2015. The 349 USD price represents a substantial premium over ASR-measured alternatives delivering equal or better distortion and noise performance [2], with the premium driven primarily by limited-edition collector positioning, Made-in-USA production, and cosmetic engraving — none of which contribute to measured audio performance. The CCD topology's elevated output impedance creates documented frequency response incompatibilities with low-impedance headphones and multi-driver IEMs that conventional low-impedance amplifier designs do not share [4]. The "hand-selected and calibrated" marketing claim implies audible benefits from individual component selection without cited scientific support. One substantive positive: the Anniversary Edition achieves measurable improvements over its 2015 predecessor, including higher dynamic range, lower THD+N, and substantially increased rated output power at 30Ω.

## Advice

Users seeking transparent-performance portable USB DAC and headphone amplifier functionality will find ASR-measured alternatives at dramatically lower cost. The FiiO KA11 at 29.99 USD [2][3] delivers measured distortion and noise performance that surpasses the GROOVE's manufacturer-specified values at less than 9% of the price. The Fosi Audio DS2 at 59.99 USD [5] provides ASR-measured SINAD of 109 dB, hardware volume control, and a 4.4mm balanced output at approximately 17% of the price.

The Groove 40th Anniversary Edition is specifically appropriate for users driving high-impedance headphones, where the CCD topology's elevated output impedance has minimal practical frequency response impact, or for collectors for whom the limited-edition production status carries intrinsic value. Users of low-impedance headphones or balanced armature in-ear monitors should account for the documented compatibility constraints of the CCD amplifier design before purchase.
