## Overview

The GRADO Signature S750 is an open-back, over-ear dynamic headphone produced by Grado Labs, a family-owned company founded in Brooklyn, New York in 1953. Priced at 1,695 USD, it occupies the second tier of the Signature series between the S550 (995 USD) and S950 (2,195 USD). The S750 introduces Grado's newly developed S2 50mm dynamic driver with a carbon fiber and paper composite diaphragm, a new B cushion earpad featuring eight engineered airflow slits, and a detachable 4-pin balanced mini XLR cable system — a first at this tier of the Signature line [1].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Manufacturer specifications for the S750 indicate a frequency response range of 4Hz–48kHz and THD below 0.2% at 100dB SPL [1]. No independent third-party measurements have been published for this product as of the review date. The stated THD figure is a rated-output specification and does not represent distortion performance at typical listening levels; the frequency response specification provides a range only, without deviation data from any standard target curve. With no evaluable acoustic performance metrics available from any source, Scientific Validity cannot be assessed.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The S750 is a confirmed in-house design manufactured at Grado's Brooklyn facility. The newly developed S2 driver — featuring a carbon fiber and paper composite diaphragm and copper-plated aluminum voice coil — represents genuine internal engineering effort, and the 0.05 dB driver matching specification reflects meaningful accumulated precision manufacturing know-how [1].

However, the S750 is built entirely on analog and mechanical technology with no digital, DSP, or software integration. Carbon fiber composite diaphragms are a broadly established material category across the headphone industry. No S750-specific or headphone driver patents were identified. The S2 driver was adopted in the lower-priced S550 within approximately five months of the S750's launch [1], indicating short competitive advantage duration. The B cushion's slotted acoustic design, while executing Grado-specific geometry, draws on acoustic principles broadly employed elsewhere in the industry. The positive contributions from in-house design provenance and accumulated manufacturing expertise are offset by the analog-only technology integration penalty and the limited competitive moat.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The S750 carries a 1-year manufacturer warranty covering factory defects — below the 2-year industry average — with domestic return shipping covered by Grado within the US [1]. The all-aluminum, passive analog construction with no active electronics presents an inherently simple and robust design. Manufacturer-direct repair is available at the Grado Labs Brooklyn facility; replacement parts, including cables and cushions, are available directly from the manufacturer [1]. The detachable 4-pin balanced mini XLR cable eliminates the historically most common failure point in Grado's earlier captive-cable designs. No widespread defects, recalls, or service bulletins have been documented for the S750. Grado's over seven decades of continuous manufacturing history constitutes a meaningful reliability track record. Support is primarily US-centric with a distributor model for international customers; no global manufacturer service network exists. No statistical failure-rate data is yet available for this recently launched product.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Grado's philosophy for the S750 is explicitly subjective and traditional: the product is voiced by ear with no DSP, no measurement-based optimization, and no app-based EQ capability [1]. These design choices were made at a time when measurement-optimized headphones, DSP-corrected open-back designs, and app-assisted EQ were commercially mature alternatives across multiple price tiers.

A substantial portion of the 1,695 USD price is attributable to non-acoustic factors: hand-assembly labor, engraved aluminum gimbals, Signature Gold cable branding, and brand heritage premium. The S2 driver — the primary new acoustic engineering element — was adopted in the 995 USD S550 within approximately five months of the S750's launch [1], indicating that the acoustic engineering cost is not uniquely tied to the higher price point. The S750 provides functional audio value and does not employ scientifically irrational technologies. However, the deliberate rejection of modern measurement-based design approaches combined with significant cost allocation to aesthetics and heritage results in limited scientific rationality at this price point.

## Advice

The GRADO Signature S750 is a handcrafted open-back headphone with confirmed in-house engineering and durable passive construction at 1,695 USD. No independent third-party measurements are available as of the review date, making objective verification of acoustic performance claims impossible. At this price, the cost-performance ratio is extremely low: equivalent measured acoustic performance is available at substantially lower cost, with the HiFiMAN HE400se at 109 USD [3] providing confirmed low distortion and approximately 1.98 standard deviation from the Harman target curve per independent measurement [2]. Purchasers for whom Grado's artisanal Brooklyn manufacturing and all-aluminum build are primary considerations may find value in the product; those whose primary criterion is objectively measured acoustic performance per dollar will find significantly better alternatives at substantially lower prices. Audition at an authorized retailer before purchase is advisable given the absence of independent measurement data.
