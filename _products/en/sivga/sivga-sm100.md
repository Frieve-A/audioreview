## Overview

The SIVGA SM100 is a passive wired in-ear earphone from SIVGA, a Dongguan audio manufacturer. Official pages position it as a 3.5 mm wired IEM with microphone, inline control, 0.78 mm 2-pin detachable cable, zinc-alloy housing, and a published 20 Hz-40 kHz frequency response [1][2]. Its overall result is limited by conventional technology and only moderate cost-performance, while measured frequency-response deviation remains acceptable.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Manufacturer specification lists frequency response as 20 Hz-40 kHz [2]. Third-party raw frequency-response datasets recomputed against the Harman IE 2019 v2 target show frequency-response deviation of 2.55 dB STD, 2.65 dB STD, and 1.99 dB STD across three measurement databases [3]. These values are acceptable rather than excellent, and the independent evidence used here is frequency-response only. Overall measured performance is moderate.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The SM100 is a conventional passive wired IEM. Its 3.5 mm connection, detachable 2-pin cable, microphone, inline control, metal shell, and named cable material are common implementations documented on the official pages [1][2]. No SM100-specific patent, technical paper, active processing layer, or independently verified technical advantage was found in the cited material. The technology package is useful but easy to replicate.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations.

CP = 19.99 USD / 39.00 USD = 0.5126 (rounded to 0.5)

TRUTHEAR Gate with 3.5 mm Microphone is the comparison product at 19.99 USD [4]. It provides wired in-ear playback, a 3.5 mm connection, microphone cable, and inline cable control [4]. Its frequency-response deviation is 1.933 dB STD from the Harman IE 2019 v2 target over 20 Hz-20 kHz, compared with SIVGA SM100 at 1.994 dB STD over the same range, so the comparison product is slightly better on the measured metric [3][5]. The calculated CP value is therefore 0.5126, rounded to the displayed 0.5 score.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

SIVGA documents a one-year product warranty, with shorter coverage for cables and adapters, and the SM100 listing links the same return and warranty framework [2]. The passive wired architecture is simple: no battery, wireless radio, active noise cancellation, or software layer is documented on the product pages [1][2]. Manufacturer support and global sales channels are documented, but no statistical failure-rate data, recall notice, model-specific defect pattern, long parts-support commitment, or detailed repair-cost record was found. Reliability is slightly above neutral because the product is mechanically simple and warranty handling is documented.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The design philosophy is mostly practical: wired playback, microphone, inline control, and detachable cable are real user-facing functions [1][2]. However, the product is not clearly measurement-led. Official material emphasizes housing, diaphragm, and cable claims, while the independent evidence found for this model is limited to frequency-response data [2][3]. Costs appear tied to a functional wired IEM rather than decorative excess, but unsupported material-effect claims keep the philosophy neutral.

## Advice

The SM100 is reasonable to consider when a passive 3.5 mm IEM with microphone, inline control, and detachable cable is required. The measured frequency-response result is acceptable, but the technical package is ordinary and the CP result shows a cheaper measured alternative with equivalent user-facing functions. If purchase cost is the deciding factor, compare TRUTHEAR Gate with 3.5 mm Microphone first. Choose SM100 mainly when its physical layout, shell, or cable arrangement is specifically desired.
