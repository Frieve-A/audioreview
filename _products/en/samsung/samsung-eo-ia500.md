## Overview

The Samsung EO-IA500 is a wired 3.5mm in-ear earphone released in the second half of 2021 as a Samsung-branded replacement for the EO-IG955, which had been co-developed with AKG and bundled with Galaxy S8 through S10 devices. Priced at approximately USD 8–15 at retail, the EO-IA500 dropped the AKG co-branding and premium acoustic partnership in favor of a mass-market aftermarket accessory. As of 2026, the product is discontinued with no confirmed active new-stock listings in the US market [3].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Third-party measurements from Reference Audio Analyzer (RAA) show a frequency response standard deviation (STD) of approximately 3.51 dB from the Harman In-Ear 2019 target curve (SIEC stand, available approximately 30 Hz–10 kHz data range, 192 kHz/24-bit) [2]. In the 100 Hz–10 kHz band, STD is approximately 2.69 dB. For wired IEMs, an STD near ±3 dB is commonly treated as the standard tolerance band, while ±5 dB or above is problematic; the 3.51 dB result sits near the upper edge of the standard range. In RAA's Harman-target-relative data, deviation across 100 Hz–10 kHz stays roughly between +3.48 dB and -5.08 dB [2]. The manufacturer specifies 20–20,000 Hz with no ±dB tolerance stated [1]. No third-party THD, S/N ratio, IMD, or passive isolation figures were identified. With only partial frequency response data available, overall transparency cannot be fully determined, and a neutral mid-point score is assigned.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The EO-IA500 uses only mature commodity technologies: dual dynamic drivers, a 3.5mm analog jack (legacy on current smartphones), canal-type silicone eartips, and a 3-button inline remote with microphone. No proprietary patents were identified. It is sold under the Samsung brand, but offers little technical differentiation and is a fully passive design with no digital integration. The predecessor EO-IG955's AKG co-development, detachable cable, and premium acoustic tuning were removed with no improvement in published specifications. Overall technology level is low.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The EO-IA500 carries a 1-year limited warranty, below the typical 2-year industry standard. The passive wired construction — no battery, no active electronics, and no moving parts beyond the cable — inherently limits failure points compared with wireless or active products. Samsung provides manufacturer-direct global support with confirmed regional support pages across multiple markets including Canada, UAE, Levant, Saudi Arabia, and Gulf regions [1]. No statistical failure data (RMA rates or MTBF) was identified for this model. Firmware is not applicable to this passive device.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The EO-IA500 pursues a mass-market design built from standard mass-produced components, with no pseudoscientific claims identified. However, it removes the predecessor's AKG acoustic partnership, detachable cable, and tuning focus without measurable performance gains in published specifications or RAA data [1][2]. The dual-driver and "2-way woofer and tweeter" positioning reads as marketing language rather than measured acoustic advantage [1][2]. Subjective claims such as "rich audio," "smooth sound," and "balanced" lack measurement support [1]. Lower price alone does not improve measured performance, and the design philosophy is assessed as low in rationality.

## Advice

The Samsung EO-IA500 is a discontinued budget wired earphone whose primary functional value — wired 3.5mm connectivity with an inline remote and microphone — was available at an approximately USD 8 historical price point. RAA measurements show a Harman In-Ear 2019 frequency response STD of approximately 3.51 dB, near the upper edge of the standard tolerance range [2]. Third-party THD and passive isolation figures were not identified. The product is no longer actively sold at major US retailers, limiting purchase options to used or third-party channels. Buyers seeking wired IEMs with more complete measurement records, especially lower frequency response STD values, will find better-documented alternatives in the current market.
