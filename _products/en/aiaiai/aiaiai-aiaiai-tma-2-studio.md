## Overview

AIAIAI is a Copenhagen, Denmark audio company that has built its identity since 2010 around the idea of "responsible design." The TMA-2 Studio is the wired, passive, closed-back preset of its TMA-2 modular headphone platform, launched in 2015, in which the drivers, earpads, headbands, and cables are individually detachable and cross-compatible across the catalog. Configured with the S05 40mm bio-cellulose driver and aimed at studio monitoring and production, it sells for 200 USD [1].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

As a passive closed-back headphone, the primary applicable metric is frequency response, with THD and passive isolation as secondary; signal-to-noise ratio, SINAD, intermodulation distortion, and crosstalk do not apply to a device with no internal electronics. AIAIAI publishes a frequency-response range of 10Hz–40kHz but no THD figure or frequency-response tolerance [1]. Third-party measurements from headphonecheck.com [2] and Sonarworks [3] characterize the frequency response as broadly even, with a mild treble restraint above 12kHz and a low-end channel imbalance of about 2.5dB on some units, mapping to roughly ±2–3dB of deviation from the Harman target. THD is not quantified by any source, and passive isolation is reported only qualitatively for the closed-back design. Overall measured frequency-response performance is acceptable — short of best-in-class but free of problematic deviations.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The defining technology is AIAIAI's in-house TMA-2 modular architecture, in which the drivers, headbands, earpads, and cables are individually detachable and cross-compatible across the catalog — a genuine in-house system and industrial-design effort that remains relatively uncommon among studio headphones [1][3]. The transducer itself, however, is a conventional 40mm dynamic driver with a bio-cellulose diaphragm sourced externally from NAC Audio; the topology (neodymium magnet, 32Ω, 97dB/mW) is entirely standard, and no proprietary patents were located [1]. The wired Studio configuration is an analog, mechanical-only design with no DAC, amplifier, DSP, or wireless integration, and the modular concept is mechanically replicable without licensing. The in-house design merit and the absence of any digital integration offset each other, leaving an average technology level.

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations. The TMA-2 Studio's current market price is 200 USD [1]. The cheapest equivalent-or-better product is the Sony MDR-7506 at 99 USD [4], a wired passive closed-back over-ear monitor with a coiled cable and screw-on 1/4-inch adapter — an equivalent essential function set. ## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

Reliability and support are this product's strongest area. The standard 2-year limited warranty extends to 3 years at no cost through free membership registration [1]. As a passive headphone with no internal electronics and few wear-prone components, and with every component — speaker units, earpads, headbands, and cables — individually replaceable, it is a simple, robust, degradation-resistant design. AIAIAI runs a dedicated spare-parts program, including a speaker repair kit, with no stated end-of-support date and an explicit long-term parts-supply commitment that exceeds typical headphone support duration [1]. Support is provided manufacturer-direct globally through an online portal with published repair guides, alongside a worldwide authorized distributor network [1]. Documented durability complaints belong to the older non-modular TMA-1, not the current TMA-2, and no failure-rate statistics or recalls exist. These factors yield a high score.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

AIAIAI's design philosophy is rational and grounded in functional engineering: the TMA-2 uses a conventional passive dynamic driver aimed at neutral studio monitoring. Its defining direction is "responsible design" — a genuinely novel, still-uncommon modular architecture that lets users repair, upgrade, and customize the headphone rather than discard it, an innovative approach in the headphone space [3]. The current modular Studio preset is also a documented measurable improvement over the prior non-modular TMA-1 in frequency-response tuning [3]. The approach is not strongly measurement-led — AIAIAI publishes no THD figures and relies on subjective sound descriptors plus an unverified claim of bio-cellulose superiority — so no additional scientific-rigor credit is warranted; since diaphragm material can plausibly affect measured response, this remains an unverified claim rather than a claim of audible benefit for a non-audible element, so no penalty applies. Costs are spread across function, materials, aesthetics, and brand without a majority going to non-functional elements, and there is no quantity inflation. The result is an above-average, rational philosophy.

## Advice

The TMA-2 Studio suits music creators who prioritize long-term serviceability and the ability to replace or upgrade individual parts — drivers, pads, headbands, and cables — rather than discarding the whole headphone. Its measured frequency response is broadly even and well suited to monitoring. Buyers focused purely on measured performance per USD should note that the Sony MDR-7506 delivers comparable or tighter frequency-response accuracy at roughly half the price; the TMA-2's premium buys modularity, repairability, and a guaranteed parts supply rather than measurably higher fidelity. Those who value that sustainability and customization will find strong reliability value here.
