## Overview

The Creative Aurvana Live! SE, marketed as the Aurvana SE, is Creative's closed-back wired over-ear headphone positioned for use with the Super X-Fi ecosystem. Independent reporting reproduces a manufacturer-style specification table listing frequency response 10Hz–30,000 Hz, a 40mm dynamic driver with neodymium magnets, and notes that Creative does not publish impedance or sensitivity figures in the same materials [1]. The model is a black variant of the Aurvana Live! with Super X-Fi certification as the main functional distinction [1]. Creative Technology has sold personal audio products since the 1980s; headphones remain secondary to the Sound Blaster line. A dedicated Creative US product URL for this model was not reachable as a standalone page at review time; retail listings remain available [4].

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The design pairs a conventional 40mm dynamic transducer in a Foster-related industrial lineage described in independent hands-on reporting [1] with Super X-Fi certification for Creative’s HRTF and preset workflow [1][3]. Super X-Fi is a proprietary Creative stack with limited uptake as a bundled headphone feature by other brands [3]. The transducer side follows common industry practice; versus Aurvana Live!, the step is mainly certification and ecosystem integration rather than a new acoustic platform [1].

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Creative provides 12-month warranty coverage with global support infrastructure including live chat and email channels [2]. Hands-on reporting notes a stiff non-removable cable and all-plastic construction, with isolation described as modest rather than strong [1]. Community discussion for the related Aurvana Live family line includes anecdotal reports of driver or cable-related failures over time; these are not RMA statistics and should not be read as measured failure rates [5].

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Creative allocates cost toward a known transducer platform and minimal cosmetic overhead while marketing Super X-Fi certification for users who will use compatible processing [1][3]. That pairs a conventional acoustic block with optional computational correction, which is a defensible modern consumer layout when the goal is predictable manufacturing cost and ecosystem upsell. The approach is incremental versus the non–Super X-Fi sibling rather than a clean-sheet acoustic flagship program [1].

## Advice

At 39.99 USD representative retail, the Aurvana SE remains a plausible pick for buyers who want the Aurvana Live!-class voicing with Super X-Fi workflow support [1][3]. Expect a stiff entry-level cable and limited isolation [1]. Because independent headphone measurements for this exact SKU are scarce, listeners who prioritize verified curves should treat timbre as uncertain until third-party data appear [1].
