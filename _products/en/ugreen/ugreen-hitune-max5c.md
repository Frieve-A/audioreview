## Overview

The UGREEN HiTune Max5c (model HP203) represents UGREEN's entry into the budget active noise-canceling headphone market. These over-ear wireless headphones combine hybrid ANC technology with Hi-Res Audio certification and LDAC codec support, targeting users seeking premium features at an accessible 39.99 USD price point. With 75-hour battery life and comprehensive connectivity options, the Max5c positions itself as a feature-rich alternative to established competitors. UGREEN, traditionally known for charging accessories and cables, has expanded into audio products with designs emphasizing technological integration and cost-effectiveness.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Third-party measurements from Audio Science Review reveal significant performance compromises that substantially impact audio fidelity. Frequency response exhibits deep bass roll-off below 50Hz with ringing artifacts extending into the midrange, indicating poor driver control and acoustic design. Lower treble distortion measures approximately 1%, substantially exceeding the 0.5% problematic threshold for headphones [1]. The 43dB ANC performance specification represents the sole positive measurement, exceeding the 30dB excellent threshold for noise cancellation. However, missing critical measurements including S/N ratio and passive isolation prevent comprehensive evaluation. Without equalization, the preference score of 66.6 falls well below transparent performance levels [1]. While EQ correction demonstrates potential (preference score 89.1), the underlying acoustic deficiencies indicate fundamental design limitations that compromise scientific validity across multiple measurable parameters.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

The Max5c demonstrates strong technological implementation through advanced feature integration and modern connectivity standards. Bluetooth 5.4 adoption provides cutting-edge wireless performance with improved stability and efficiency. Hybrid ANC implementation achieving 43dB performance represents sophisticated signal processing capabilities typically found in higher-tier products. LDAC codec support enables Hi-Res Audio transmission up to 24-bit/96kHz, matching contemporary high-fidelity standards. The DNN algorithm for call clarity showcases AI integration for practical functionality enhancement. Fast charging technology (15 minutes for 5 hours) demonstrates efficient power management design. App integration for EQ and settings customization reflects modern user interface expectations. While the core implementation appears to utilize competitive OEM/ODM platforms rather than proprietary development, the comprehensive feature set and advanced technology adoption indicate strong technical execution within the budget category constraints.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

At 39.99 USD current market price, comprehensive analysis reveals no cheaper alternatives with equivalent or better functionality and measured performance. The Max5c provides over-ear ANC design with 43dB noise reduction, Bluetooth 5.4 connectivity with LDAC Hi-Res Audio support, exceptional 75-hour battery life (45 hours with ANC), fast charging capability, app-based EQ control, DNN call enhancement, and dual device connectivity. Systematic comparison with budget ANC headphones identified the OneOdio Focus A6 at 69.99 USD as the nearest equivalent, offering similar features (48dB ANC, LDAC, Bluetooth 6.0, 75hr/40hr battery) but at significantly higher cost. No products were found below the Max5c price point providing comparable feature density and measured ANC performance. This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations. The combination of advanced features, measured ANC capability, and market-leading battery specifications at this price point establishes the Max5c as the most cost-effective option in its functional category.

## Advice

For users prioritizing extended battery life, comprehensive connectivity features, and effective noise cancellation within strict budget constraints, the HiTune Max5c provides exceptional functional value. The 43dB ANC performance and LDAC codec support deliver tangible benefits for commuting, travel, and wireless listening scenarios. However, potential purchasers must understand the significant audio quality compromises revealed by third-party measurements. The deep bass roll-off, midrange ringing, and elevated treble distortion substantially impact musical enjoyment for critical listening applications. Users with smartphones or digital audio players supporting parametric EQ can potentially mitigate some frequency response issues, though fundamental acoustic limitations remain. The Max5c suits users valuing practical features over audiophile-grade sound quality, particularly those requiring maximum battery life for extended use. For critical music listening or professional audio applications, consider alternatives with superior measured acoustic performance, even at higher cost. The product represents rational value for feature-focused users willing to accept audio quality trade-offs for comprehensive connectivity and exceptional battery performance.
