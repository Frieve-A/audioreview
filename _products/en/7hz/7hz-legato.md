## Overview

The 7Hz Legato represents the company's entry into dual dynamic driver IEM design, released March 20, 2023 at 109 USD. The product features a 12mm woofer combined with a 6mm tweeter/midrange driver, housed in CNC-machined aviation-grade aluminum shells with a crossover network employing 8 Japan-made tantalum capacitors. The manufacturer markets a step-up from the budget Salnotes Zero toward a more complex layout aimed at bass emphasis and perceived stage width. Public third-party data reviewed here characterize **the assembled IEM's** measured behavior (primarily frequency response) and **do not isolate** the contribution of the dual-dynamic plus crossover topology from enclosure, damping, and tuning choices. No cited source provides a controlled comparison showing that adopting this dual-dynamic layout **improved** objective metrics such as distortion performance or error versus a neutral reference **relative to** the single-dynamic Salnotes Zero or other single-driver designs. The company has established itself in the value IEM segment through strategic component sourcing and design optimization rather than fundamental driver development.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Third-party measurements from In-ear Gems show frequency response with L-shaped signature featuring bass emphasis below 150Hz, flat response between 500Hz-1kHz, and gentle ear gain at 7.5dB versus Harman target's 12dB [1]. The manufacturer specifies THD <1% @1kHz [3], though this provides insufficient detail to determine compliance with headphone distortion thresholds. Available frequency response data indicates intentional but significantly deviated tuning from neutral reference, with most bass energy concentrated in the true bass region rather than mid-bass muddiness. Those curves describe **one sample of the product as built and tuned**; they **cannot** by themselves demonstrate that switching to a dual-dynamic driver with crossover **caused** higher fidelity, because no published A/B or controlled comparison holds test conditions and target curve equal while **only** driver topology differs. Independently, Audio Science Review documentation for the single-dynamic 7Hz Salnotes Zero reports lower measured distortion and better target-compliance results at much lower price [2], so **better objective numbers are not uniquely tied to the Legato's multi-driver hardware** in the evidence available here. Assessment remains conservative due to limited comprehensive third-party measurement data from major testing laboratories for the Legato itself.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The Legato employs established IEM technologies in an OEM/ODM configuration without proprietary innovations. Core components include dual dynamic drivers with fourth-generation DLC composite diaphragms, N52 neodymium magnets, and Japanese tantalum capacitor crossover network housed in CNC aluminum shells. While component selection demonstrates appropriate engineering for the price point, all technologies represent mature implementations available across the industry. The dual dynamic driver architecture and crossover design follow standard practices without competitive technical advantages or novel approaches that other manufacturers would seek to adopt. Industry-wide, high-performing single-dynamic IEMs are commonplace; **the reviewed public dataset does not benchmark this dual-dynamic implementation against a single-driver counterpart of the same model**, so the engineering literature cited here does not establish a technology-level advantage attributable specifically to the second driver.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations.

CP = 19.99 USD / 109 USD = 0.1834 → 0.2

The 7Hz Salnotes Zero at 19.99 USD [4] is the least expensive qualified alternative with equivalent-or-better measured performance documented by Audio Science Review [2]. Equipped with detachable 0.78mm 2-pin connectors and 3.5mm termination for wired in-ear use, it shows impressively low distortion and strong target compliance versus the Legato's manufacturer THD specification and third-party frequency response description [1][2].

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Product coverage includes 1-year warranty for IEMs with 3-months for cable, limiting protection compared to industry standards of 2+ years. Support operates through authorized retailers rather than direct manufacturer channels, with repair processing through 7Hz taking up to 7 working days. Build quality receives consistent praise in reviews [5], with CNC aluminum construction and detachable cable system using quality OCC silver-plated conductors. The moderate structural complexity of dual drivers with crossover network presents standard reliability expectations, though no comprehensive failure rate data or long-term durability assessments are available.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

7Hz allocates cost toward dual dynamic drivers, tantalum crossover capacitors, and precision manufacturing while holding a mid-tier price at 109 USD. From a measurement-first standpoint, **the added driver and crossover add parts and tuning variables**; the sources summarized here **do not show** that this complexity produced **better objective results** than the firm's own lower-cost single-dynamic model with documented measurements [2], nor do they separate "more drivers" from the chosen bass-heavy curve in [1]. The marketing story of moving from Salnotes Zero to a dual-dynamic layout therefore **cannot be treated as scientifically verified sonic progress** using available data. Separately, 7Hz explicitly rejects measurement-based tuning approaches, stating "WE TUNE WITH OUR EARS AND HEARTS, NOT WITH GEARS AND CHARTS" while targeting subjective "vintage sound" characteristics from 1960s-80s audio. That orientation favors narrative and preference over verifiable target alignment, even where industrial execution of the mechanical build remains competent.

## Advice

Consider the Legato when the measured bass-forward signature in [1] matches your listening goals and you accept that **no published dataset proves** the dual-dynamic hardware itself raised fidelity versus simpler designs. For strictly **objective** performance per third-party work, the single-dynamic 7Hz Salnotes Zero [2] already posts stronger documented figures at much lower cost. The Legato's 1-year warranty limitation requires consideration for long-term ownership plans. Users requiring comprehensive measurement data for purchase decisions should note the limited third-party testing available for this product.
