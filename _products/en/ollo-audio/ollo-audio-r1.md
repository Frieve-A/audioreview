## Overview

The OLLO Audio R1 is a closed-back over-ear headphone from OLLO Audio, a boutique studio headphone manufacturer founded in 2017 in Sempeter pri Gorici, Slovenia. Released for shipping in January 2026 at 385 USD, the R1 succeeds the discontinued S4R as OLLO's dedicated recording and tracking model. It features a patented 50mm composite PU+PET diaphragm, American walnut earcups, a stainless steel headband, and the USC II unit-specific calibration system — a factory-measured IEC 60318-1 correction file delivered with each unit for DAW or hardware DSP application. The frequency response is deliberately tuned to a U-shaped curve for performer engagement rather than flat monitoring accuracy [1].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Manufacturer specifications report THD below 0.12% at 94 dBSPL at 1kHz, measured with an IEC 60318-1 ear simulator [1]. This represents acceptable distortion performance for a headphone. Passive isolation averages approximately 10 dBSPL(c) across the full frequency range (measured bidirectionally with pink noise per IEC 60318-1), with attenuation of approximately 5 dBSPL at 250 Hz, approximately 11 dBSPL at 500 Hz, approximately 13 dBSPL at 1 kHz, and 30 dBSPL or above above 6 kHz [1]. The 5 dBSPL attenuation at 250 Hz is particularly limited for a headphone designed to prevent click-track bleed during recording sessions, and the 10 dBSPL(c) average represents marginal overall isolation. The stated frequency response spans 5 Hz to 22 kHz with a 1/3-octave peak-to-peak variation of 13.4 dBSPL, consistent with the intentional non-flat U-shaped tuning; deviation from the Harman target curve cannot be quantified from available data. No independent third-party measurements have been published for the R1 as of this review date, so all figures represent manufacturer specifications.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

The R1 is designed and manufactured in-house at OLLO Audio's Slovenian workshop. The 50mm dynamic driver uses a patented composite PU+PET (polyurethane and polyethylene terephthalate) layered diaphragm, for which OLLO holds a specific pattern patent [1]. The technically most substantive element is the USC II (Unit Specific Calibration) system: every unit is individually measured on an IEC 60318-1 ear simulator at the factory, producing a serial-number-specific XPS correction file applicable via AU/AAX/VST3 DAW plugins (Pro Tools, Ableton, Reaper, Studio One, Nuendo) or to third-party DSP hardware including RME TotalMix, UA Console, and Neumann MA 1 Mission Control [1]. The open-format XPS approach reflects genuine accumulated manufacturing and software know-how. Commercial precedents for unit-specific calibration exist, and the composite membrane concept is commercially established, so neither qualifies as cutting-edge on an absolute scale. Technology integration appropriately combines precision analog driver manufacturing with a digital DSP calibration pipeline. Competitive advantage is moderate: the membrane patent provides IP protection but is designable around; the USC approach can be independently replicated by well-resourced competitors.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations.

The R1 is a wired passive over-ear headphone with 3.5mm and 6.3mm connectivity, no ANC, no built-in DSP, and no wireless capability. Current market price is 385 USD [2].

The Sennheiser HD 280 Pro, available at approximately 89 USD [5], does not replicate OLLO's USC II unit-specific calibration workflow. It is used here as the lower-cost comparator for the R1's core passive recording/tracking role, where the decisive user-facing requirements are wired closed-back over-ear operation, 3.5mm and 6.3mm studio connectivity, and microphone-bleed reduction. Users who specifically require bundled unit-specific correction should treat USC II as the R1's differentiating workflow rather than as part of this passive-isolation comparison. Performance comparison on quantified axes:

- **THD:** HD 280 Pro manufacturer specification is 0.1% [3], equal to or better than the R1's manufacturer specification of below 0.12%. Third-party measurements from InnerFidelity/Stereophile show HD 280 Pro THD+noise consistent with approximately 0.1–0.2% at 90 dBSPL at 1kHz [4]. This comparison is provisional, as R1 THD data is limited to manufacturer specifications only.
- **Passive Isolation:** InnerFidelity/Stereophile third-party measurements confirm HD 280 Pro broadband isolation of −16 dBr across 100 Hz–10 kHz [4], substantially exceeding the R1's approximately 10 dBSPL(c) average.

For the core passive recording/isolation role, the HD 280 Pro demonstrates equivalent-or-better measured performance on both evaluated axes at less than a quarter of the R1's price.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

The R1 carries a 5-year limited warranty: years 1–2 cover full repairs including manufacturer-arranged DHL pickup for complex cases; years 3–5 cover replacement parts with buyer-paid shipping [1]. This substantially exceeds the standard 2-year industry coverage. The passive wired design has no internal electronics beyond the driver, a stainless steel headband, and detachable mini-XLR cables per earcup, inherently eliminating the failure-prone components found in active or wireless designs. All parts — drivers, earpads, headband assembly, and cable — are user-replaceable with standard household tools; nine spare part SKUs for the R1 are currently available from OLLO Audio's webstore at EUR 27–49 each. OLLO provides manufacturer-direct global support with a Help Desk ticket system targeting 48-hour assessment response, and a flat-rate EUR 30 professional repair service with manufacturer-arranged return logistics for complex warranty-covered work [1]. The R1 began shipping in January 2026, so statistical field failure data is not yet available; no manufacturing defects or recalls have been reported to date.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The USC II calibration system represents rational measurement-based engineering: per-unit IEC 60318-1 factory measurement producing a unit-specific DSP correction file, deployable in standard DAW and hardware environments, is a data-driven approach to controlling unit variance [1]. The R1 also shows meaningful progression from OLLO's previous recording model, adding the patented membrane construction, USC II calibration, a 5-year warranty, and a 90-day trial period at a moderate price increase [1]. However, a notable share of cost is directed toward premium materials — American walnut earcups and stainless steel hardware — that do not contribute to measured audio performance, representing misaligned cost allocation relative to acoustic function. The U-shaped tuning is presented as a performer preference design choice rather than a claim of measured fidelity improvement, avoiding unsupported perceptual assertions; however, this also confirms that the core acoustic design does not prioritize measurement-driven accuracy.

## Advice

The R1 presents a strong case specifically on support and quality control: the USC II per-unit calibration workflow, 5-year warranty with arranged repair logistics, and comprehensive spare-parts availability make it compelling for studios that prioritize long-term serviceability and calibration integration within an existing DAW or hardware DSP environment.

However, the passive isolation of approximately 10 dBSPL(c) average — with only approximately 5 dBSPL attenuation at 250 Hz — is a measurable shortcoming for the headphone's primary stated use case of preventing monitoring bleed from reaching microphones during recording sessions. Buyers whose primary requirement is reliable microphone isolation should assess whether this specification is sufficient before committing to 385 USD. The Sennheiser HD 280 Pro, at approximately 89 USD with third-party confirmed broadband isolation substantially exceeding the R1's specification, represents the most cost-effective alternative for the core recording use case. The R1's price over these alternatives reflects boutique manufacturing, the USC calibration ecosystem, and premium materials rather than a measured acoustic performance advantage.
