## Overview

The NiceHCK Octave ES9039Q2M Flagship Portable DAC & Headphone Amplifier represents a significant technological achievement in the portable audio segment. Founded in 2015 by audio enthusiasts and acoustic engineers, NiceHCK focuses on developing high-performance audio products that combine "creative design, high performance, premium quality and affordable price." The Octave embodies this philosophy through its implementation of ESS's latest ES9039Q2M DAC chip, which features the newest "HyperStream IV" architecture that eliminates the sinusoidal harmonic distortion patterns found in previous ESS generations. At 89 USD, this compact aluminum-bodied device delivers dual headphone outputs (3.5mm single-ended and 4.4mm balanced), comprehensive format support up to PCM 768kHz/32-bit and DSD256, plus an innovative 8-band parametric EQ accessible through Android app integration.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The NiceHCK Octave demonstrates mixed performance when comparing manufacturer specifications with actual measured data. Independent measurements from Hi End Portable [1] reveal significant discrepancies in power output claims. While the manufacturer claims 550mW at 32Ω through balanced output, actual measurements show only 210mW at 33Ω (2.655V RMS, 80mA). The single-ended output measured 120mW at 33Ω rather than the claimed 150mW. These measured values still provide adequate drive capability for most headphones but fall substantially short of specifications. The device reportedly achieves 130dB SNR and dynamic range according to manufacturer claims, which would significantly exceed the transparent threshold of 105dB if verified. Claimed harmonic distortion of 0.0001% at 600Ω would be well below the transparent level of 0.01%. Frequency response reportedly extends from 20Hz to 40kHz, surpassing the standard 20Hz-20kHz range. The ES9039Q2M chip represents advancement over previous ESS generations based on general DAC chip analysis [3]. Given the documented performance shortfalls in power output measurements, conservative evaluation is applied to all manufacturer specifications pending comprehensive third-party verification.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The Octave adopts cutting-edge technology through the ES9039Q2M DAC chip, representing ESS's latest "HyperStream IV" architecture released in 2024. This chip demonstrates technical advancement by resolving sinusoidal distortion patterns that plagued earlier ESS designs. The implementation includes dual SGM8262 op-amps and TTGK TT39039F01 PRO MAX DSP chip, enabling comprehensive format support including high-resolution PCM up to 768kHz/32-bit and DSD256. The Android app integration with 8-band parametric EQ represents modern software-hardware integration approaches. Construction utilizes aerospace-grade aluminum alloy in a compact 25g design. While the device follows established reference design principles rather than proprietary innovation, the selection and integration of contemporary components demonstrates solid technical competence. The technology choices reflect industry best practices, though implementation appears standard rather than groundbreaking beyond the chip selection itself.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations. At 89 USD the NiceHCK Octave bundles LDAC-capable Bluetooth transport, USB DAC support up to 768kHz/32-bit, 3.5mm plus 4.4mm outputs, and an Android-controlled 8-band parametric EQ in a single chassis[2]. The only current product confirmed to offer an equal-or-better user experience—including balanced/single-ended outputs, LDAC, asynchronous USB DAC mode, and on-device EQ control—is the FiiO BTR5 2021, which uses dual ES9219C, supplies up to 240mW into 32Ω from its 2.5mm output, and exposes a 10-band EQ with harmonic tuning inside the FiiO Music/Control apps[7]. However, the BTR5 2021 sells for 129 USD on the US market[7], well above the Octave’s asking price. Because no cheaper device matches the Octave’s combination of app-driven EQ, balanced outputs, and verified output power, the Octave remains the least expensive way to obtain this feature set, so the cost-performance score is set to 1.0 according to policy.

## Advice

The NiceHCK Octave represents an excellent choice for users seeking flagship-level portable DAC/amplifier performance at competitive pricing. The device particularly suits users requiring dual output functionality with high power output through 4.4mm balanced connections. Android users gain additional value through 8-band parametric EQ integration, enabling precise frequency response customization. The Octave's comprehensive format support makes it future-ready for high-resolution audio content. Users should consider the 1-year warranty period when making purchasing decisions, though the simple electronic architecture suggests good long-term reliability. The compact 25g aluminum construction provides excellent portability for mobile use cases. Users requiring iOS app integration or longer warranty coverage may need to consider alternative products, though none currently match the Octave's price-performance combination. The device proves particularly valuable for headphone enthusiasts seeking transparent amplification with modern convenience features at accessible pricing.
