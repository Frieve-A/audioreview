## Overview

The Marantz Professional MPH-2 represents a discontinued closed-back studio monitoring headphone featuring 50mm dynamic drivers designed for professional recording environments. Originally marketed as bridging studio monitor engineering with private audio applications, the MPH-2 incorporates noise-isolating over-ear design with plush cushions for extended comfort during long recording sessions. The headphones feature a 180-degree rotatable frame, collapsible design for portability, and detachable 10ft locking cable with included 1/8" to 1/4" adapter. Built with rugged stainless steel telescopic arms, the MPH-2 targets musicians and engineers requiring fatigue-free monitoring capabilities.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Scientific validity evaluation utilizes available third-party measurement data from Reference Audio Analyzer [4]. Measured performance reveals significant discrepancies from manufacturer specifications: sensitivity measured at 113.0 dB/V SPL versus manufacturer claim of 100±3dB, indicating 13dB higher output than specified, and average impedance of 38.04 ohms versus manufacturer specification of 32 ohms. These measurement disparities suggest specification reliability issues. Frequency response measurements [4] confirm significant deviations from the Harman target curve: a peak of approximately 9dB is observed around 6kHz, substantially exceeding the problematic threshold of ±5dB. Additionally, 3-6dB elevation in the bass region and approximately -6dB dip around 10kHz are confirmed, indicating problematic-level deviations across multiple frequency bands. These frequency response deviations correspond to problematic-level borderline evaluation in audio quality assessment. While total harmonic distortion, signal-to-noise ratio, and passive isolation performance measurements remain unavailable, the significant frequency response deviations result in a Scientific Validity evaluation of 0.3, corresponding to problematic-level borderline.
