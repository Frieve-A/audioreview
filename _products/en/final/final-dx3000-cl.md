## Overview

The final DX3000 CL (model code FI-DX3BDPL) is the first closed-back headphone in final's DX series, released globally on November 13, 2025, at 599.99 USD [1][4]. Manufactured by S'NEXT Co., Ltd. at their Kawasaki factory in Japan, it features a 40mm Paper-Carbon Composite dynamic driver, glass-fiber reinforced resin housing, and adhesive-free screw-and-O-ring construction designed for long-term user repairability. The DX4000 CL sits above it in the DX closed-back lineup.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The DX3000 CL is fully in-house designed and manufactured at final's Kawasaki facility in Japan [1]. Core technologies include: a Paper-Carbon Composite dynamic driver combining Japanese Washi paper with carbon fiber for simultaneous high damping and diaphragm rigidity; a Free-Edge Surround structure removing mechanical constraint from the driver surround; a Rear Diffuser Array Structure using non-uniformly spaced ribs behind the driver to disperse internal standing waves; and a Spider-Web Front Diffuser positioned in front of the diaphragm for high-frequency coherence control. Final's in-house design capability and accumulated acoustic engineering know-how are genuine assets. However, the available product materials do not provide patent or licensable-IP evidence for these implementations, and the product relies entirely on passive analog and mechanical engineering with no digital, software, or AI integration.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The DX3000 CL's assembly using exclusively O-rings and precision screws — with tool-free user-replaceable earpads and a detachable 4.4mm balanced cable — constitutes a simple, repair-optimized passive design. Final provides global manufacturer support through its Japanese entity and European/international entity (Netherlands), with an authorized dealer network in the US through retailers including Bloom Audio. The standard warranty for US market buyers through authorized retailers is 1 year [4]. A 3-year extended warranty is available at no cost via product registration within 30 days of purchase through the European entity [5]; uniform availability to US buyers through primary US retail channels is not confirmed. No statistical failure data exists for this product, which launched in November 2025.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The DX3000 CL's engineering investments — a composite diaphragm for simultaneous high damping and rigidity, diffuser arrays for internal resonance control, free-edge surround for reduced mechanical constraint, and adhesive-free construction for long-term repairability — represent genuine functional cost allocations with identifiable acoustic rationale [1]. Final does not publish frequency response targets, THD benchmarks, or SNR design goals for this product; press materials consistently frame performance objectives in subjective terms, indicating a non-measurement-led design philosophy. All technologies employed serve functional audio engineering purposes. The DX3000 CL is the first closed-back product in the DX series, so model-to-model acoustic performance progression cannot be assessed.

## Advice

The DX3000 CL is priced at 599.99 USD in the US market [4]. As of June 2026, no credible third-party acoustic measurements have been published, making objective performance verification currently impossible. The FiiO FT1 at 149 USD [3] offers equivalent user-facing functions — wired closed-back over-ear playback with 4.4mm balanced connectivity, no DSP, no wireless — with confirmed measured performance from RTINGS [2] (THD 0.08% at 94dB SPL, passive isolation −12.46 dB). Prospective purchasers are advised to defer purchase and await independent acoustic measurements before committing at 599.99 USD.
