## Overview

The Brise Audio Fugaku represents an ambitious attempt at creating the ultimate portable audio system, combining MEMS hybrid earphone technology with a dedicated analog amplifier and premium cable system. Released in 2024 at 16,999 USD, this 8-driver, 5-way configuration system features the world's first MEMS speaker integration alongside balanced armature and dynamic drivers. The system includes a 4kg dedicated portable amplifier with active crossover circuits, pure titanium housing with PVD black coating, and a 16-core pure silver cable. While technically impressive in its multi-amplifier approach and cutting-edge driver technology, the system faces significant challenges in practical implementation and market positioning.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

The Fugaku demonstrates exceptional technological sophistication through its pioneering integration of MEMS driver technology with multi-amplifier active crossover systems. The proprietary 7-pin connector enables individual amplification of each driver group, with BA and MEMS drivers receiving single-ended drive while dynamic drivers utilize balanced drive methods [1][2]. The dedicated amplifier features a sophisticated two-layer board design with 4-layer power supply and 8-layer amplifier sections, incorporating MUSES72323 electronic volume control and 12-channel power amplification [2]. The MEMS hybrid approach represents cutting-edge 2024 technology that other manufacturers would require significant time and investment to replicate. Brise Audio's successful integration of diverse driver technologies with complex crossover networks and premium materials demonstrates high technical expertise and innovation that establishes new technological benchmarks in the IEM industry.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Brise Audio offers comprehensive warranty coverage of 1-3 years depending on product grade, with repair services providing 1-2 business day response times during weekdays [4]. However, the Fugaku's complex multi-amplifier system with numerous electronic components significantly increases potential failure points compared to conventional IEM designs. The 4kg amplifier weight creates additional mechanical stress concerns for portable use, potentially affecting long-term durability. While Brise Audio provides manufacturer support through official channels and maintains repair infrastructure, their primary expertise lies in cable manufacturing rather than complex electronic systems. The company's limited track record with sophisticated amplifier products raises questions about long-term support capabilities for such an intricate system. The inherent complexity of the multi-amp crossover configuration makes field repairs challenging and increases the likelihood of component-level failures.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Brise Audio's design philosophy demonstrates scientific merit through its multi-amplifier active crossover approach, which represents a rational method for optimizing individual driver performance [1]. The adoption of cutting-edge MEMS technology and advanced functional integration of amplifiers, crossover circuits, and diverse driver technologies shows innovative thinking aligned with performance optimization. However, the 16,999 USD pricing appears excessive relative to measurable performance benefits, suggesting cost optimization was not prioritized. While the multi-amp configuration and MEMS integration represent meaningful technological advancement, the extreme pricing relative to the technological innovation level raises questions about cost-effectiveness. The company's focus on "expressing all information from the source" through dedicated driver amplification demonstrates scientifically sound reasoning, but the implementation results in a system whose costs appear disproportionate to the actual performance improvements achieved.

## Advice

The Fugaku targets an extremely niche market of audio enthusiasts willing to pay premium prices for cutting-edge technology and unique multi-amplifier functionality. Potential buyers should carefully evaluate whether the multi-amplifier approach provides audible benefits justifying the 16,999 USD investment, recognizing that no directly equivalent products exist for comparison. The 4kg amplifier weight severely limits true portability despite the "portable" designation. Those seeking innovative MEMS technology and multi-amp configurations should consider this system primarily as a technical showcase and research platform rather than a practical daily-use solution. Buyers should ensure access to Brise Audio's repair network given the system's complexity. For users seeking high-end IEM performance without multi-amplifier requirements, conventional premium IEMs like the Sony IER-Z1R at approximately 2,000 USD offer excellent sound quality with significantly greater portability and proven reliability.
