## Overview

The Brise Audio AKAGANE-HP is a premium headphone cable from the Japanese manufacturer Brise Audio, priced at 1,200 USD and up. This cable represents a core model in Brise Audio's headphone cable lineup, featuring AKAGANE-35 wire material in an 8-wire configuration with carbon nanotube technology integration. The company, established with over 20 years of cable experience, positions this product as inheriting precise expressiveness and high resolution while significantly increasing conductor volume compared to their previous models. The AKAGANE-HP features proprietary terminals, brass slider construction, and NEUTRIK XLR connectors in a handmade Japanese manufacturing approach.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.0} $$

The AKAGANE-HP falls under the high-priced cable category that claims audible sound quality improvements without providing ABX blind test data or equivalent scientific evidence of audibility. The manufacturer makes specific claims about "delicate highs that never become piercing," "rich midrange," and "powerful low end with grounded presence," along with assertions about expanded soundstage and improved imaging. However, no quantitative specifications for resistance, capacitance, frequency response, or crosstalk measurements were found that would allow objective evaluation of cable performance. According to engineering consensus, properly constructed cables show negligible differences in the audio frequency range (20Hz-20kHz). Without scientific validation of claimed audible effects, this product receives the minimum score for high-priced products claiming unmeasurable benefits.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The AKAGANE-HP demonstrates moderate technical sophistication through several proprietary elements. The company employs AKAGANE-35 wire material in an 8-wire configuration with carbon nanotube (CNT) technology integration claimed to suppress minute vibrations and electrical irregularities. The construction incorporates special high-performance resin insulation, brass slider components with black coating for cable management, and proprietary terminal designs. Brise Audio's 20+ years of cable manufacturing experience and established handmade production processes requiring approximately 10-20 units per day indicate significant technical expertise accumulation. However, the technology level is limited by the inherently analog nature of cable products and lack of digital integration or cutting-edge approaches that would clearly advance industry standards beyond traditional cable construction methods.
