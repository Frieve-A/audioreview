## Overview

The EarFun UA100 is EarFun's first portable USB DAC/headphone amplifier, launched in October 2023 as part of the company's 5th anniversary lineup. It provides both a 3.5mm single-ended and a 4.4mm balanced headphone output via USB-C, supporting PCM up to 32-bit/768kHz and DSD512 in a 10.6g CNC-machined aluminum housing. A UAC 1.0/2.0 mode switch enables driverless compatibility with gaming consoles in addition to Android, iOS, Windows, and macOS. The product is priced at 79.99 USD [1].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Manufacturer-published specifications for both outputs indicate high audio performance [1]. The 3.5mm single-ended output specifies THD+N below 0.0008%, S/N ratio at 121 dB (A-weighted), frequency response of 20Hz–40kHz (±0.5 dB), and crosstalk at −71 dB. The 4.4mm balanced output carries identical THD+N and frequency response specifications, with S/N ratio at 117 dB (A-weighted) and crosstalk at −109 dB. All stated values represent high performance across every reported metric. No independent third-party measurements have been conducted for this product, so the manufacturer specifications remain externally unverified and are treated with caution.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The UA100 is built entirely from three commercially sourced, off-the-shelf components: the ESS ES9038Q2M DAC, the Ricore RT6863 headphone amplifier deployed as a stereo pair, and the Comtrue CT7601 USB audio bridge [1]. The ES9038Q2M was introduced around 2017–2018 and had already become a widely deployed, mature commodity in competing portable DAC/amplifiers well before the UA100's October 2023 launch. The RT6863 was similarly common across multiple brands by 2022. EarFun holds no patents for any aspect of this design; the clock jitter suppression prominently marketed as a product feature is ESS Technology's intellectual property. The hardware has been confirmed to be identical to the Shanling UA2 Plus, with the UA100 differing only in branding [4]. There is no in-house circuit design, no accumulated proprietary engineering expertise, and no technical differentiation that competing manufacturers could not replicate immediately by sourcing the same chipset.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The EarFun UA100 is priced at 79.99 USD [1]. The TRN Black Pearl, available at 36.99 USD [3], provides both 3.5mm SE and 4.4mm balanced USB-C outputs with PCM up to 384kHz and DSD256 support, covering all essential user-facing functions of the UA100. Independent measurements using an E1DA Cosmos ADCiso professional ADC (Audio Science Review community, July 2025) [2] show the TRN Black Pearl achieving S/N ratio of approximately 130 dB (A-weighted, full-scale) — 9 dB above the UA100's manufacturer-specified 121 dB — and SINAD exceeding 109 dB. THD+N was qualitatively confirmed as excellent by the same measurement session. Frequency response was confirmed flat across the full measured range. Crosstalk was not directly measured, so the UA100's −109 dB balanced-crosstalk manufacturer specification cannot be independently compared. The TRN Black Pearl additionally provides a 10-band parametric EQ absent from the UA100. This comparison is provisional because the UA100's performance figures are unverified manufacturer specifications and the comparator's crosstalk is not reported.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The UA100's passive wired design — USB-C input feeding fixed analog outputs with no battery, no wireless components, and no mechanical moving parts — represents inherently simple, robust construction with minimal failure modes. The CNC-machined aluminum housing resists physical wear. EarFun provides an 18-month warranty covering manufacturing defects, processed through an online claim portal, with a 30-day money-back guarantee [1]. Global manufacturer-direct support is available through warranty claim processing and a community forum. As a USB Audio Class device, the UA100 requires no firmware updates. No widespread hardware failures, manufacturing defects, or recalls have been documented as of June 2026.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The UA100's design approach is pragmatic and measurement-oriented: the primary bill-of-materials investment flows to semiconductor components with established high-SINAD potential, while aesthetic and material costs are kept minimal, and no scientifically unverifiable audio claims are made [1]. The product's fundamental value proposition — providing a dedicated DAC and headphone amplifier circuit in place of smartphone or laptop built-in audio — is functionally justified as a product category. The approach is genuinely measurement-focused rather than subjective, and costs are directed toward functional performance rather than brand premium. However, the execution is entirely conservative: the hardware is identical to an existing competitor product, no novel circuit topology or software integration is developed, and no innovative solution is introduced. One marketing claim misattributes ESS Technology's clock jitter suppression patent to EarFun, though the underlying technological effect is real.

## Advice

The EarFun UA100 provides 3.5mm and 4.4mm balanced headphone outputs with broad platform compatibility, and its manufacturer specifications report THD+N below 0.0008%, S/N ratio of 121 dB, and frequency response extending to 40kHz. Its compact aluminum build and driverless gaming console support via UAC 1.0 mode make it a practically capable portable DAC/amplifier.

Buyers prioritizing independently verified performance should consider that the TRN Black Pearl (36.99 USD) has been third-party measured to achieve S/N ratio of approximately 130 dB and SINAD exceeding 109 dB, while providing the same essential dual-output functionality plus a 10-band parametric EQ. Its crosstalk has not been reported, so the UA100's unverified manufacturer crosstalk specification cannot be independently compared. Users who specifically require confirmed UAC 1.0 gaming console compatibility and are uncertain about TRN Black Pearl support for that use case should choose the UA100 only for that confirmed compatibility. For everyone else, the independently measured TRN Black Pearl delivers verified stronger S/N and SINAD data at less than half the price.
