## Overview

The AIYIMA DAC A10 is a budget desktop DAC and headphone amplifier featuring an ESS9018K2M DAC chip, XMOS208 USB receiver, and TPA6120A2 headphone amplifier. Priced at 85 USD, it offers multiple input options including USB, optical, and coaxial connections with support for high-resolution audio up to 384kHz/32bit PCM and DSD256. The device provides dual headphone outputs with both standard 3.5mm and balanced 4.4mm jacks, along with RCA line outputs. The device targets users seeking affordable desktop audio processing with comprehensive connectivity options.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The DAC A10 presents excellent manufacturer specifications with THD of 0.00019% and SNR of 120dB, both significantly exceeding transparent performance levels (0.01% THD and 105dB SNR respectively). Frequency response covers the standard 20Hz-20kHz range, though deviation data is not specified. The headphone output provides 360mW into 32Ω loads, adequate for most headphone impedances. USB input supports up to 384kHz/32bit PCM and DSD256, while optical/coaxial inputs handle up to 192kHz/24bit. However, these are manufacturer specifications rather than independent third-party measurements. Based on claimed specifications suggesting transparent-to-excellent performance levels, but applying conservative adjustment per policy for unverified manufacturer claims, the score is adjusted from potential 0.8 level to 0.7.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations.

At 85 USD, the DAC A10 competes in the budget desktop DAC/headphone amplifier segment. The FiiO K7 integrates USB/optical/coaxial inputs, RCA line outputs, and both 6.35mm and 4.4mm headphone outputs while delivering ≥2W @ 32Ω from its dual THX AAA 788+ stage with THD+N <0.0003% (1kHz/32Ω/A-weighted) and SNR ≥120dB [2]. Apos sells the K7 for 209.99 USD [3], making the DAC A10 the cheapest path to these functions and transparent-level measurements, so Cost-Performance saturates at 1.0 due to the absence of a lower-priced equivalent.
