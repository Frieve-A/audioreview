## Overview

The Technics EAH-AZ100 represents the company's flagship true wireless earbuds announced in January 2025, celebrating Technics' 60th anniversary. Currently priced at 250 USD, these earbuds feature proprietary magnetic fluid driver technology derived from the acclaimed TZ700 wired in-ear monitors. Key specifications include 10mm magnetic fluid drivers, frequency response of 20Hz-40kHz, Bluetooth 5.3 connectivity with LDAC/AAC/SBC/LC3 codec support, and full adaptive digital hybrid noise cancellation. The product offers multipoint connection to 3 devices simultaneously, IPX4 water resistance, and spatial audio optimized for Dolby Atmos with head tracking functionality.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Scientific validity evaluation is based on frequency response graph and EQ recommendation table from SoundGuys third-party measurement data [1]. Calculating deviations from Harman target curve based on EQ recommendation table reveals +5dB deviation at BASS band and 250Hz, placing performance at problematic levels (±5dB or above problematic). Deviations of -3dB at 2500Hz and -4dB at 4000Hz fall within standard levels (±3dB standard). No deviation at 500Hz, 1000Hz, and 8000Hz, with +2dB at 12000Hz also within standard levels. According to headphone/earphone evaluation criteria (±3dB standard, ±5dB or above problematic), multiple frequency ranges fall into problematic levels, warranting 0.3 evaluation for frequency response alone. However, other indicators including THD, S/N ratio, and ANC performance in dB lack sufficient measurement data and cannot be evaluated. Applying adjustment toward 0.5 for insufficient measurement data, final evaluation is 0.4. Battery life testing confirms 10 hours and 40 minutes with ANC enabled, validating manufacturer claims.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

The EAH-AZ100 demonstrates exceptional technology level through innovative magnetic fluid driver implementation. This proprietary technology utilizes NASA-developed ferrofluid with nanoscale magnetic particles to provide precise acoustic damping, suppressing unwanted resonances and oscillations in the voice coil. The miniaturization of reference-class technology from the TZ700 into true wireless form factor represents significant engineering achievement. Additional advanced features include Acoustic Control Chamber functioning as Helmholtz Resonator for optimized frequency balance, Voice Focus AI technology for enhanced call clarity, and comprehensive connectivity with Bluetooth 5.3 supporting latest LC3 codec. The combination of proprietary acoustic innovation with cutting-edge digital processing places this product at the technology forefront.

## Cost-Performance

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations. The Technics EAH-AZ100 current market price is 250 USD. Comparison target is the Soundcore Liberty 4 NC priced at 80 USD, which provides equivalent-or-better user-facing functions and performance. Equipped with LDAC codec support, active noise cancellation (98.5% noise reduction), Hi-Res audio certification, IPX4 water resistance, and multipoint connectivity with superior 50-hour total battery life. The substantial price difference demonstrates poor cost-performance despite technological advancement claims.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The design philosophy shows partial rationality with both strengths and limitations. Magnetic fluid driver technology has legitimate scientific basis using ferrofluid for acoustic damping, and the Acoustic Control Chamber functions as a scientifically sound Helmholtz Resonator. However, measured performance data reveals that these innovations have not translated into superior audio quality, suggesting implementation challenges or fundamental design limitations. The approach prioritizes physical driver improvements over potentially more cost-effective digital signal processing optimization. The substantial price premium of 250 USD reflects investment in proprietary hardware technology, though superior auditory results are achieved by products using advanced software-based approaches at significantly lower cost. While the 2025 model demonstrates technological progression with innovative features and integrates cutting-edge capabilities including spatial audio processing and AI-enhanced voice functions, the poor measured performance outcomes indicate that emphasis on physical hardware innovation over software optimization has led to suboptimal results relative to investment.

## Advice

The Technics EAH-AZ100 is difficult to recommend given its poor cost-performance and problematic frequency response. While the magnetic fluid driver technology represents interesting engineering, SoundGuys measurements reveal problematic-level deviations exceeding ±5dB from Harman target in bass and treble regions, undermining justification for the 250 USD asking price. The Soundcore Liberty 4 NC at 80 USD delivers equivalent core functionality including LDAC, active noise cancellation, Hi-Res audio, and multipoint connectivity with superior battery life, making the Technics 212% price premium unjustifiable for most users. The EAH-AZ100 may appeal to technology enthusiasts specifically interested in magnetic fluid driver innovation, but practical users seeking excellent audio performance and value should strongly consider the Soundcore alternative or wait for substantial price reductions on the Technics model.
