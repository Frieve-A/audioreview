## Overview

IsoAcoustics ISO-155 isolation stand features patented directional isolation technology designed for studio monitors up to 40 lbs. The stand measures 6.1" x 7.5" (155mm x 190mm) and provides 14 variations of height and tilt adjustment, with height ranging from 3" (76mm) in the low setting to 8.25" (210mm) in the tall setting and tilt adjustment up to 6.5 degrees [1]. The frames utilize high-impact ABS construction with stainless steel poles, designed to decouple speakers from surfaces and prevent vibration transmission. The 3-part directional isolation system includes top isolator, bottom isolator, and connector, developed from broadcast studio experience. IsoAcoustics has distributed isolation products globally across over 60 countries since 2012.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Speaker isolation stands address vibration transmission between speakers and supporting surfaces, which can affect frequency response in certain environments. While the ISO-155 addresses a legitimate acoustic consideration, speaker isolation products do not fit standard audio equipment measurement criteria (THD, SNR, frequency response) used for active components. The 3-part directional isolation system provides functional vibration management, though specific quantitative isolation effectiveness data for the ISO-155 model is not publicly available. The company has conducted NRC anechoic chamber testing on some product lines (specifically GAIA isolators), demonstrating consistent frequency response without colorization [2], though this testing data does not specifically cover the ISO-155 model. Without standardized measurement criteria for speaker isolation products and limited quantitative data specific to this model, scientific validity assessment relies on general isolation principles rather than measurable performance thresholds.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The product demonstrates solid technical expertise with patented directional isolation technology developed from broadcast studio experience. Founder Dave Morrison's 20-year involvement in CBC radio and television studio construction provides credible technical foundation [3]. The isolation system features directional characteristics with internal taper and concave design that prevents off-axis movements while maintaining on-axis energy alignment. Collaboration with the National Research Council for prototype testing and optimization shows commitment to scientific validation. The 3-part isolation mechanism represents innovation beyond simple foam padding approaches common in the field. However, the purely analog/mechanical design lacks integration with modern digital technologies that could enhance functionality or provide additional features.
