## Overview

The Bowers & Wilkins DB1D represents a high-end active subwoofer featuring dual 12-inch Aerofoil cone drivers in a balanced configuration. Priced at 7,200 USD, this subwoofer combines B&W's proprietary driver technology with modern digital signal processing, offering comprehensive connectivity and app-based control. The DB1D targets audiophiles and home theater enthusiasts seeking exceptional low-frequency performance with contemporary digital features. Key specifications include frequency response from 10Hz to 350Hz (-3dB), extension to 8.5Hz (-6dB), and a 2000W Class D Hypex amplifier with digital preamplifier and Dynamic EQ capabilities.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

The DB1D showcases significant technological advancement through multiple proprietary innovations. B&W's Aerofoil cone technology employs variable thickness carbon fiber construction with syntactic foam core, optimized through advanced computer modeling for maximum stiffness while maintaining low mass [5]. The back-to-back driver configuration effectively cancels cabinet resonances through balanced force design. Digital integration includes sophisticated DSP with Dynamic EQ, app-based setup via Bluetooth Low Energy, and comprehensive connectivity options. The reported 2000W Class D Hypex amplification [4] represents current high-efficiency technology, though this specification requires official confirmation. This combination of proprietary materials science, advanced digital processing, and modern connectivity demonstrates technical sophistication that competitors would desire to adopt. The technology integration surpasses typical subwoofer implementations, representing innovative approaches other manufacturers are likely to follow.

## Reliability & Support

Bowers & Wilkins provides solid support infrastructure through an established global dealer network and clear repair procedures. The 2-year warranty for powered loudspeakers matches industry standards, covering defects in materials and workmanship under normal use [1]. ## Advice

For purchasers seeking ultimate low-frequency performance with modern digital features, the DB1D represents the most cost-effective option in its class. The exceptional extension to 8.5Hz and sophisticated digital processing justify the 7,200 USD investment for applications requiring extreme low-frequency capability. The app-based control and room correction features particularly benefit users with complex installation requirements or multiple listening environments. However, buyers focused solely on basic subwoofer functionality without digital features should consider alternatives like the Rythmik FV15HP at 2,127 USD, which provides excellent performance for traditional applications. The DB1D suits home theater enthusiasts, audiophiles with large listening spaces, and users who value modern connectivity and control features alongside exceptional measured performance.
