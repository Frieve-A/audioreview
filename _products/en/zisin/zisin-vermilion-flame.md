## Overview

ZiSin is a Chinese audio brand with over a decade of experience in cable manufacturing, specializing in high-purity conductor cables. The Vermilion Flame (朱璃焰), launched in August 2025, is the company's second IEM product after the single-driver Black Kirin, representing its expansion into multi-driver designs. Priced at 109 USD on AliExpress, it is a passive wired IEM with a 1DD+2BA hybrid driver configuration, a visually distinctive blood-red resin faceplate, and a detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable with 3.5mm SE and 4.4mm balanced termination options.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Manufacturer specifications state a frequency response of 20Hz–20,000Hz with no ±dB deviation specified, and a passive sound isolation of 26 dB [1]. The claimed 26 dB isolation is within an acceptable range. THD, S/N ratio, IMD, and crosstalk are unspecified by the manufacturer. No independent third-party measurements have been published for the Vermilion Flame. With manufacturer claims as the sole data source, a reliable assessment of acoustic performance cannot be made.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

Every constituent technology in the Vermilion Flame has been standard in the budget Chinese IEM segment since 2018–2020: the 1DD+2BA hybrid driver configuration, titanium-coated composite diaphragm dynamic driver, balanced armature drivers from third-party suppliers, and Litz OCC+OFC detachable cable. No proprietary patents or unique intellectual property have been identified. The product is consistent with OEM/ODM origin — a cable manufacturer entering IEM production using established driver hardware without original acoustic engineering. As a passive wired IEM, no digital integration is involved. None of these technologies provides a competitive advantage, and none represents a contribution that other manufacturers would seek to adopt.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations.

The current market price of the Vermilion Flame is 109 USD [1].

The Truthear GATE [4] (20.69 USD on Amazon [3]) provides equivalent or superior user-facing functions — wired analog connectivity, detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable, no ANC, no DSP — with confirmed measured performance exceeding the Vermilion Flame's unverified figures at a fraction of the price. Passive sound isolation: up to 35 dB measured [3] versus the Vermilion Flame's 26 dB manufacturer claim. THD: confirmed distortionless up to 104 dBSPL by Audio Science Review formal measurement [2] versus no data for the Vermilion Flame. Frequency response: confirmed excellent compliance by Audio Science Review GRAS coupler measurement [2] versus the Vermilion Flame's unverified 20Hz–20,000Hz specification with no ±dB deviation stated. This comparison is provisional on the Vermilion Flame side as all performance figures are manufacturer claims only.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

ZiSin provides no documented manufacturer warranty; the sole buyer protection is AliExpress platform coverage of approximately 90 days, well below the standard 1-year threshold. Support is conducted entirely through the ZiSin Official Store on AliExpress with no independent manufacturer infrastructure, no documented parts supply program, and no authorized service centers. The passive IEM design is inherently simple and robust: the detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable eliminates the most common IEM failure point, and steel nozzles contribute to structural durability. ZiSin is an emerging IEM manufacturer with limited reliability track record.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The Vermilion Flame's design philosophy has three material problems. First, cost allocation prioritizes aesthetics: the blood-red resin faceplate drives marketing positioning and community reception as the primary differentiator, representing investment unrelated to measured acoustic performance. Second, the premium cable positioning — inherited from ZiSin's cable brand identity — has no demonstrable impact on frequency response or distortion at audio frequencies, yet figures prominently in product marketing. The "monitoring grade" label is applied without any measurement basis. Third, the development direction follows the established Chi-Fi hybrid template exactly; as an OEM IEM expansion from a cable brand, no original acoustic engineering or design innovation is present.

## Advice

Buyers seeking a wired IEM at this price should be aware that the Vermilion Flame has no published independent measurements to substantiate its 109 USD price point relative to thoroughly verified alternatives. The Truthear GATE, available from approximately 21 USD [3][4], carries Audio Science Review measurements confirming distortionless performance up to 104 dBSPL [2], excellent frequency response compliance [2], and 35 dB passive isolation [3] — all independently verified figures that the Vermilion Flame cannot provide.

Buyers prioritizing the Vermilion Flame's distinctive aesthetic and the convenience of an included 4.4mm balanced cable may find value in those features. Buyers for whom confirmed acoustic performance is the primary criterion should prioritize products with published independent measurement data.
