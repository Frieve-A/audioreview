## Overview

The Onkyo GX-D90 is an active powered 2-way desktop speaker system released in Japan in July 2002 as part of the WAVIO PC audio line, with a launch MSRP equivalent to 124 USD per pair [1]. It houses a 15W+15W Class-AB amplifier, a 9 cm OMF (Onkyo Micro Fiber) woofer in a bass-reflex enclosure, and a 2 cm balanced-dome tweeter rated by the manufacturer to 90 kHz. Connectivity includes optical and coaxial S/PDIF digital inputs supporting up to 24-bit/96 kHz PCM, one analog RCA stereo input, a front-panel 3.5 mm headphone output, and a subwoofer line-out [1]. The cabinet is MDF with an aluminum front baffle; the amplifier, DAC, and VLSC (Vector Linear Shaping Circuitry) analog filter stage are all housed in the right-channel enclosure. The product is discontinued; the original Onkyo entities filed for bankruptcy in April 2022 [4]. The current secondary market price is 69 USD [3].

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The GX-D90 has two documented technology strengths in the launch materials: Onkyo's VLSC DAC output filter circuit and the integration of DAC, preamp, power amplifier, and dual 24-bit/96 kHz S/PDIF inputs into a desktop powered speaker [1]. These are documented integration and proprietary-circuit positives. Against them, the 24-bit/96 kHz S/PDIF input, Class-AB amplifier, composite fiber cone woofer, and balanced-dome tweeter are entirely conventional by current standards; none represent cutting-edge technology adoption. No independent measurement source is available to demonstrate an audible or measurable advantage for VLSC over conventional DAC output filtering. The positive integration/proprietary-circuit factors and the negative current-standard/verification factors cancel, leaving the score at 0.5.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The original Japan domestic warranty was 1 year. Available repair and owner reports indicate age-related service risk rather than a quantified population failure rate: a restoration report identifies the LM1876 power amplifier IC as the main heat source, notes high rear-panel temperature at moderate volume, observes power-supply voltage-sag symptoms, and attributes S/PDIF control-board stress to proximity to the power IC [6]. Kakaku owner discussions also document repair costs and input-related problems for the model [2]. All original Onkyo support entities ceased operations following bankruptcy in April 2022 [4]. For a discontinued 2002 Japan-market unit, third-party electronics repair is the practical support path, with no cited authorized service route for this model.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Evaluated against commercially available alternatives in July 2002: the desktop powered speaker market at that time was dominated by analog-only products, with virtually no competitor offering dual S/PDIF digital inputs at 24-bit/96 kHz at a 124 USD-equivalent launch price. The cost allocation of the GX-D90 is directed toward functional audio capability: the OMF woofer, integrated DAC, and dual optical/coaxial digital inputs all address concrete user-facing functions, supporting a positive adjustment for costs contributing to function and performance. The original WAVIO lineage showed documented functional progression: the GX-100HD (2007) continued the platform, and the GX-500HD (2009) stepped up to 40W+40W with a larger driver, supporting a positive adjustment for model progression. The integration of DAC, preamp, and power amplifier into the speaker enclosure was meaningfully advanced for the 2002 desktop speaker market. The approach at launch was functionally novel for a compact desktop powered speaker. One element is irrational regardless of era: the 90 kHz tweeter response is marketed as a sound quality benefit, but human hearing generally does not extend meaningfully beyond 20 kHz and no research supports audibility of 90 kHz extension. VLSC is not penalized here; it operates as a functional analog LPF, even though its claimed superiority over conventional DAC filters is unverified.

## Advice

The GX-D90 is a secondary market-only product, available in Japan at 63-75 USD. Buyers should weigh practical hardware risks before purchasing a 24-year-old unit: relay or contact failure, digital input failure from thermal stress, power supply instability, and proprietary cable connector degradation, all without a manufacturer repair path. Users specifically seeking digital input powered desktop speakers should evaluate current products instead. The Edifier R1280DBs (199.99 USD) provides optical and coaxial digital inputs, analog RCA inputs, subwoofer line-out, and Bluetooth connectivity with a 2-year warranty and active manufacturer support [5]. The GX-D90 can be a serviceable option for buyers who already own a working unit or who can arrange third-party repair, but purchasing on the secondary market for primary use carries meaningful reliability risk with no official recourse.
