## Overview

The Fyne Audio F500 is a discontinued passive bookshelf loudspeaker from Fyne Audio's original F500 Series. It targets small to medium rooms and uses the company's named IsoFlare and BassTrax acoustic systems in a compact standmount cabinet. Current evidence shows remaining US stock from 1,275.00 USD per pair for wood finishes, while Fyne's own page marks the model as discontinued [1][3].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Manufacturer specifications state a Frequency Response of 45Hz-34kHz at -6dB typical in room [1]. Third-party in-room measurements from Pursuit Perfect System show an exact-model frequency-response graph averaged across multiple listening-room positions, with the source reporting multiple midrange and treble ups and downs, but it does not publish a numeric Frequency Response deviation or THD value [2]. Overall measured evidence is moderate: a real third-party graph exists, but the available numeric audio-performance value is the manufacturer's -6dB bandwidth specification.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The F500 shows genuine loudspeaker engineering through Fyne's in-house IsoFlare acoustic layout and BassTrax downward port diffuser, both used as central functional elements rather than cosmetic labels [1]. At the same time, the platform is still a passive mechanical loudspeaker using mature cabinet, port, crossover, and speaker-terminal technologies. The result is above a generic passive design, but it does not include current digital processing, active correction, software control, or another hard-to-license technology layer.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The F500 is priced at 1,275.00 USD per pair as remaining new stock [3]. The cheapest product confirmed as equivalent or better is the ELAC Debut 3.0 DB63, a current passive bookshelf speaker sold at 499.00 USD per pair [5][6]. The DB63 provides the same passive wired speaker-level functionality, and its manufacturer Frequency Response of 42Hz-38kHz at -6dB covers the F500's 45Hz-34kHz at -6dB specification at both ends on the same specification axis [1][5]. Because the F500's only available numeric performance value is this manufacturer specification, the comparison is made on that basis. In addition, the DB63 is independently verified by Klippel near-field-scanner measurements showing on-axis response linearity of -2.55/+2.6dB from 80Hz to 16kHz [7], whereas the F500's third-party evidence is a non-numeric in-room graph [2]. The F500's higher rated sensitivity (89dB versus 87dB at 2.83V/1m) is a 2dB difference with no practical impact within both products' recommended amplifier power ranges.

CP = 499.00 USD ÷ 1,275.00 USD = 0.39

Rounded to one decimal place, the Cost-Performance score is 0.4.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Fyne Audio documents a 7-year warranty for passive loudspeakers purchased through authorized dealers, with repair or replacement for qualifying manufacturing defects [4]. The F500's passive construction avoids active electronics and software update risk, and no exact-model recall or recurring physical defect was documented in the investigation. Support is routed through dealer and distributor channels, while parts duration and repair cost beyond warranty are not publicly specified.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The design philosophy is mostly rational: Fyne's main claims address acoustic behavior such as radiation pattern, bass-energy distribution, cabinet coloration, and crossover integration [1]. These are relevant engineering targets for a passive loudspeaker. The limitation is verification depth: the exact F500 lacks public lab-grade anechoic data, numeric Frequency Response deviation, and THD, while some promotional wording is not measurement evidence. The score therefore remains moderate rather than high.

## Advice

The F500 is reasonable to consider only when a new pair is available through a channel that preserves the documented warranty. Its strongest reader-facing points are passive simplicity, a long warranty, and functional acoustic design work. Its main limitation is evidence quality: exact-model public data stops at an in-room graph and a manufacturer -6dB Frequency Response specification. Buyers should also note that current passive bookshelf speakers with full Klippel-derived public measurements, such as the ELAC Debut 3.0 DB63 at 499.00 USD per pair, deliver an equal-or-better documented specification and measurement set at well under half the price [5][6][7].
