## Overview

The SPL Phonitor xe is a flagship standalone desktop headphone amplifier from Sound Performance Lab (SPL), a German professional audio company founded in 1983. Announced at High End Munich in May 2019, it sits at the top of SPL's Phonitor lineup. The unit features SPL's proprietary VOLTAiR ±60V rail voltage technology with custom in-house SUPRA op-amps, a hardware Phonitor Matrix analog crossfeed circuit, two balanced XLR and two single-ended RCA analog inputs, and both balanced 4-pin XLR and single-ended 6.35mm headphone outputs. An optional internal DAC768v2 module adds USB, coaxial, optical, and AES digital inputs. The unit is manufactured at SPL's facility in Niederkrüchten, Germany. The amplifier-only configuration is listed at 3,000 USD in SPL's North American official store [5].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

Third-party measurements by Addicted to Audio using an RME ADI-2 PRO FS R BE ADC show THD of 0.0007% and IMD of 0.0041%, both indicating excellent distortion performance [2]. Crosstalk measured at −89.6 dB demonstrates excellent channel separation [2]. The manufacturer specifies dynamic range of 130.5 dB on the single-ended output and 135.5 dB on the balanced output, THD+N of 0.00082% at 1 kHz and 0 dBu, and frequency range of 10 Hz – 100 kHz at 0 dBu [1]. All evaluated metrics are at a high performance level. The measured THD and IMD are far below audibility concerns, but they remain approximately an order of magnitude above the best-performing headphone amplifiers in this category, where independently confirmed THD values an order of magnitude lower have been achieved.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The Phonitor xe is built around SPL's proprietary VOLTAiR ±60V rail platform using custom in-house SUPRA op-amps not available from any other source. This represents genuine in-house design, proprietary technology, and accumulated analog-circuit know-how [1]. However, the VOLTAiR platform dates to SPL's professional products of the 1990s — a 30-year-old technology that is established rather than cutting-edge. It has not become a broadly adopted industry approach, and modern standard-voltage designs consistently achieve equivalent or superior measured performance at substantially lower cost. Through-hole PCB assembly is a legacy manufacturing method that SPL markets as producing sonically superior results through component selection — a claim without scientific verification [1]. The amplifier topology is unchanged from the predecessor Phonitor x while the retail price increased. The optional DAC768v2 uses an industry-standard AKM AK4493 SEQ chip and a commodity Spartan-7 FPGA, with no technical novelty beyond the proprietary output filter stage. Digital integration is limited to the optional add-on module.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The Phonitor xe is priced at 3,000 USD [5]. The Topping L70, available at 279 USD [3], provides the same core analog input and headphone output formats used for ordinary headphone-amplifier operation: balanced XLR and single-ended RCA analog inputs, plus balanced 4-pin XLR and single-ended 6.35mm headphone outputs. It additionally offers a preamp output, IR remote control, 12V trigger, 4.4mm balanced output, and 3.5mm output not present on the Phonitor xe. It does not provide SPL's hardware Phonitor Matrix crossfeed, so the comparison is valid for the high-fidelity headphone-amplifier role but not for buyers who specifically require hardware analog crossfeed.

The Topping L70 achieved SINAD of 120.4 dB (corresponding to THD+N ≈ 0.0000954%) in a formal Audio Science Review measurement [4], compared to the Phonitor xe's third-party measured THD of 0.0007% [2]; distortion performance of the L70 is substantially superior. The cited ASR review does not publish separate IMD or crosstalk values for the L70, so those fields are not used as direct advantages. Manufacturer-specified dynamic range is 146 dB for the L70 versus 135.5 dB for the Phonitor xe [3][1].

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The Phonitor xe carries a 2-year manufacturer warranty [1]. The unit is solid-state construction with no vacuum tubes or fragile transducer elements; the amplifier section is inherently durable. The motorized Alps RK27 potentiometer and dual mechanical VU meters introduce some moving-part complexity, placing the design in the average bracket for construction robustness. SPL provides manufacturer-direct global support via an RMA repair request system with spare parts available from the factory in Germany [1]. Third-party repair services are also confirmed available. SPL's 40-year history manufacturing professional studio equipment supports a positive reliability assessment. No statistical failure rate data has been documented for this specific product; known minor issues are limited to faceplate silkscreen wear on a limited early production batch and a macOS USB sample-rate compatibility behavior for the optional DAC module, neither constituting a systematic concern.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

SPL attributes the Phonitor xe's sonic character to VOLTAiR high-voltage rails (claimed to produce a "relaxed and pleasant sound experience") and through-hole PCB assembly (claimed to enable "superior-sounding components"). Neither claim is supported by ABX blind test evidence or controlled independent measurement — the Phonitor xe's measured performance is matched and substantially exceeded by products using standard-voltage, surface-mount designs at a fraction of the price [2][4]. Significant cost is allocated to a 4.9 kg all-metal chassis, dual mechanical VU meters, three available finish options, and a German manufacturing premium, none of which correspond to measurable audio performance improvements. From the Phonitor x to the Phonitor xe, the amplifier section is technically unchanged while the retail price increased by approximately 400–900 USD, with additions limited to mechanical VU meters and an updated DAC module. The overall engineering approach is conservative, with the core amplifier topology and Phonitor Matrix technology unchanged for over a decade, and no adoption of DSP optimization or modern efficient amplifier approaches.

## Advice

The Phonitor xe achieves excellent measured distortion performance but at a price roughly eleven times that of amplifiers with comparable or superior measurements. The Topping L70 at 279 USD achieves SINAD of 120.4 dB (corresponding to THD+N ≈ 0.0000954%), representing substantially lower distortion than the Phonitor xe's third-party measured THD of 0.0007%, while also providing a preamp output, remote control, and additional headphone output formats [4]. The Phonitor Matrix hardware crossfeed circuit is a genuine user-facing function for listeners who prefer an analog crossfeed implementation; it should be treated as a listening-mode feature, not as evidence of higher measured fidelity. Software equivalents are freely available for most platforms but require separate configuration. The high-voltage VOLTAiR design, heavy chassis, and German manufacturing origin do not demonstrate measured audio performance advantages over alternatives at far lower cost. Buyers whose requirements specifically center on SPL's form factor, input/output configuration, manufacturer support network, or hardware Matrix function should weigh the substantial cost premium against competing products that offer equivalent or superior measured performance at a fraction of the price.
