## Overview

The SPL Phonitor xe + DAC768 is a flagship desktop headphone amplifier/DAC combo manufactured in-house in Niederkrüchten, Germany by SPL (Sound Performance Lab). Introduced at High End Munich 2019, it pairs the Phonitor xe amplifier — featuring SPL's proprietary VOLTAiR ±60V rail technology and the Phonitor Matrix analog crossfeed circuit — with an internal DAC768 module (AKM AK4490EQ, PCM up to 768kHz/32-bit, DSD256). From March 2024 onward, new production units ship with the DAC768v2 (AKM AK4493 SEQ, Spartan 7 FPGA); the two modules are not interchangeable. This review covers the original DAC768 version at its 2,499 USD retail price. SPL has produced professional studio equipment since the early 1980s, and the Phonitor xe sits at the top of their Professional Fidelity consumer/prosumer lineup.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

Third-party measurements from Addicted to Audio (using an RME ADI-2 PRO FS R BE ADC) and the Hi-Fi News Lab Report confirm outstanding performance across all primary distortion and noise metrics [2][3]. THD measured at 0.0007% indicates excellent distortion control. IMD measured at 0.0041% demonstrates excellent intermodulation characteristics. Crosstalk measured at -89.6 dB represents excellent channel separation. The full DAC-to-headphone-output chain S/N ratio of 111 dB (A-weighted, Hi-Fi News) [3] is excellent. The amplifier-stage-alone S/N of 99 dB (analog input to headphone output, Hi-Fi News) is lower but represents a good level for pure analog-input use. Manufacturer-specified dynamic range of 135.5 dB and damping factor of 180 at 40 Ω are consistent with the measured data and reflect the high headroom afforded by the ±60V rail design [1]. The available measured indicators land well into excellent territory.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

The Phonitor xe + DAC768 has a strong technology profile built on four genuine strengths. VOLTAiR is a patented SPL proprietary technology using custom 120V SUPRA operational amplifiers; no competing manufacturer employs ±60V consumer audio rails, and this competitive barrier has held for over three decades [1]. The complete product — chassis, circuit boards, and analog stages — is designed and manufactured in-house at SPL's own facility in Germany with no OEM/ODM involvement. SPL has accumulated over four decades of specialized high-voltage analog audio engineering know-how not easily replicated by new entrants. The DAC module was actively updated from AK4490 to AK4493 SEQ with an FPGA upgrade (Spartan 6 to Spartan 7), indicating ongoing development. Two factors limit the technology assessment: VOLTAiR's ±60V approach derives from 1980s–90s professional studio work and is not cutting-edge by 2026 standards, and modern competitors achieve comparable or superior measured performance via THX AAA and high-SINAD op-amp topologies without replicating the high-voltage approach, indicating the technology has not become an industry benchmark others seek to adopt. Technology integration is appropriate (FPGA-based DAC module plus discrete analog amp) but not advanced.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

The SPL Phonitor xe + DAC768 is priced at 2,499 USD. The FiiO K7, available at 219.99 USD [6], provides an integrated USB DAC/headphone amplifier with coaxial and optical S/PDIF digital inputs, analog RCA line input, 6.35mm SE and 4.4mm balanced headphone outputs, and an RCA preamp output [5]. This covers the core DAC/headphone-amplifier/preamp use case, but it does not replace the SPL-specific Phonitor Matrix analog crossfeed controls; Matrix is treated here as a separate user-facing function, not as evidence of an unverified higher-fidelity signal path. For the non-Matrix use case, third-party measurements from L7audiolab confirm that the FiiO K7 delivers equal-or-better published performance across the available primary metrics [4]:

- THD: 0.0003% (K7) vs 0.0007% (review target) — K7 superior
- S/N Ratio: 120 dB (K7, L7audiolab) vs 111 dB (review target, Hi-Fi News Lab Report) — K7 superior
- SINAD: 115 dB (K7, L7audiolab) — review target lacks a directly measured SINAD value
- IMD: Confirmed measured via SMPTE protocol (L7audiolab); K7's 115 dB SINAD and 0.0003% THD support excellent distortion/noise performance, but a direct percentage comparison to the review target's 0.0041% is not used
- Crosstalk (balanced): ≥124 dB channel separation (K7 manufacturer spec minimum, 4.4mm balanced) vs −89.6 dB measured crosstalk (review target) — K7 superior (provisional based on manufacturer spec floor)

CP = 219.99 USD / 2,499 USD = 0.088, rounded to 0.1.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

SPL provides a 2-year warranty covering defects in material and workmanship, administered through the purchasing dealer for in-warranty claims and through SPL's official online repair request system post-warranty [1]. A global dealer and distributor network covers Europe, Asia-Pacific, Middle East/Africa, and Latin America, with SPL managing North American distribution directly since January 2023. Official spare parts request and repair request forms are publicly available. These support elements are useful, but the product's notable electromechanical complexity creates countervailing risk: a motorized Alps RK27 potentiometer (a mechanical component subject to wear) and dual VU meter mechanisms add failure points not present in simpler passive attenuator designs. User-reported issues including a VU meter LED failure, XLR input failure, and headphone jack malfunctions have appeared in forum discussions, though no statistical RMA data is publicly documented by SPL. The Windows USB driver was updated once in August 2019 with no subsequent updates documented as of June 2026, and firmware update capability for the DAC768v2's Spartan 7 FPGA is not publicly documented. The 2-year warranty period is average.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The design philosophy shows genuine proprietary value alongside significant allocation of cost to non-functional elements. On the positive side, VOLTAiR provides a verifiable and physically deterministic headroom benefit — the ±60V rails deliver +32.5 dBu maximum input gain versus approximately +21.5 dBu for conventional ±15V designs — and the Phonitor Matrix provides user-adjustable analog crossfeed controls with measurable stereo-image changes. This is a real control function, not proof of a higher-fidelity signal path. However, substantial costs are directed toward elements that contribute nothing to measured audio performance: a 4.9 kg machined aluminum chassis, dual VU meters confirmed as a purely aesthetic feature, and German manufacturing overhead. SPL's claim that through-hole component assembly produces superior audio results compared to surface-mount assembly is an unverified audibility assertion; no controlled comparison data supports this claim. The approach is conservative: no DSP-based crossfeed processing (which would offer greater configurability than the analog Phonitor Matrix hardware), no app control, and no streaming capability. The product launched in 2019 when solid-state alternatives delivering excellent measured performance were already commercially mature at a fraction of the cost. The non-functional cost investment, unverified audibility claim for construction methodology, and conservative implementation strategy weigh heavily against the genuine merits of VOLTAiR headroom and the Phonitor Matrix control function.

## Advice

The SPL Phonitor xe + DAC768 achieves excellent measured performance: THD of 0.0007%, IMD of 0.0041%, and crosstalk of -89.6 dB place it among the best-measuring desktop headphone amplifiers with confirmed third-party data. If the SPL-specific Phonitor Matrix analog crossfeed controls are required and budget is not a limiting factor, this unit delivers that proprietary function alongside verified excellent distortion and noise performance in a solid-construction package.

For listeners who do not specifically require the Phonitor Matrix, the FiiO K7 at 219.99 USD [6] delivers equal-or-better published performance for the core DAC/headphone-amplifier role: THD of 0.0003%, S/N ratio of 120 dB, and SINAD of 115 dB. The approximately 11× price premium of the SPL unit is not supported by measured performance differences. The premium reflects SPL's proprietary high-voltage topology, in-house German manufacturing, premium build materials, and Matrix control function. Buyers considering this product should also note that the original DAC768 module has been replaced by the DAC768v2 in new production since March 2024, and the two modules are not interchangeable; verify which module version is included before purchase.
