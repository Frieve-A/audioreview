## Overview

The Acoustic Revive Power Sensual-MD-K is a high-end power cable manufactured by Acoustic Revive, a Japanese audio accessory company. The cable incorporates the proprietary Degawa-style MD-K unit technology (Patent #31710024) and features PC Triple C 5.6sq extra-thick conductors. The product claims to improve audio performance through negative ion generation, far-infrared emission, and electromagnetic field management. At 2 meters in length with Furutech NCF connectors, this cable represents Acoustic Revive's premium approach to power delivery for audio systems.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.0} $$

The scientific validity is severely compromised by unsubstantiated claims and lack of controlled testing evidence. The manufacturer promotes multiple audible improvements including enhanced S/N ratio, improved soundstage dimensionality, and increased musical dynamics, but provides no ABX blind testing or equivalent evidence of audibility [1]. The product emphasizes pseudoscientific concepts such as negative ion generation being "10x more than tourmaline" and far-infrared emission affecting audio quality. Third-party research demonstrates that even extreme comparisons between poor outdoor cables and audiophile power cords show only 0.2dB differences in THD+N [2]. Given the high price point and absence of controlled measurement evidence, this product falls under the framework's criterion for zero scientific validity scoring.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The technology implementation shows mixed characteristics. The Degawa-style MD-K unit represents proprietary patent technology with strategic magnet placement and natural ore particles, demonstrating some technical sophistication in design execution. The PC Triple C conductor technology and Furutech NCF connector integration indicate attention to construction quality. However, the underlying principles rely on concepts lacking scientific foundation for audio applications. The magnetic field management approach and mineral powder integration, while technically implemented, are based on theories not validated for audible audio improvements. The technology represents traditional analog approaches without meaningful innovation compared to standard power delivery methods.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Reliability and support characteristics show concerning limitations. Acoustic Revive warranty policies are highly restrictive, becoming void for direct imports, overseas purchases, or unauthorized dealer transactions, and require official seal verification for validity [3]. Parts and service support are unavailable without proof of authorized dealer purchase, creating potential support challenges for consumers. The cable construction itself is inherently reliable as a simple power delivery device with robust materials. Production operates on a made-to-order basis with 2-3 week lead times. Support infrastructure relies primarily on authorized dealer networks rather than direct manufacturer support, with contact limited to specific distributors in each region.

The design philosophy demonstrates fundamental irrationality through multiple problematic approaches. The product makes explicit claims of audible effects for scientifically non-audible elements including negative ion generation and far-infrared emission affecting audio quality.
