## Overview

The TOMOCA Products TCC-100ST is a stereo passive fader box manufactured by TOMOCA Electronics, a Japanese professional audio specialist founded in October 1970 and headquartered in Akihabara, Tokyo. The unit provides two-channel analog level control via dual 100mm plastic linear faders mechanically coupled by a gang bar for synchronized stereo operation. It features balanced XLR connectors (two XLR-3-31F77 inputs, two XLR-3-32F77 outputs) and explicitly supports phantom power pass-through for downstream condenser microphones. The product targets broadcast, PA, and studio applications requiring portable professional-grade level adjustment. The representative Japan market price is approximately 458 USD [3][4].

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

Every technology employed in the TCC-100ST has been industry-standard in professional audio since the 1970s and 1980s: carbon-track plastic linear faders, standard XLR-3 balanced connectors, and a mechanical gang bar linkage. No proprietary patents have been identified. While the product appears to be an in-house TOMOCA design — a minor credit — all components are commercially available parts with no meaningful competitive differentiation. Any manufacturer could replicate this design without technical barrier. The product line has seen no measurable technical evolution for decades; the TCC-100ST is a stereo configuration of the mono TCC-100, not a performance advancement. No digital, software, or signal-processing integration is present.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The fully passive architecture — fader elements, mechanical gang bar, and XLR connectors with no active electronics — is inherently robust, presenting very few failure modes. The plastic fader elements are the primary wear component; TOMOCA provides a dedicated fader replacement repair service for the TCC series. Warranty terms are not publicly documented, preventing any warranty-based adjustment. Support is Japan-domestic only, provided through TOMOCA's official online shop and manufacturer-direct channels with no confirmed international infrastructure. The product is actively deployed as rental equipment by professional Japanese AV companies, demonstrating adequate durability under sustained professional-use conditions. No recalls or product-specific defects are documented. TOMOCA's long operational history supplying institutional broadcast clients establishes a track record of adequate reliability in professional environments [1].
