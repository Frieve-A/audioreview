## Overview

The TANCHJIM ONE DSP is a USB-C digital signal processing in-ear monitor featuring integrated 24-bit DACs and proprietary acoustic technologies. Released in early 2024, it represents TANCHJIM's entry-level DSP offering at 29 USD. The product utilizes a 10mm dynamic driver with titanium dome structure and composite PU/PEEK materials within a triple composite rear cavity design. The USB-C connector houses dual-channel 24-bit DACs supporting up to 96kHz sampling rates, along with built-in amplification and microphone circuitry. TANCHJIM, founded in 2015, has developed multiple acoustic patents and positions this model as delivering "acoustic and aesthetic beauty" through measurement-focused design targeting the Harman 2019 frequency response curve.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Third-party measurements from Audio Science Review [1] demonstrate THD performance described as "very low, just shy of best measured" with frequency response showing "very good to excellent compliance with target" using professional GRAS 45-CA measurement fixture. Manufacturer specifications list THD below 0.062% at 1kHz. ASR measurements confirm sensitivity of 126 dB/Vrms at 1kHz and impedance of 16Ω with flat characteristics. Frequency response analysis reveals compliance with ASR target curve, with a boost in 150-350 Hz range and shortfall between 6-9 kHz. Group delay measurements show uneventful phase response without problematic behavior. Overall measured performance indicates good distortion control and frequency response accuracy within acceptable deviation ranges.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

TANCHJIM demonstrates above-average technology sophistication through in-house design capabilities and proprietary patent adoption. The company holds patents for the ternary composite rear cavity structure and anti-interference filter design, representing specialized acoustic engineering beyond standard implementations. USB-C DSP integration with 24-bit DACs reflects appropriate contemporary technology adoption, while the titanium dome driver with PU/PEEK composite materials shows material optimization expertise. TANCHJIM's dedicated R&D team, established since 2015 founding, includes specialized acoustic engineers with accumulated know-how in driver design and cavity acoustics. The integration of DSP functionality with mobile app support demonstrates modern digital/software integration approach. Technology implementation shows innovation within budget constraints rather than cutting-edge advancement.

## Cost-Performance

After comprehensive search across multiple measurement sources, no products were identified with equivalent-or-better measured performance and user-facing functions at lower prices. The TANCHJIM ONE DSP provides USB-C DSP functionality with integrated DAC/amplifier, built-in microphone supporting 24-bit 96kHz recording, and detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable system. Third-party validated measurements show very low THD performance and very good to excellent frequency response compliance with 126 dB/Vrms sensitivity. Competing products either lack essential DSP/USB-C functions, demonstrate inferior measured performance, or cost significantly more. Traditional 3.5mm IEMs cannot provide equivalent digital signal processing capabilities, while other DSP models examined showed either incomplete measurement validation or higher pricing. The review target represents the cheapest option with this specific combination of DSP functionality and validated measurement performance.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

TANCHJIM provides manufacturer direct support through global distributor network with email and phone contact channels. Warranty coverage includes 1 year for earphone body and 3-6 months for cable, which falls below typical 2-year industry standard. The detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable design enhances longevity by allowing cable replacement, addressing the most common failure point in IEMs. Construction utilizes mix of medical-grade PC material and aluminum alloy cavity with moderate complexity and few moving parts. Firmware update support is available through TANCHJIM PC App with latest DSP firmware V1.0.2 addressing microphone functionality issues. Known issues include cable quality concerns with memory and curling reported by users, though replacement cables have been provided to resolve problems. No major hardware failures or recalls documented, but limited support duration and below-average warranty period impact overall reliability assessment.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

TANCHJIM demonstrates largely rational design philosophy through measurement-focused approach and scientific sound quality improvement methodology. The company explicitly uses "scientifically-based measurements" and designs to "specific frequency response targets rather than arbitrary tuning preferences," indicating objective development process. Cost allocation prioritizes performance and function with DSP implementation, advanced driver materials, and proprietary acoustic technologies rather than aesthetics or brand premium. The 29 USD price point for DSP functionality demonstrates cost-effective approach using general-purpose 24-bit DACs and efficient production methods. Model progression from TANCHJIM Zero shows functional improvements including DSP capability, detachable cables, and enhanced comfort through acoustic engineering refinements. Modern efficient approach incorporates software signal processing and mobile app integration for user customization. However, some proprietary claims including "faithfully recreating original sound" lack independent verification, and the measurement-focused evidence could be more comprehensive for complete rationality assessment.

## Advice

For users seeking USB-C digital signal processing capability in budget-friendly IEMs, the TANCHJIM ONE DSP represents the most cost-effective option with validated measurement performance. Third-party measurements confirm low distortion and good frequency response compliance, making it suitable for users prioritizing objective audio quality. The detachable cable design provides upgrade potential and enhanced durability. However, users requiring extended warranty coverage or premium support services should consider higher-tier alternatives. The DSP functionality requires compatible USB-C devices and may not suit users preferring traditional analog connectivity. Cable quality concerns suggest immediate replacement planning may be beneficial. Overall recommended for budget-conscious users seeking modern DSP features with verified measurement performance.
