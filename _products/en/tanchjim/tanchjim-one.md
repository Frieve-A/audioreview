## Overview

The TANCHJIM ONE is a 10mm dynamic driver in-ear monitor positioned in TANCHJIM's portfolio, priced at 27.99 USD. The product features medical-grade high-transparency PC material construction with aluminum alloy cavity and utilizes a patented ternary composite rear cavity design for tuning optimization. Available in multiple variants including 3.5mm analog and USB-C with DSP functionality, the TANCHJIM ONE incorporates interchangeable 0.78mm 2-pin cables and targets affordable access to measurement-driven audio technology.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Third-party measurements from Audio Science Review show THD of 0.062%, indicating excellent distortion control approaching the finest performance levels. Frequency response demonstrates very good to excellent compliance with target curves, with minor boost in 150-350 Hz range and shortfall in 6-9 kHz region. ASR noted "very low" distortion performance that is "just shy of the best we have seen." The 126 dB/Vrms sensitivity and 16Ω impedance specifications were confirmed through independent testing. Overall measured performance demonstrates high competency with comprehensive validation from credible measurement sources.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The TANCHJIM ONE demonstrates above-average technology level through claimed patented Ternary Composite Rear Cavity design and in-house development capabilities. The company operates as an independent design house with experienced R&D team rather than OEM/ODM arrangement. However, core technologies utilize predominantly standard implementations including 10mm dynamic driver size and PU+PEEK composite materials that are now widespread in the market. The proprietary acoustic cavity engineering represents claimed innovation, though patent numbers are not disclosed and competitive advantages beyond marketing claims remain unsubstantiated. USB-C variant includes contemporary DSP integration with 24-bit DAC supporting up to 96kHz sampling rates.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

CP = 23 USD ÷ 27.99 USD = 0.82

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations. The Moondrop Chu II at 23 USD serves as comparison target, demonstrating equivalent-or-better measured performance. Equipped with detachable cable system and 3.5mm connectivity, the Chu II shows THD performance described as "extremely good" by ASR with distortion measured at approximately 0.05% compared to TANCHJIM ONE's 0.062% specification. Frequency response analysis reveals Chu II achieves deviation of approximately ±1.8dB from target versus TANCHJIM ONE's ±2.0dB deviation per ASR measurement, indicating comparable measured performance with the cheaper alternative providing equivalent functionality at 82% of the price.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Simple robust design with medical-grade PC materials and aluminum alloy cavity construction provides inherently reliable structure with minimal failure-prone components. Ultra-lightweight 2.8g per earpiece reduces mechanical stress during use. However, warranty period and coverage details are not officially documented by the manufacturer. Support infrastructure is primarily China-based with limited global coverage. No statistical failure data or track record information is available for assessment. Primary durability concern relates to included cable quality rather than earpiece construction, with driver flex reported on some units depending on ear anatomy and tip selection.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

TANCHJIM ONE exemplifies exceptionally rational design philosophy with measurement-driven development approach. The company explicitly states "quantitative evaluation based on high-precision measurement is the foundation for creating high-performance, exceptional-sounding products," demonstrating scientific focus over subjective claims. Cost allocation prioritizes performance functions including DSP integration and composite diaphragm materials rather than aesthetic premium or brand positioning. Modern efficient technologies are adopted through mass production techniques and tiered product variants allowing scalable features. The claimed proprietary cavity innovations serve specific acoustic purposes rather than marketing differentiation. Active cost reduction is demonstrated through sub-30 USD pricing across all variants while maintaining measurement verification standards.

## Advice

The TANCHJIM ONE offers strong value for measurement-focused listeners seeking performance with third-party validation. ASR's positive assessment and state-of-the-art performance claims with minimal EQ adjustment make this suitable for users prioritizing objective performance metrics. The 0.8 cost-performance score indicates 18% premium over cheapest equivalent alternatives, which may be justified for users valuing the specific acoustic tuning and build quality. Consider the Moondrop Chu II if budget is primary concern and equivalent measured performance is acceptable. The USB-C DSP variant provides additional functionality for users requiring digital connectivity and tuning profiles.
