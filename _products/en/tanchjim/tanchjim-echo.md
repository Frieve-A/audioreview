## Overview

The TANCHJIM ECHO is a true wireless earbud designed for audio enthusiasts seeking low-latency performance. Released in November 2021, the product emphasizes synchronization capabilities and audio quality through aptX Adaptive technology and a 10mm beryllium-plated dynamic driver. TANCHJIM, founded in 2015, has established itself through measurement-focused engineering and proprietary Dynamic Magnetic Technology (DMT) driver designs. The ECHO represents the company's first foray into wireless technology while maintaining their scientific approach to tuning. Currently priced at 99.99 USD, the product is available at multiple major retailers including HiFiGo, Linsoul, ShenZhenAudio, and Apos Audio.

Third-party frequency response measurements from Crinacle (IEC711 coupler) [1] show a V-shaped curve with elevated bass and treble relative to the Harman target, and recessed lower-midrange. The deviation from the Harman target exceeds the ±3dB standard threshold in multiple bands (bass, lower-midrange, and treble regions), placing performance at the average level between problematic and transparent. For TWS, frequency response is the primary evaluable metric; THD, S/N ratio, and IMD are not documented by available third-party sources. Evaluation is based solely on the measured FR deviation from the target curve, not on measurement data availability.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

TANCHJIM ECHO demonstrates above-average technology level through mixed approach combining proprietary driver design with standard components. The 10mm beryllium-plated driver represents TANCHJIM's DMT technology and measurement-focused engineering methodology, which was cutting-edge for TWS category in 2021. The company shows high know-how accumulation through specific frequency response targeting approach rather than arbitrary tuning preferences. However, by 2026, beryllium drivers in TWS have become more commonplace among premium offerings. Other technologies including QCC3040 Bluetooth chip, DSP processing, and binaural mirroring represent standard contemporary implementations. The design demonstrates partial in-house development for acoustic components while utilizing licensed solutions for connectivity and processing functions.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

One-year warranty period for earphones body with six months for accessories falls below industry average standards. Support infrastructure operates through manufacturer direct channels and authorized international distributors including Linsoul, ShenZhenAudio, and HiFiGo, providing global coverage through established retailer networks. Build quality demonstrates moderate complexity with IPX4 waterproof rating and sturdy construction quality noted in available assessments. No widespread reliability issues have been documented, though some units may experience microphone malfunction requiring replacement. Post-warranty repair services are available for additional charges according to manufacturer policy. The primary limitation involves shorter warranty duration compared to competitive offerings in the category.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

TANCHJIM ECHO demonstrates highly rational design philosophy through explicit measurement-focused approach. The company states "quantitative evaluation based on high-precision measurement is the foundation" and designs to "specific frequency response targets rather than arbitrary tuning preferences," indicating objective-first development process [2]. Cost allocation prioritizes performance elements including beryllium-plated driver, DSP chip, and Finite Element Analysis technology over aesthetics or brand premium. Technology choices are modern and efficient, utilizing Bluetooth 5.2, Qualcomm QCC3040 chipset, and digital signal processing. The 99-100 USD pricing focuses on audio performance per dollar with emphasis on sound quality over feature abundance. No subjective, nostalgic, or scientifically questionable approaches are detected in the design methodology or marketing claims.

For potential purchasers seeking true wireless earbuds with measurement-validated performance, the TANCHJIM ECHO offers compelling value through professional frequency response data and comprehensive codec support. The beryllium-plated driver and measurement-focused tuning approach provide objective foundation for audio quality expectations. However, consider the shorter warranty period before purchasing. The product suits users prioritizing audio quality validation over extended feature sets or warranty coverage.
