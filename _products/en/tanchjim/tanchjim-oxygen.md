## Overview

The TANCHJIM Oxygen represents the company's flagship in-ear monitor released in 2019, featuring a 10mm carbon nanotube diaphragm dynamic driver with dual-cavity dual-damping technology. TANCHJIM positioned this product as their breakthrough into the premium IEM market following Harman Target Response curve tuning. Established in 2015 by a team of acoustic engineers with experience at Japanese audio brands, TANCHJIM built their reputation on this model as a scientifically-tuned alternative to subjective-based IEM designs. The Oxygen gained recognition for bringing measurement-based design principles to the sub-300 USD market segment.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Reference Audio Analyzer provides publicly accessible third-party measurements on SIEC stand: sensitivity 128.8 dB/V SPL (112.6 dB/mW) in 100Hz–10kHz band, impedance 23.8 ohm average in 40Hz–15kHz, and frequency response with Harman In-Ear 2019 target comparison [3]. The measured sensitivity exceeds manufacturer specification (110dB), and impedance (23.8 ohm) aligns with the specified 32Ω within typical unit variance. Frequency response exhibits reasonable compliance with Harman target as evidenced by RAA's recommended equalizer settings and placement among similar-tuned products (deviation comparable to Moondrop Aria 2 at 0.37 and Moondrop Lan at 0.58 in RAA's SIEC comparison). Manufacturer specifications indicate THD below 0.2% [1]; RAA does not report THD for this headphone category. The specified THD of <0.2% falls between excellent (0.05% or below) and problematic (0.5% or above) thresholds for IEMs. Additional frequency response documentation from ThePhonograph [2] supports the measurement picture.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The TANCHJIM Oxygen demonstrates in-house design capabilities utilizing carbon nanotube diaphragm technology within a dual-chamber dual-damping configuration representing accumulated acoustic engineering expertise from team members with Japanese audio brand experience. The carbon nanotube implementation incorporates advanced material science for reduced distortion characteristics compared to conventional dynamic drivers. However, this core technology represents established late-2010s innovation that has become widely adopted across the industry by manufacturers including Campfire Audio Solaris, Moondrop KXXS, iBasso IT01S, and BLON BL-03, eliminating technological differentiation advantages. The product maintains analog-only single dynamic driver architecture without integration of modern digital signal processing, software-based acoustic enhancement, or contemporary connectivity features found in cutting-edge implementations. While demonstrating competent engineering execution and proprietary dual-chamber acoustic tuning methods, the underlying technology framework offers limited competitive advantage duration as carbon nanotube driver manufacturing techniques can be replicated by new market entrants within typical development cycles.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

This evaluation is based solely on functions and numerical performance data. Driver types and configurations (dynamic, planar magnetic, BA, hybrid, horn, etc.) are completely excluded from consideration. **Comprehensive Product Analysis:** The TANCHJIM Oxygen at 279.99 USD provides detachable cable functionality via 2-pin 0.78mm connector, 3.5mm gold-plated output connectivity, single 10mm dynamic driver configuration, 32Ω impedance matching, 110dB sensitivity specifications, and manufacturer THD specification below 0.2%. Third-party measurements from Reference Audio Analyzer [3] confirm sensitivity 112.6 dB/mW, impedance 23.8 ohm, and frequency response with Harman target compliance. **Comparison Analysis:** The Moondrop Chu II at 23.00 USD demonstrates equivalent-or-better performance through verified third-party measurements: **THD performance:** Moondrop Chu II shows measured distortion performance significantly better than 0.2% with Audio Science Review assessment indicating very low distortion versus TANCHJIM manufacturer specification below 0.2% [4]. **Frequency Response:** Moondrop Chu II exhibits excellent target compliance with ASR measurement demonstrating small gaps between response and target; Oxygen's RAA data shows comparable Harman target adherence. The comparison product provides equivalent essential user-facing functionality including detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cable design and 3.5mm connectivity. Both products have third-party measured performance data (RAA SIEC for Oxygen, ASR GRAS 45CA for Chu II); Chu II is judged equivalent-or-better in FR and distortion.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

**Warranty Coverage:** One-year warranty period on the IEM with three-month cable coverage falls below industry standard two-year average but reflects the product's simple passive construction characteristics with minimal electronic components prone to failure modes. **Support Infrastructure:** Global support network operates through manufacturer direct channels and authorized distributors including Linsoul Audio, providing reasonable international coverage through both email support systems and telephone contact options for technical assistance and warranty claims processing. **Known Reliability Issues:** Cable quality concerns affect accessory components with reported plastic connector durability limitations and non-standard polarity implementation requiring specific replacement cable compatibility, though these issues affect peripheral components rather than core earphone functionality. **Construction Assessment:** The 304 stainless steel earpiece construction demonstrates robust mechanical design inherently resistant to material degradation and environmental exposure, with dual-chamber acoustic architecture utilizing minimal moving parts that reduce potential failure vectors. The simplified analog dynamic driver configuration eliminates electronic failure modes present in active noise cancellation or digital signal processing implementations, contributing to long-term reliability expectations despite shorter warranty coverage periods.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

TANCHJIM demonstrates largely rational design philosophy grounded in scientific measurement principles rather than subjective audio traditions. **Scientific Approach:** The company explicitly employs "quantitative evaluation based on high-precision measurement" methodologies and designs products to "specific frequency response targets rather than arbitrary tuning preferences," including documented implementation of the Harman Target Response curve developed through controlled listening research [5]. **Functional Cost Allocation:** Carbon nanotube diaphragm technology and dual-chamber acoustic design directly contribute to measurable performance improvements in distortion characteristics and frequency response control, representing rational investment in audible performance factors. **Technology Evolution:** Model progression shows rational cost optimization through simplified packaging and cable configurations while maintaining core acoustic performance, with successor Origin model demonstrating continued technological advancement through DMT 5th generation driver implementation. **Non-Functional Cost Concerns:** However, significant cost allocation toward high-purity gold-silver alloy cables, nano-grade silver ion vacuum plating processes, and manual hand-polishing aesthetic treatments may not contribute measurably to audible performance characteristics. Additionally, unverified claims such as "99% bacteria resistance" lack independent scientific validation, representing marketing-driven rather than evidence-based development aspects. The overall design direction emphasizes measurement-based acoustic engineering while incorporating premium materials and aesthetic elements that may exceed functional performance requirements.

## Advice

The TANCHJIM Oxygen faces significant cost-performance disadvantages at its 280 USD price point given the availability of verified equivalent-performing alternatives priced substantially lower with independent third-party measurement validation. Users prioritizing measurement-based tuning principles and objective performance verification should consider products with comprehensive third-party acoustic analysis. While the carbon nanotube driver technology demonstrates effective implementation, similar advanced driver technologies are now widely available across multiple price segments, eliminating the unique value proposition originally established at product introduction. Potential purchasers should carefully evaluate whether premium construction materials, aesthetic finishing processes, and brand positioning justify the substantial price premium over functionally equivalent alternatives that provide verified equivalent-or-better measured performance. Third-party measurement data from Reference Audio Analyzer is available for frequency response, sensitivity, and impedance; THD remains manufacturer-specified only.
