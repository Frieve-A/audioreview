## Overview

The Letshuoer Cadenza4 is a hybrid in-ear monitor featuring a sophisticated driver configuration combining a 10mm dual-chambered beryllium coated dynamic driver with three balanced armature drivers from Knowles and Sonion. Priced at 249 USD, this IEM employs advanced manufacturing techniques including 50μm 3D printed acoustic tubes and a four-way electronic crossover system. The product represents Letshuoer's "return to basics" philosophy, emphasizing natural, balanced tuning over exotic driver configurations. Founded in 2016 by IEM enthusiasts with 16-year average experience, Letshuoer has established itself as a manufacturer focused on measurement-based tuning and technical refinement.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

The Cadenza4 demonstrates high technical sophistication through advanced manufacturing and driver implementation. The 10mm dual-chambered beryllium coated dynamic driver utilizes lightweight, rigid materials to improve response speed and reduce nonlinear distortion [3]. Three balanced armature drivers from established manufacturers Knowles and Sonion handle mid and high frequencies through a four-way electronic crossover system [3]. The implementation of 50μm 3D printed acoustic tubes represents contemporary manufacturing technology, enabling precise resonance control and consistent sound delivery [4]. Collaboration with HeyGears for 3D printing demonstrates adoption of cutting-edge production methods. The CNC aluminum alloy chassis with 3D printed resin housing combines durability with weight optimization. The 392-strand silver-plated monocrystalline copper cable with modular connectors (2.5mm, 3.5mm, 4.4mm) provides practical functionality. This technology level reflects significant engineering investment and contemporary manufacturing approaches.

## Reliability & Support

## Advice

The Letshuoer Cadenza4 delivers competent reference monitoring with contemporary manufacturing quality, though pricing limits its value proposition compared to alternatives. The hybrid configuration provides balanced frequency response suitable for extended listening sessions without fatigue, albeit with frequency response deviations that fall short of transparent reproduction standards. Users seeking similar functionality should consider the Truthear Zero Red at 55 USD, which offers comparable reference tuning and detachable cable system at significantly lower cost. The Cadenza4's advanced 3D printing technology and beryllium coating provide marginal improvements over alternatives but insufficient to justify the 353% price premium. This product suits users specifically requiring the exact driver configuration and willing to pay premium for incremental refinements. Budget-conscious users prioritizing core functionality should evaluate equivalent alternatives offering similar user benefits at substantially reduced cost. The modular cable system and build quality remain strengths, though warranty coverage falls below industry standards. Users demanding maximum value should explore competing products providing equivalent functions and measured performance at substantially reduced pricing.
