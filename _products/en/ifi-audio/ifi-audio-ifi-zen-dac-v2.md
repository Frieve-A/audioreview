## Overview

The iFi Zen DAC V2 is a half-width desktop USB DAC and headphone amplifier launched in May 2021 by iFi audio, a UK brand operating as part of Abbingdon Music Research (AMR) in Southport. It combines a Burr-Brown "True Native" DAC chip, a 16-core XMOS USB receiver, a full MQA decoder, balanced 4.4 mm and single-ended 6.3 mm headphone outputs, and 4.4 mm balanced plus RCA line outputs, all USB-bus-powered. Launch MSRP was 189 USD, last listed US retail 199 USD. The product is now officially superseded by the Zen DAC 3 (2024) and is listed as discontinued or out of stock at several major US dealers [1][5].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Manufacturer specifications for the line section are excellent: THD+N <0.0015% at 0 dBFS, SNR <-116 dB(A) and dynamic range >116 dB(A) on both RCA and 4.4 mm balanced outputs [1]. Third-party headphone-section measurements are markedly weaker than the line section: THD of 0.018%, THD+N of approximately -71.7 dB(A), dynamic range of 94.8 dB(A) and noise of -94.9 dB(A) [3]. Crosstalk of -78.1 dB, IMD of 0.010% and frequency response of +0.03 / -0.32 dB (40 Hz – 15 kHz) are clean, and Reference Audio Analyzer shows headphone output impedance of approximately 0.1 Ω [2]. Several headphone-section indicators sit between excellent and problematic levels rather than clearing the highest tier, so overall measured performance is moderate rather than top-class.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The Zen DAC V2 is designed in-house by iFi audio, which earns it credit for genuine engineering ownership in this segment. Beyond that the technical content is a competent integration of widely available third-party silicon: the Burr-Brown True Native DAC chip belongs to a generation in production since the late 2000s, the XMOS xCORE-200 16-core USB receiver is from a class that has been on the market since the mid-2010s and is shared across nearly every competing brand, and the MQA full decoder is licensed from a company that entered administration in 2023, making it obsolescent rather than forward-looking. Differentiated names – GMT clock, TrueBass, PowerMatch – describe a standard low-jitter crystal and buffer, a standard analog bass-boost circuit and a standard two-position gain switch; none are patents competitors must license, and equivalent functions exist on rival products at the same price point. Model progression is also weak: V1 to V2 was an incremental USB-controller refresh, and the V2 to Zen DAC 3 transition shows mixed measurement movement (unchanged THD, slightly worse SNR, lower single-ended power), so there is no durable technological moat [1].

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

The current US retail price of the iFi Zen DAC V2 is 199 USD [1]. The cheapest equivalent-or-better desktop USB DAC and headphone amplifier with credible third-party measurements is the SMSL DL100 at 179 USD [5], which has been measured in depth by Audio Science Review [4].

CP = 179 USD / 199 USD = 0.90

The SMSL DL100 is equipped with USB input, 6.3 mm single-ended and 4.4 mm balanced headphone outputs, RCA and balanced line outputs, PCM up to 384 kHz, DSD256 and full MQA decoding (adding HDMI ARC, optical, coaxial and Bluetooth inputs plus a remote on top), and demonstrates equivalent-or-better measured performance:

- THD+N (line out): 0.00009% vs 0.0015% (comparison better) [4]
- Dynamic Range (line out): approximately 130 dB(A) vs >116 dB(A) (comparison better) [4]
- S/N Ratio (line out): 132 dB vs -116 dB(A) (comparison better) [4]
- THD (headphone output): negligible distortion at maximum volume per ASR vs 0.018% (comparison better) [4]
- Frequency Response (headphone output): flat audioband vs +0.03 / -0.32 dB (comparison equivalent) [4]
- Output Impedance (headphone, SE): near-zero vs approximately 0.1 Ω (comparison equivalent) [4]

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

iFi audio provides global manufacturer-direct support with regional offices in the UK, USA, Netherlands, Hong Kong and mainland China, and has a documented track record of repairing iFi products more than a decade after release [1]. The Zen DAC V2 has a simple all-metal construction with no moving parts beyond the volume potentiometer and switches, making it inherently robust against degradation. Firmware updates are distributed via the official Download Hub and several revisions have been issued since launch. The main weakness is the warranty period: 1 year for the US and UK (2 years in the EU). Reported physical issues are limited to a low-volume channel imbalance typical of analog potentiometers and isolated reports of a tight USB-B socket, with no documented widespread failures, recalls or service bulletins [3].

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The Zen DAC V2's stated philosophy leans toward subjective and conservative choices rather than measurement-driven optimization. iFi explicitly rejects DSP in favor of analog signal processing as a brand identity, and selects Burr-Brown silicon over higher-SINAD ESS or AKM alternatives available at the same price tier – both are voicing and identity decisions rather than performance ones. The product makes audibility claims for elements without demonstrated audible benefit: a full MQA decoder (MQA Ltd. has since entered administration, and blind tests do not show audible benefit over lossless), a GMT femto-clock claim of more than 20 dB jitter reduction (not third-party verified at system level), and TrueBass voicing claims (no third-party frequency response plots verifying "no muddying of mid-range"). The model trajectory is incremental and non-monotonic: V2 added an XMOS 16-core USB controller and full MQA decoding versus V1 but kept the same DAC chip and analog architecture, while the Zen DAC 3 successor shows unchanged THD, slightly worse SNR and reduced single-ended power [1]. On the rational side, the product is a genuine dedicated audio device with a coherent function set and does not use tubes, R2R or analog records, so it avoids the most negative philosophical indicators.

## Advice

The Zen DAC V2 is most useful to listeners who specifically want a single USB-bus-powered box with a 4.4 mm balanced headphone output, MQA full decoding and a switchable analog bass boost, ideally feeding moderately efficient over-ear headphones. Listeners driving low-impedance IEMs or sensitive monitors should be aware that the headphone section measures noticeably worse than the line section, so very revealing transducers may expose its dynamic range and THD+N. From a pure measurement and value standpoint, the SMSL DL100 at 179 USD offers cleaner DAC and headphone-amp performance, broader connectivity (HDMI ARC, optical, coaxial, Bluetooth) and a remote, and prospective buyers should consider it before paying the Zen DAC V2's last-listed 199 USD, especially as the V2 has been officially superseded by the Zen DAC 3 in iFi's current catalog [1][4][5].
