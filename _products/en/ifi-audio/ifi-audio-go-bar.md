# iFi audio GO bar Product Review

## Overview

The iFi audio GO bar is an ultraportable USB DAC and headphone amplifier released in May 2022, priced at 339 USD for the standard edition. Despite its compact USB stick-sized form factor measuring 66mm × 23mm × 13mm and weighing just 28g, it delivers advanced audio processing capabilities including 32-bit/384kHz PCM support, DSD256 decoding, full MQA capability, and up to 475mW output power. Key features include a 4.4mm balanced output, 3.5mm S-balanced output, proprietary XBass+ and XSpace analog processing, iEMatch gain switching for sensitive IEMs, and four selectable digital filters. iFi audio, a subsidiary of Abbingdon Music Research, positions the GO bar as a "portable audio powerhouse" targeting audiophiles seeking high-quality portable audio enhancement.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

Manufacturer specifications claim SNR of 132dB balanced and 108dB unbalanced, THD below 0.002% for balanced output, and frequency response from 20Hz to 45kHz with -3dB points. Third-party measurements from SoundStage Network [1] demonstrate exceptional performance across all key metrics. THD measured at 0.0023% indicates excellent distortion control, while THD+N of 0.0026% confirms superior linearity. The S/N ratio of 127dB represents world-class noise performance far exceeding typical portable devices. Frequency response spans 20Hz to 45.9kHz with minimal deviation, providing comprehensive bandwidth coverage. IMD measurements show excellent results with CCIF at -80dB and SMPTE at -86dB, indicating clean signal reproduction. Crosstalk performance of -83.5dB/-73.6dB between channels demonstrates proper channel separation. Dynamic range of 113.3dB confirms high-resolution capability. Independent verification by Archimago's Musings [2] corroborates these findings with THD+N measurements of -93dB. The comprehensive measurement data consistently demonstrates performance levels that significantly exceed industry standards across all evaluated parameters.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

The GO bar demonstrates solid technological sophistication through multiple proprietary implementations and enhanced standard components. Core technology includes a Cirrus Logic CS43131 DAC paired with a custom-programmed 16-core XMOS microcontroller offering double the processing capability of standard 8-core implementations. iFi's proprietary GMT (Global Master Timing) precision clock system provides ultra-low jitter performance, while custom digital filters including the proprietary GTO (Gibbs Transient Optimized) mode offer advanced signal processing options. The iEMatch technology provides adjustable gain reduction specifically designed for sensitive IEM compatibility, and XBass+/XSpace analog processing delivers hardware-based audio enhancement. While core components utilize established technology, iFi's enhanced implementations, custom firmware development, and accumulated know-how from the AMR heritage represent meaningful technological advancement above typical products in this category.

## Cost-Performance

CP = 1.0 (no cheaper equivalent-or-better product exists)

After comprehensive evaluation across multiple price ranges from 9 USD to 339 USD, no products were identified that combine equivalent-or-better measured performance with the GO bar's comprehensive feature set at a lower price. The exceptional 127dB SNR performance, combined with comprehensive DSD256 support, full MQA decoding capability, 4.4mm balanced output, advanced DSP features (XBass+, XSpace, iEMatch), and 475mW power output, establishes a unique combination in the portable DAC/amplifier market. Competing products such as the Qudelix 5K (109 USD) and FiiO BTR7 (200 USD) show inferior SNR performance despite having credible third-party measurements, while products lacking adequate measurement verification cannot be considered valid comparison targets. The iFi GO bar represents the most cost-effective option available for users requiring this specific combination of measured performance and advanced functionality.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The GO bar presents mixed reliability and support characteristics with both strengths and limitations. Positive aspects include manufacturer direct support through iFi audio's global system with online ticket submission and two-business-day response times. The solid-state electronic design with minimal mechanical components provides inherently robust construction, and active firmware update support demonstrates ongoing product maintenance. Warranty coverage varies by region with 1 year in the US and UK, extending to 2 years in the EU per local requirements. Some platform-specific limitations exist including firmware updates requiring Windows computers, occasional power draw compatibility considerations with certain mobile devices, and Host Volume Sync functionality that works best with Windows systems. While repair services are available with 100-day warranty coverage on parts and labor, users should consider their specific platform requirements when evaluating compatibility across different operating systems and mobile devices.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

iFi audio demonstrates a contradictory design philosophy combining rational engineering approaches with problematic subjective evaluation advocacy. Positive aspects include significant cost allocation toward measurable performance improvements through advanced DSP processing, precision GMT clock systems, and quality component selection including premium capacitors and custom op-amps. The technology approach shows rational integration of digital and analog domains using modern DSP for enhancement features while maintaining proper analog output stages. Model progression from GO blu (199 USD) to GO bar (339 USD) demonstrates clear functional advancement and appropriate price-performance scaling. However, the company's public advocacy for "feelings matter more than specs" and encouragement to "stop obsessing over specs" represents an irrational philosophical stance contrary to scientific audio evaluation principles. Additionally, substantial investment in premium materials such as 18ct gold plating for the Anniversary Edition contributes to cost without measurable audio performance benefit, indicating mixed cost optimization priorities.

## Advice

The iFi audio GO bar represents the optimal choice for users requiring the highest measured performance in a portable DAC/amplifier with comprehensive format support and advanced features. Its exceptional 127dB SNR, comprehensive DSD256/MQA capability, balanced output, and advanced analog processing make it particularly suitable for high-resolution audio enthusiasts and users with sensitive or demanding headphones. However, potential buyers should consider platform compatibility requirements, particularly the Windows-only firmware update limitation and documented power draw issues with certain mobile devices. Users prioritizing basic functionality over advanced features may find better value in simpler alternatives, while those requiring extensive wireless connectivity should consider Bluetooth-capable options despite their inferior measured performance. The GO bar is most appropriate for users who can leverage its advanced technical capabilities and accept the associated compatibility constraints.
