## Overview

The Wharfedale Super Linton represents an elevated evolution of the acclaimed Linton 85th Anniversary design, incorporating significant engineering improvements while maintaining the company's heritage of measurement-focused development. This 3-way bass-reflex stand-mount speaker features dual-layer cabinet construction with latex-based damping, upgraded driver motor systems, and a completely redesigned crossover network. With 90dB sensitivity, 39Hz-20kHz frequency response, and optional matching stands, the Super Linton targets audiophiles seeking premium bookshelf performance at 2,499 USD.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The Super Linton demonstrates solid measurable performance with frequency response of 39Hz-20kHz (±3dB) meeting standard levels for bookshelf speakers, where ±3dB within usage band represents acceptable performance. Bass extension to 32Hz (-6dB) exceeds transparent level expectations for the category. The 90dB sensitivity significantly surpasses problematic levels, providing efficient operation with various amplifiers. Third-party measurements from Erin's Audio Corner [1] provide detailed harmonic distortion data: at 86dB @ 1m, THD remains below -40dB (approximately 1% or less) across most frequencies from 100Hz to 10kHz, with peaks reaching approximately -30dB (approximately 3%) around 1.5kHz and at 20kHz. In the bass region (20-50Hz), THD rises to approximately -20dB (approximately 10%), exceeding the problematic threshold of 1%. The distortion profile shows generally low levels in the midrange (300Hz-800Hz) with THD around -60dB (approximately 0.1%), meeting excellent criteria. Dynamic compression remains minimal across the 76-102dB SPL testing range. The available data places performance between problematic and transparent levels, with excellent midrange performance but problematic bass distortion levels.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The Super Linton employs competent contemporary engineering with sophisticated acoustic design elements. Dual-layer MDF cabinet construction with latex-based damping demonstrates rational resonance control implementation. The dedicated midrange chamber incorporating long-hair fiber absorption represents sound driver isolation principles. Crossover design shows technical advancement through split circuit boards reducing interference, air-core inductors for midrange and tweeter sections, and proprietary "Super-Power" laminated silicon-iron core inductor for bass driver. Tweeter technology incorporates ceramic magnet motor system derived from flagship Dovedale model. However, the fundamental design remains traditional passive 3-way technology without cutting-edge digital integration or significant proprietary innovations requiring extended development timeframes for competitors to replicate.

Wharfedale demonstrates exemplary rational design philosophy aligned with scientific validation principles. Company pioneered laser interferometry for speaker cone analysis in 1970s, establishing measurement-focused development approach emphasizing "accuracy and neutrality in loudspeaker design."
