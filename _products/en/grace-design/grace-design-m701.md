## Overview

The Grace Design m701 represents the Colorado-based company's first complete audio interface, leveraging their 25+ years of converter and analog circuit expertise built since the 1990s. This modular professional recording interface combines Grace Design's proprietary trans-impedance amplifier technology with modern connectivity, offering up to 64 analog channels and 212 digital I/O channels in a configurable 2U platform. Positioned at 2995 USD, the m701 targets professional recording facilities requiring high-channel-count I/O with reference-quality conversion. Grace Design's family-owned heritage and focus on engineering fundamentals over rapid market expansion underlies their methodical approach to developing this first interface product, incorporating technologies refined across their established preamp and monitor controller lines.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

Third-party measurements from Sound on Sound [1] demonstrate exceptional measured performance across all parameters. THD+N measures 0.0002% for A-D conversion and 0.00055% for D-A conversion, indicating excellent distortion control. Dynamic range achieves 121.1dB for A-D conversion and 126.7dB for D-A conversion, representing outstanding signal-to-noise performance. Crosstalk measures -122dB at 10kHz, demonstrating excellent channel separation. Sound on Sound confirmed the m701 outperformed established competitors like Prism Sound's Dream ADA-128 by over 2dB in both A-D and D-A measurements, positioning it among the industry's top converters. All measured specifications demonstrate world-class performance levels that significantly exceed typical professional interface standards.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

The m701 demonstrates exceptional technology level through multiple proprietary innovations and cutting-edge implementation. Grace Design's signature trans-impedance amplifier technology provides current feedback designs enabling precise waveform tracking and harmonic structure resolution, representing 30+ years of accumulated expertise. The modular 8-slot architecture allows flexible configuration up to 64 analog channels, incorporating modern digital connectivity including Dante, Ravenna/AES67/ST2110, and Avid DigiLink protocols. GraceNet browser-based control eliminates software installation requirements while providing comprehensive configuration capabilities. Multiple filter options including minimum-phase, linear-phase, hybrid, and apodizing filters address upstream conversion artifacts. The proprietary 32×8 low-latency cue mixer and dual redundant power supply design demonstrate sophisticated engineering integration. All technologies represent genuine in-house development with measurable performance advantages that establish lasting competitive differentiation.

## Advice

The Grace Design m701 suits professional recording facilities requiring high-channel-count I/O with reference-quality conversion performance. The modular design provides future expandability for growing studio requirements, while the 5-year transferable warranty supports long-term investment value. Consider the base unit pricing requires additional modules for full 64-channel analog configuration, increasing total system cost. The proven reliability engineering and manufacturer support make this appropriate for mission-critical recording environments where downtime costs exceed the premium pricing.
