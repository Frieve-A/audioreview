## Overview

The Marantz SR7005 is a 7.1-channel AV receiver from the 2010-2011 era that was originally priced at 1600 USD. It features 125 watts per channel amplification with current feedback topology in the preamplifier stage and discrete amplification circuits. The receiver supports comprehensive audio formats including Dolby TrueHD and DTS-HD Master Audio, offers 192kHz/24-bit D/A conversion, and includes Audyssey MultEQ XT room correction. With 6 HDMI inputs (1.4a standard) and extensive connectivity options, it represented a solid mid-to-high-end offering during its production period. The unit has been discontinued and is no longer available as a new product.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

The SR7005 incorporates current feedback topology in the preamplifier stage, representing proprietary Marantz technology distinct from conventional voltage feedback designs. Discrete amplification circuits with modular construction demonstrate significant engineering expertise and technical sophistication for the era. The receiver utilizes Analog Devices SHARC 32-bit digital signal processing for audio decoding and room correction functions. However, from a 2025 evaluation perspective, the technology represents mature implementations that were contemporary in 2010-2011 but are now superseded by more advanced approaches. The combination of analog amplification expertise with appropriate digital processing integration shows solid technical implementation, though without cutting-edge innovation that would be competitive with current technology standards.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

Marantz provides 3-year warranty coverage for AV receivers, exceeding typical industry standards. The discrete amplification design and modular construction suggest inherently robust architecture resistant to component degradation. Global support infrastructure includes authorized service facilities for both in-warranty and out-of-warranty repairs. However, the SR7005 has documented issues with I/O Controller Chip failure on the Main Board, typically caused by static discharge, resulting in video output malfunction. This represents a specific reliability concern that affects long-term dependability. Service manuals remain available through third-party technical resources, supporting continued serviceability despite discontinuation.

As a discontinued product, the SR7005 should only be considered from the used market where cost-performance analysis reveals significant pricing disadvantages compared to equivalent era alternatives.
