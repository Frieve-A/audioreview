## Overview

The Classic Pro CRM1 is an electret condenser measurement microphone launched in September 2025 by Sound House's professional audio brand Classic Pro [1]. Designed for acoustic measurement applications, the CRM1 features an omnidirectional pickup pattern with 20 Hz–20 kHz frequency response and standard XLR connectivity [2]. As part of Classic Pro's cost-performance focused lineup, the CRM1 targets entry-level measurement applications where budget constraints are primary considerations. The microphone employs conventional electret technology with FET preamp and pressure element design for omnidirectional characteristics suitable for room measurement and basic acoustic analysis [2].

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

Current market price of 19.87 USD positions the CRM1 as the most affordable option among measurement microphones with equivalent or better specifications. Comprehensive analysis of competing products reveals no cheaper alternatives meeting basic measurement requirements: Superlux ECM999 (35.50 USD) [3] offers inferior S/N ratio (70 dB) and maximum SPL (132 dB); Behringer ECM8000 (49.99 USD) [4] lacks complete specification disclosure; miniDSP UMIK-1 (129.98 USD) [5] provides superior performance (110 dB S/N, calibration files) but at 6.5x price premium. Equipped with omnidirectional pattern, 20 Hz–20 kHz response, 76 dB S/N ratio, and 134 dB maximum SPL, specifications meet basic measurement functionality requirements while maintaining significant cost advantages over comparable products.

## Advice

The Classic Pro CRM1 serves budget-conscious users requiring basic measurement functionality for room analysis, speaker positioning, or educational applications. At 19.87 USD, it represents the most affordable entry point into measurement microphone capabilities while maintaining adequate specifications for non-critical measurement tasks. Consider upgrading to miniDSP UMIK-1 or equivalent if calibration files, higher precision, or professional measurement accuracy are required. The CRM1's 76 dB S/N ratio and 18 dB equivalent input noise limit precision in low-level measurement scenarios but remain sufficient for typical room measurement applications. Sound House's 3-year warranty and technical support provide reasonable assurance for entry-level purchase decisions. Avoid if measurement precision requirements exceed entry-level specifications or if professional calibration documentation is mandatory for your application.
