## Overview

The Astell & Kern AK100II is a discontinued portable digital audio player released in 2014 [1], featuring a single Cirrus Logic CS4398 DAC and premium aluminum construction. As one of the early high-end DAPs targeting audiophile users, it offered 64GB internal storage with microSD expansion up to 128GB [1], support for high-resolution audio formats up to 24-bit/192kHz PCM and DSD64/128, and both balanced (2.5mm) and unbalanced (3.5mm) outputs. Note: third-party reviews indicate conflicting storage specifications, with some sources reporting 32GB [2]. The device runs on a custom operating system with a 3.31" AMOLED touchscreen interface [1], representing Astell & Kern's attempt to establish the premium portable audio market segment during the early DAP era.

## Advice

For current purchasers, the Astell & Kern AK100II represents poor value proposition due to technology obsolescence and availability of superior alternatives [2]. Users seeking high-resolution portable audio should consider modern DAPs like the FiiO M21, which provides equivalent functionality with advanced technology at significantly reduced cost [3].
