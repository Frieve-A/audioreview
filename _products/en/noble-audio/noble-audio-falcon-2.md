## Overview

The Noble Audio FALCON2 represents the company's first venture into completely wireless earphones, developed under the guidance of Dr. John Moulton, an audiologist known as "Wizard" who founded Noble Audio in 2013. As the successor to the original FALCON, the FALCON2 aims to embody high quality and ease of use with aptX Adaptive codec support, 10-hour battery life, and IPX7 water resistance. Noble Audio brings their established expertise in custom in-ear monitors to the true wireless segment, emphasizing scientific principles informed by Dr. Moulton's audiological background.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Noble Audio provides standard support infrastructure but falls short with only 12-month warranty coverage for TWS products, significantly below the industry standard of 2 years for electronics [4]. The warranty is non-transferable, valid only for original purchasers from authorized sources, with standard mail-in repair process to their Texas facility. The company maintains an established presence in the IEM market since 2013, providing confidence in long-term support availability. However, warranty exclusions include loss, theft, damage from misuse, and accessories are typically not covered, representing standard but not exceptional support terms.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Noble Audio demonstrates rational scientific foundation through Dr. Moulton's audiological expertise and explicit rejection of subjective marketing claims, maintaining a "no bull" operational philosophy that avoids audio industry "snake oil" approaches [5]. The combination of rigorous scientific principles with practical hearing science knowledge represents sound design rationale. However, the company's traditional focus on exotic materials and artisanal craftsmanship may contribute costs unrelated to measured audio performance. The FALCON2 represents a conservative approach to TWS technology implementation without notable innovation or advanced functional integration such as DSP, AI, or sophisticated software features that could enhance user experience or performance.
