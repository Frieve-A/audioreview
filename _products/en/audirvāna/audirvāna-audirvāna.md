## Overview

Audirvāna is a digital music player software developed by French company Audirvāna SAS, founded in Paris in 2011 by Damien Plisson. The software transforms computers into high-performance digital audio sources through direct-to-DAC access, bypassing default system audio parameters. Audirvāna offers two distinct products: Studio (subscription-based with streaming integration) at 85 USD annually, and Origin (one-time purchase for local library playback only) at 119.90 USD. Studio version features integrated Qobuz and TIDAL streaming, 10-band parametric EQ, convolution engine for room correction, and cross-platform support including mobile devices. The latest Studio 3.0 release introduced the unified "Allegro" interface across Mac, Windows, Linux, iOS, and Android platforms.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Audirvāna provides global online support through comprehensive help center and community forum with official development team monitoring. Refund policy covers accidental purchases and unwanted subscription renewals, processed within days. Regular software updates maintain platform compatibility and feature expansion, with Studio 3.0 released March 2026 featuring expanded DSP suite and redesigned interface. However, documented stability issues affect reliability assessment. Linux platform experiences crashes when switching machines and changing DACs, with frequent Remote app disconnections reported. Windows users report occasional application crashes, and Studio 3.0 introduced high GPU utilization issues reaching 40-60 percent even when running in background. Despite active development addressing reported bugs and three-year development cycles, platform-specific stability concerns particularly on Linux systems impact overall reliability evaluation.

## Advice

Audirvāna Studio suits users requiring integrated streaming with advanced DSP processing capabilities, particularly those needing cross-platform compatibility including mobile device control. The parametric EQ and convolution engine provide genuine room correction benefits for critical listening environments. Audirvāna Origin serves users exclusively managing local libraries who prefer one-time purchases over subscription models. Consider platform-specific stability reports before purchase, especially for Linux environments where documented issues may affect user experience. Users requiring only basic playback without streaming integration or advanced DSP processing may find free alternatives like foobar2000 sufficient for their requirements, though without mobile or cross-platform support.
