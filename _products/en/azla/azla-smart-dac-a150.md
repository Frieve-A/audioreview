## Overview

The AZLA Smart DAC A150 is a USB-C to 3.5mm DAC cable that succeeds the company's A100 model, supporting UAC 2.0 and high-resolution audio up to 384kHz/32-bit. At 14 USD, this 7cm cable features silver-plated OFC wiring with weaving shield processing, aluminum housing, and 4-pole microphone input support. Compatible with PCs, smartphones, tablets, and gaming devices including Nintendo Switch 2, the A150 includes a USB-A adapter and carrying pouch. AZLA, a Korean audio brand established in 2017, emphasizes scientific approaches to audio product development.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

The A150's reliability and support profile falls significantly below industry standards. The product carries only a 6-month manufacturer warranty compared to typical 2-year coverage for electronic accessories, while accessories receive merely 1-week initial defect coverage [3]. The integrated cable design creates additional failure vectors compared to rigid dongle alternatives, with cable-type adapters being inherently more susceptible to connector wear and cable damage under repeated bending stress. Support infrastructure appears primarily dealer-based through distributor channels, with limited clarity regarding long-term repair services or parts availability. The manufacturer specifically disclaims responsibility for equipment compatibility failures, further limiting support scope. Combined with the short warranty period and cable construction vulnerability, these factors substantially reduce confidence in product longevity and post-purchase support quality.

## Advice

For users requiring basic USB-C to 3.5mm DAC conversion at competitive pricing, the A150 offers contemporary specifications without significant cost premium. The product suits general-purpose applications including smartphone audio, laptop connections, and gaming device compatibility where measurement transparency is not critical. However, users prioritizing long-term reliability should consider alternatives with standard 2-year warranties and rigid dongle construction. For critical audio applications requiring verified performance specifications, postpone purchase until comprehensive third-party measurements become available or consider alternatives with published measurement data. The 6-month warranty period makes this unsuitable for professional or high-usage scenarios. Budget-conscious users seeking basic high-resolution DAC functionality will find adequate value, while measurement-focused audiophiles should explore options with documented performance specifications.
