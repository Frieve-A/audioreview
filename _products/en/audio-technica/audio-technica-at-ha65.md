## Overview

The Audio-Technica AT-HA65 is a 5-channel headphone amplifier released in April 2010, designed for professional and semi-professional applications requiring simultaneous headphone monitoring. Audio-Technica, founded in 1962, has established a reputation for precision engineering and accessible high-quality audio products. The AT-HA65 features both analog and digital inputs, individual volume controls for each channel, and cascade connectivity for expanded headphone connections. **Important note: This product is officially discontinued ("生産完了") by Audio-Technica and is only available through the used market.** Despite the company's long-standing heritage and mission to provide precision audio equipment, this particular product faces significant challenges in contemporary audio performance standards when compared to current alternatives.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Based on manufacturer specifications, the AT-HA65 demonstrates problematic performance levels across multiple critical measurement criteria. Frequency response is specified at 20-20kHz (-3dB), placing it exactly at the problematic level boundary according to established audibility thresholds. The signal-to-noise ratio of 97dB falls between problematic (80dB) and transparent (105dB) levels, while the total harmonic distortion specification of 0.1% sits precisely at the problematic threshold. These measurements indicate performance that may introduce audible degradation under typical listening conditions. The maximum output of 300mW per channel at 32Ω provides adequate power for most headphone applications, though this represents basic functionality rather than exceptional performance. Multiple indicators clustering around problematic level boundaries demonstrate that this amplifier struggles to achieve transparent audio reproduction by contemporary measurement standards.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The AT-HA65 employs mature analog amplification technology with basic digital input capabilities supporting up to 96kHz/24-bit resolution. Released in 2010, the design reflects technology standards of that era without adoption of cutting-edge innovations or proprietary advancements that would distinguish it from contemporary solutions. The inclusion of optical digital input and output pass-through represents standard professional features, while the cascade connection capability provides functional utility for larger installations. However, the product demonstrates no evidence of patented technology, advanced signal processing, or integration of modern approaches such as DSP, advanced analog circuits, or computer-based control systems. The half-rack form factor and basic external power supply design represent conventional approaches without technological sophistication. As a discontinued product from 2010, it represents the technological capabilities available at that time without subsequent updates or improvements that would reflect advances in the state of the art.

## Cost-Performance

The Fosi Audio PH05, priced at 49.99 USD new, provides equivalent 5-channel headphone amplification with superior measured performance. Equipped with equivalent 5-channel amplification and independent volume control, the PH05 demonstrates superior specifications: power output (310mW vs 300mW), SNR (105dB vs 97dB), and THD (0.001% vs 0.1%) are equivalent-or-better. When comparing actual purchase options available to consumers, the used AT-HA65 represents excellent value as it can be acquired at significantly lower cost than equivalent new alternatives. Since no cheaper equivalent-or-better product exists in the used market, the AT-HA65 achieves maximum cost-performance score, making it an attractive budget option for users comfortable with purchasing discontinued professional equipment.
