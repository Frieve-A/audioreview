## Overview

The Audio-Technica ATH-910 is a full-size closed-back dynamic headphone originating from the 1980s–1990s, marketed under a professional monitoring positioning. A closely related variant, the ATH-910PRO, was distributed in European markets with substantially identical stated specifications [1]. Both models are discontinued; no new units are available in the US market, and the product trades exclusively on the secondhand market at approximately 40 USD [2]. Audio-Technica's current professional closed-back lineup — ATH-M50x and ATH-M70x — represents the company's present product generation.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

On this site, Technology Level is judged against present-day engineering practice, not reconstructed from supplier options typical at introduction. In that framing, the ATH-910 employs standard passive dynamic headphone construction with no identifiable proprietary or patented innovations [3]. For historical context: a large-format dynamic driver with a high-energy permanent magnet such as samarium cobalt was a conventional premium approach in portable and professional headphones during the ATH-910's era, before neodymium-iron-boron magnets became the dominant mass-market solution. Measured against current practice, the passive acoustic enclosure and fixed wired path are fully commoditized, and the product includes no DSP, wireless, ANC, or digital integration. No ATH-910-specific patents were identified in a search of the Audio-Technica patent portfolio [3]. Audio-Technica's historically in-house transducer development origin slightly offsets an otherwise strongly negative position relative to modern integration and differentiation.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The chassis is mechanically conventional for sealed dynamic headphones with no motorized parts or batteries, but the tethered cord is repeatedly flexed at the earcup exits and mechanically consumable compared with headsets offering detachable cabling. Passive electronics lowers intrinsic circuit hazard, yet without statistically compiled failure audits there is slim objective basis to pronounce this headband assembly categorically tougher than contemporaneous peers operating under similar workloads. Everyday secondhand buyers should assume no practical factory warranty channel, weak manufacturer parts visibility, uncertain authorized repair access, and repair economics dominated by independent technicians, DIY work, or uneconomical quotes—conditions typical once model-specific support programs close. Surviving units decades after manufacture provide anecdotal longevity hints [2] but do not erase those purchase-time service downsides. Net of those trade-offs, the assessment lands clearly below the midpoint for this category.

## Advice

The ATH-910 is a vintage secondhand product for which no third-party acoustic measurements exist, and whose sole manufacturer audio specification (nominal frequency response range) lacks the tolerance data required for objective assessment. Equivalent-or-better wired headphone functionality backed by clearer manufacturer specs and broader serviceability is readily available new in current markets. Buyers specifically seeking an Audio-Technica closed-back monitoring headphone should consider current production models such as the ATH-M50x, which offer publicly available third-party measurement data and ongoing manufacturer support, providing a substantially more defensible purchasing basis. There is no evidence-based argument for preferring the ATH-910 over current-generation alternatives on measured acoustic performance grounds.
