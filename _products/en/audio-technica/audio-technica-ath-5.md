## Overview

The Audio-Technica ATH-5 is a wired over-ear dynamic headphone released in 1977 as part of Audio-Technica's second-generation ATH lineup, introduced to mark the company's 15th anniversary [1]. Produced in Japan alongside the ATH-3 and ATH-4, it was the top dynamic model of that generation. The product has been discontinued for approximately 50 years; Audio-Technica's official product page no longer exists, and the headphone is available exclusively through secondary markets.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Specifications from Signet TK22 documentation — the claimed North American equivalent of the ATH-5, though this equivalency is unconfirmed by Audio-Technica [2] — indicate a frequency response range of 20Hz–20,000Hz with no deviation tolerance stated, and a sensitivity of 96dB/mW. The only available THD figure (0.4% at 110dB SPL) is a rated-output specification unsuitable for assessing typical operating distortion. No independent third-party measurements are available for this product. With no evaluable audio performance data, Scientific Validity cannot be determined and is set at 0.5.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The ATH-5 was developed in-house by Audio-Technica, applying phono cartridge transducer manufacturing expertise to dynamic headphone driver production in Japan. Evaluated from a 2026 standpoint, however, the product employs standard moving-coil dynamic driver technology that was already the commodity approach in 1977. No proprietary patents, unique driver geometries, or documented technical innovations have been identified. The product is entirely passive and analog — predating consumer DSP entirely — with no digital integration of any kind. No competitive technical differentiation exists that would require meaningful time or effort to replicate.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations.

The ATH-5 price basis is approximately 100 USD for a new-in-box secondary-market unit [3]. For that price, the buyer still receives a discontinued 50-year-old passive headphone with no manufacturer warranty, no active parts support, and no reliable ATH-5 measurement baseline.

The CP comparator is the Samson SR850, available new at approximately 49.99 USD [4][5]. It covers the same practical use case: passive wired over-ear listening, no wireless function, no ANC, and no app-dependent electronics. Unlike the ATH-5, it has published third-party measurement results [4], so it is the cheaper equivalent-or-better practical option on the best available evidence.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The ATH-5 is discontinued with no applicable manufacturer warranty. Audio-Technica's current global support infrastructure covers only active products; manufacturer repair parts for a model discontinued approximately 50 years ago are not available, and official repair service is unavailable. The simple passive construction — no active electronics, no firmware dependencies — is inherently resistant to electronic failure. However, age-related degradation of earpads, cable insulation, and headband padding is expected for units of this age, and no systematic failure data or active support resources exist for this model.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Audio-Technica applied existing phono cartridge transducer manufacturing competency to dynamic headphone drivers — a pragmatic capability transfer that avoids non-functional cost inflation and makes no pseudoscientific claims. The product serves a genuine audio function.

Design limitations are significant. The 1977 design was executed by subjective listening rather than measurement-based methodology, predating modern measurement-first approaches entirely. No proprietary advancement, DSP integration, or scientific design validation is documented. The approach represents a conservative implementation of commodity technology with no evidence of objective performance optimization or innovation in design direction.

## Advice

The Audio-Technica ATH-5 holds historical interest as an early product in Audio-Technica's headphone lineup, but cannot be recommended for audio performance purposes. No reliable manufacturer specifications are available, no third-party measurements exist, and any purchased unit is approximately 50 years old with expected component degradation. At secondary market prices of approximately 100 USD [3], the Samson SR850 (approximately 49.99 USD [4][5]) delivers confirmed third-party measured performance, an active manufacturer warranty, and new-condition construction at roughly half the cost. Purchasers should approach the ATH-5 strictly as a vintage collectible.
