## Overview

The Audio-Technica ATH-CKP200 SonicSport is a wired in-ear sport earphone introduced in 2013, offering an adjustable hinged ear hook, IPX5 water resistance for sweat and post-workout washing, and a 3.5mm wired connection. Positioned as an entry-level product in Audio-Technica's SonicSport lineup with an original MSRP of USD 49.95, it has since been discontinued with no active new-unit retail availability. A variant, the ATH-CKP200iS, added an inline microphone and remote control in 2014; all other specifications were unchanged.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

The ATH-CKP200 is an in-house Audio-Technica design, which is a positive factor. All components, however — the 8.5mm moving-coil driver, IPX5-rated enclosure, adjustable hinged ear hook, dual-silicone-eartip system, and asymmetric cable routing — were industry-standard and widely available in competing sport earphones prior to the 2013 launch. No proprietary patents are associated with this product. Evaluated against the 2026 market, every technology is fully commoditized, no competitive differentiation persists, and the product has no digital integration of any kind. No lasting technical advantage was created.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The passive wired design — no battery, no digital electronics — is inherently resistant to the degradation modes affecting active devices, and IPX5 certification provides confirmed water and sweat resistance. Audio-Technica maintains a global manufacturer-direct service network. These positives are offset by the product being discontinued, an estimated 1-year warranty consistent with the budget tier, and active parts and repair support having effectively ended. No statistical failure rate data is available for this model.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The ATH-CKP200's design concentrates cost on genuine sport-utility features: IPX5 waterproofing, an adjustable ear hook for secure fit, and a dual-eartip system (sealed tips for isolation; ridged tips for outdoor ambient awareness) addressing a real safety consideration. No pseudoscientific audio claims are made and no meaningless material investment is evident. The overall approach is practical. However, the design is thoroughly conservative with no novel acoustic engineering, and the only documented product evolution — the ATH-CKP200iS (2014) — added only microphone and remote hardware with zero acoustic performance advancement, representing stagnation in the core acoustic mission throughout the product's life.

## Advice

The ATH-CKP200 is discontinued and unavailable at new retail prices. For buyers seeking an IPX5 sport earphone with an ear-hook design, current alternatives offer independently verified audio performance at well under the original USD 49.95 asking price. The JBL Endurance Run 2 Wired (USD 19.95) provides equivalent sport functionality with an additional inline microphone and has independent Audio Science Review measurements [3] confirming acceptable frequency response and low distortion. For any active purchase in this category, current products with available third-party measurement data are strongly preferred over a 2013 design for which no audio quality verification exists from any source.
