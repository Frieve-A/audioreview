## Overview

VIE Style VIE Fit2 represents the company's entry into the true wireless earphone market, featuring their patented "Soft Shell" technology and 360-degree rotating speaker design [1]. Launched via Kickstarter in 2020, these earphones incorporate 8mm neodymium dynamic drivers with Bluetooth 5.0 connectivity and Qualcomm QCC3020 chipset [2]. The product offers 6 hours of continuous playback with an additional 33 hours via the charging case, IPX5 water resistance, and support for SBC/AAC/AptX codecs [1][2]. VIE, primarily a neurotechnology company, partnered with Yamaha for audio technology development in their previous SHAIR headphone project.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

Multiple documented user reports indicate persistent connection stability issues across various devices including Samsung Galaxy A8 2018, iPhone, and Samsung S8 [4]. Users report "Connection between the earbuds unstable, transmission unstable" and "Connection is unstable on all my BT devices" [4]. The rotating speaker mechanism introduces additional mechanical complexity compared to conventional fixed designs, potentially increasing failure risk [1]. VIE operates as a small Japanese neurotechnology company with limited global support infrastructure compared to major audio manufacturers [1]. User feedback suggests inadequate support response quality [4]. Standard warranty period applies, but the product's complex mechanical design and documented connectivity problems raise concerns about long-term reliability.

VIE approaches audio product development from their neurotechnology background, though this scientific foundation does not translate to demonstrable audio measurement improvements [1]. ## Advice

The VIE Fit2 targets users seeking unique mechanical design features rather than proven audio performance. The patented rotating speaker technology may appeal to early adopters interested in unconventional earphone constructions. However, documented connection stability issues across multiple device types present significant usability concerns for practical daily use. Since the product is currently sold out with no clear availability timeline, evaluation remains primarily academic. Users seeking similar TWS functionality should consider currently available alternatives with established reliability records and measured performance data. The product represents an interesting technological approach but practical limitations and availability constraints limit its viability as a purchase recommendation.
