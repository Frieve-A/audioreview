## Overview

The Topping DX5 II represents a significant evolution in desktop audio, combining dual ESS ES9039Q2M DAC chips with Topping's proprietary X-Hybrid amplification architecture. Released in June 2025, this 299 USD desktop DAC/headphone amplifier delivers 7600mW output power, 10-band parametric equalization, and comprehensive connectivity including Bluetooth 5.1 with LDAC support. The device features a 2.0-inch Aurora UI display and multiple output options including 4.4mm balanced, 4-pin XLR balanced, and 6.35mm single-ended connections [1].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

Third-party measurements from Audio Science Review demonstrate exceptional performance with THD+N measured at <0.00006%, indicating excellent distortion control [2]. The device achieves 133dB SNR and 133dB dynamic range, representing high measured performance. ASR ranked the DX5 II among the "top-20 best ever DACs" for distortion and noise characteristics, with residual noise described as "very low" and IMD performance rated as "exceptional." Jitter measurements show -140dB USB input performance, confirming audibly negligible timing errors [2].

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

The DX5 II demonstrates cutting-edge technology through its proprietary X-Hybrid amplification representing evolution from established NFCA architecture. Topping employs 30+ engineers for in-house R&D, developing licensable technologies with measurable performance benefits [3]. The dual ES9039Q2M DAC implementation utilizes recent chip technology introduced in March 2023, while the X-Hybrid triple-stage design provides efficiency improvements over previous generation. The 10-band parametric EQ with desktop software integration and comprehensive digital connectivity establish substantial technological capability [3].

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The DX5 II receives below-average reliability assessment due to multiple documented issues and limited warranty coverage. Known problems include EQ profile saving failures, volume reset safety concerns defaulting to 0dB after power cycles, 4.4mm connector mechanical issues causing channel imbalance, and inadequate DC protection compared to earlier Topping models [5]. The device carries a standard one-year warranty with repair services available, though third-party support options exist. Regular firmware updates demonstrate ongoing manufacturer support, with version V2.07 released January 27, 2026, addressing several known issues [5].

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

Topping exemplifies exceptional design philosophy rationality through measurement-driven scientific approach and complete elimination of subjective claims. The company utilizes Audio Precision APx555B analyzers for development, targeting scientifically verifiable transparency thresholds while excluding unscientific features [1]. Cost allocation prioritizes performance functions over aesthetics, achieving 33% price reduction from previous DX5 model while maintaining top-tier measured performance. ## Advice

The Topping DX5 II suits users requiring desktop DAC/amplifier functionality with comprehensive connectivity and parametric EQ capability. Its exceptional measured performance and advanced features justify the 299 USD cost for users prioritizing scientific validation over subjective preferences. However, potential buyers should consider the documented reliability issues and one-year warranty limitation. The device excels for desktop setups requiring balanced outputs, Bluetooth connectivity, and digital processing features, though users needing absolute reliability may prefer alternatives with longer warranty coverage or established track records.
