## Overview

The AKG K26P is a discontinued closed-back supraaural headphone designed for portable use, featuring AKG's proprietary 3D-Axis folding mechanism. Originally sold for 50 USD, this 73-gram lightweight headphone was positioned as a budget-friendly option for mobile listening, later replaced by the K414P. The K26P combines 32-ohm impedance with high 125 dB/V sensitivity for direct device connection. AKG, founded in 1947, has established itself through scientific measurement approaches including the Harman Target Curve development, representing decades of acoustics research and engineering expertise in both professional and consumer audio markets.
