## Overview

The Bose Noise Cancelling Headphones 700, released May 29, 2019, represent Bose's flagship ANC headphones featuring proprietary TriPort acoustic technology and an 8-microphone beamforming system. The product incorporates 11 levels of adjustable noise cancellation, Bluetooth 5.0 connectivity with SBC/AAC codec support, and 21-hour battery life. Notable design elements include touch-sensitive controls, voice assistant integration, and a metal headband construction with 90-degree rotating ear cups. The product was discontinued in October 2024, marking the end of this particular model line in favor of newer iterations.

## Scientific Validity

Audio Science Review measurements demonstrate that when DSP processing is active, the headphones achieve "very good compliance with our target" frequency response [1]. Modern ANC headphones commonly utilize digital signal processing for frequency response optimization, making this standard industry practice rather than a deficiency. Distortion performance shows excellent characteristics at normal listening levels, meeting headphone category criteria for transparency. The 17-millisecond group delay when active represents typical DSP processing latency. The 11-level ANC adjustment system provides comprehensive user control. While specific THD and S/N ratio measurements require third-party verification, available measurements indicate performance within acceptable ranges for the headphone category. The combination of effective DSP correction and good distortion characteristics places performance at satisfactory levels when evaluated against appropriate headphone criteria.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

The headphones incorporate Bose's patented TriPort acoustic technology, utilizing external ports to maximize acoustic space within smaller enclosures. The 8-microphone system employs beamforming arrays for speech isolation and noise rejection, demonstrating sophisticated signal processing capabilities. However, Bose maintains proprietary development without disclosing specific DSP computational capabilities, unlike competitors Sony and Apple who provide technical transparency. The technology represents mature ANC implementation with incremental improvements rather than breakthrough innovation. The conservative approach of relying on legacy TriPort technology without significant advancement limits differentiation in an increasingly competitive market. While the integration of digital processing, touch controls, and wireless connectivity demonstrates appropriate contemporary technology adoption, the overall technological advancement remains limited compared to cutting-edge implementations from competitors.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

Bose provides only 1-year warranty coverage, below the industry standard 2-year period. The company maintains a policy of not providing repair services for headphones, particularly for out-of-warranty units, instead requiring complete product replacement [3]. Returns must be accompanied by RMA numbers that Bose provides, and out-of-warranty replacements typically cost between 40-100 USD when available. User reports indicate firmware update issues requiring manual troubleshooting steps including USB connections for manual updates [2]. As an end-of-life product discontinued in October 2024, current inventory represents remaining stock with diminishing long-term support prospects. The combination of short warranty periods, no repair options, product discontinuation status, and limited parts availability for a product reaching end-of-life significantly constrains reliability support compared to current-generation competitors with active product lines and comprehensive service networks.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The design philosophy adopts a modern and rational approach of actively complementing physical limitations with digital signal processing (DSP). Utilizing DSP to achieve ideal frequency response within a compact and lightweight enclosure is more efficient than relying solely on physical driver design, representing a rational design decision aligned with this policy. The integration of touch controls and ANC also represents logical choices that enhance the user experience. While concerns regarding support due to discontinuation and the high pricing relative to measurable performance remain challenges from a cost-effectiveness perspective, the engineering direction itself is correct and based on scientific grounds.
