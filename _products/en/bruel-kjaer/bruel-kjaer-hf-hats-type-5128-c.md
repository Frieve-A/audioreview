## Overview

The Brüel & Kjær High-frequency Head and Torso Simulator (HF HATS) Type 5128-C represents a significant advancement in professional acoustic measurement technology. Developed by HBK (formed in 2019 through the merger of HBM and Brüel & Kjær), this instrument provides realistic reproduction of acoustic properties of an average adult human head and torso across the full audible frequency range up to 20 kHz. The Type 5128-C incorporates revolutionary MRI-based ear canal design derived from data of 40 individuals, extending measurement capabilities far beyond the 8 kHz limitation of previous HATS models. Designed for comprehensive electroacoustic testing, it serves critical roles in smartphone audio performance evaluation, headset communication quality assessment, and testing of speakers, microphones, headphones, and hearing aids.

The Type 5128-C demonstrates exceptional scientific validity through comprehensive international standards compliance and advanced measurement capabilities. The instrument covers the complete audible frequency range from 20 Hz to 20 kHz, significantly extending beyond the 8 kHz limitation of previous generation models. Compliance with ITU-T Rec. ## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

The Type 5128-C incorporates world-class technology innovations that establish new industry standards for acoustic measurement precision. The revolutionary MRI-based design methodology utilized scanner technology to collect ear geometries from a large population, with sophisticated post-processing to determine average human ear canal geometry [3]. Custom proprietary innovations include a pre-polarized ¼-inch microphone with new low-noise preamplifier, anatomically correct ear canal with angled eardrum simulator, and quick-release click-on system for ear simulator mounting. Advanced technology integration features TEDS electronic calibration system and precise transition mimicking human ear's soft-to-bony canal structure. The unprecedented precision manufacturing processes enable reliable 20 kHz measurement capability, representing a substantial technological leap from the 8 kHz limitation of the Type 4128 predecessor. Additional innovations include larger silicone rubber areas around pinnae for better circumaural headphone accommodation, custom-designed loudspeaker with optimized geometry and stronger magnet system, and robust ear structure with improved wear resistance. The comprehensive integration of cutting-edge MRI technology, precision manufacturing, and electronic calibration systems demonstrates exceptional technology level worthy of industry leadership.
