## Overview

The Talomen W50x is a wired closed-back over-ear headphone released in December 2024, priced at USD 39.99. Talomen, a Hong Kong-based brand founded by music producer Kent Leung and claiming distribution in 36 countries, positions the W50x as a studio monitor headphone for recording, mixing, podcasting, and DJing. It features 50mm neodymium drivers, a detachable 3-meter cable, dual 3.5mm/6.35mm connectivity, 90-degree swiveling earcups, and a foldable form factor [1][2].

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.0} $$

Every component of the W50x relies on thoroughly mature, commodity-level technology. The 50mm neodymium dynamic driver and passive closed-back enclosure are standard approaches common in the budget headphone market for decades, with no proprietary driver design or acoustic engineering documented. The dual-connector bundle and detachable cable are similarly standard practice requiring no specialized expertise. The sole differentiating claim — a "patented" anti-drop twist-lock cable connector — has no verifiable patent documentation and provides zero audio performance benefit, functioning only as a mechanical cable retention feature built on a concept already established in professional audio connectors. The product profile is strongly consistent with OEM/ODM sourcing for budget lineup expansion: a brand with no disclosed manufacturing facility or engineering team, a generic specification sheet, and marketing-heavy positioning without technical substance. The design is entirely passive and analog with no DSP, software, or AI integration. No competitor would face meaningful lead time to produce an equivalent product, and no accumulation of technical expertise is identifiable from any public source.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

The W50x is a fully passive wired headphone with no electronics, eliminating firmware-related failure modes entirely. The detachable cable design removes the most common mechanical failure point in wired headphones, representing a structural advantage. No formal warranty period is documented anywhere on Talomen's official pages; only a 30-day return window for defective products is stated, with no multi-year coverage [3]. Manufacturer-direct support is available across multiple global markets including the US, UK, Canada, and Japan. Talomen is a very new market entrant (launched December 2024) with no established long-term reliability track record; no product failures, recalls, or service bulletins have been reported. Repair capability is limited to cable replacement via the official store; no authorized service centers are documented.
