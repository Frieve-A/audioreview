## Overview

Rasteme Systems Co., Ltd. was a Japanese digital audio company founded in 1986, specializing in USB DAC and headphone amplifier products. Based in Niigata Prefecture with a Tokyo sales office, the company operated under the motto "High technology and quality to be reasonable." Their notable products included the UDAC32 (¥99,750, 2012) and UDAC32R (¥32,000, 2012) USB DAC/headphone amplifiers featuring Burr-Brown components and TCXO clock technology. The company also provided custom-made and OEM services alongside their original digital audio products. Rasteme Systems ceased operations around 2014, with all products discontinued and support infrastructure eliminated.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Rasteme Systems employed appropriate technology for the 2012 era, including XMOS USB controllers, Burr-Brown PCM1795 DAC chips, TPA6120 headphone amplifiers, and PGA2320 electronic volume controllers. The company developed proprietary SMV (Smoothing Volume) technology for fine volume adjustment and utilized high-accuracy TCXO oscillators. However, evaluated against current 2025 technology standards, this represents 13-year-old implementations that have been surpassed by modern digital audio solutions. The technical approach lacked significant innovation compared to contemporary competitors, relying primarily on established component solutions rather than developing breakthrough technologies that would influence industry direction.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Rasteme products featured quality construction using high-grade Burr-Brown components and TCXO oscillators with simple electronic designs having few moving parts, suggesting inherent reliability. Customer feedback indicated responsive and courteous support when the company was operational. However, the company provided only 12-month warranties, below the industry standard 24 months. Most critically, Rasteme Systems is now completely defunct, eliminating all manufacturer support, warranty coverage, parts availability, and firmware updates. Current owners of Rasteme products have no recourse for technical support or repair services, representing a complete failure of long-term reliability infrastructure.
