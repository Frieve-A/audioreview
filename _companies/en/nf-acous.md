## Overview

NF ACOUS (formerly NF Audio) is a Shenzhen-based audio brand operated by Shenzhen Ningfan Acoustics Co., Ltd. under founder-CEO Felix Yang. Founded in 2014 as an IEM specialist for stage performances, the company completed proprietary BA driver R&D in 2016, introduced the NE4 Evolution with a swappable electronic crossover faceplate in 2021, and formally rebranded from NF Audio to NF ACOUS in December 2024. The current lineup spans the Professional Series (NM25, NM20, NH20 closed-back monitor headphone), Music Series (NA20, RA15, NA2 METAL, NA2+, NA3), Game Series (RG15, NG3, RG05 collaborations), and Custom IEM line (NF3–NF8 Pro, ND12 from 398.99 USD). The flagship NM2 reportedly exceeded 100,000 units sold globally. [1]

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

Frequency response data from multiple independent measurement databases covering the current NF ACOUS IEM lineup reveal a consistent pattern of treble elevation. For the NA20, measurements using a 711 Clone coupler document elevated bass, a scooped midrange, and a pronounced treble peak around 5 kHz; distortion plots at typical listening levels show low distortion as a percentage [2]. Cross-source FR data from independent measurement databases yields the following elevations at 5 kHz relative to the 1 kHz reference: NM25 approximately +9.5 dB, RA15 approximately +11.4–11.5 dB (both nozzle variants), NA20 approximately +12.5 dB, and NM20 approximately +16.2 dB [3]. These deviations indicate substantial FR coloration across the IEM lineup and are not consistent with flat or neutral monitoring reproduction. Manufacturer THD specifications are stated as "< 1%" for all current products; these represent rated-output limits, and the NA20 distortion plots at typical listening levels indicate actual in-band distortion is substantially lower [1][2]. Passive isolation is specified at 25 dB (manufacturer) across the IEM lineup [1]. The NH20 closed-back monitor headphone has no independent third-party measurements; manufacturer specifications document FR 5–50 kHz and THD < 1% [1]. The consistent 5 kHz FR elevation across the IEM lineup is the primary measured performance concern, while distortion behavior at typical levels appears acceptable based on available data.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

NF ACOUS holds a confirmed Chinese patent for the Clutter Trap acoustic resonance cavity (ZL 201922255581.4), applied to the NM20 and independently cited in company press materials [1]. The company has maintained in-house design integration since founding, covering R&D, acoustic design, and precision manufacturing under one entity. Proprietary BA driver R&D was completed in 2016, enabling independent custom IEM fabrication capability. The NE4 Evolution (2021) introduced a swappable electronic crossover faceplate system: three physically interchangeable faceplates each contain distinct crossover circuit configurations that alter driver roles among the Knowles BA array, producing three independently documented FR signatures. This is a genuinely novel hardware implementation — standard IEM tuning uses swappable nozzle filters, not crossover PCB faceplates — and no competitor adoption was found after four-plus years. SRA (Selective Reflection Adjustment) on the NA20 is claimed as patented, though independent patent registry confirmation was not found. Current flagship products (NM20, NM25, NA20) use beryllium-plated diaphragm dynamic drivers, which has become commercially widespread in the mid-range IEM segment. No AI, cloud, or advanced DSP integration is present in the primary product line. [1]

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.6} $$

Standard warranty coverage is 1 year for IEM units (documented at authorized resellers Linsoul and HiFiGo), below the 2-year average. The passive IEM lineup is inherently simple in construction — no active electronics, few moving parts, user-replaceable 0.78mm 2-pin detachable cables throughout, and replaceable earpads on the NH20 — qualifying as a robust and maintainable design. NF ACOUS has operated continuously since 2014 with no documented product recalls or widespread systematic defect campaigns, and the NM2 reportedly exceeded 100,000 units sold globally, representing a meaningful commercial-scale reliability track record. Support is provided via direct manufacturer email and an authorized international dealer network covering multiple global distributors; this is primarily dealer-based support rather than a structured direct manufacturer service system. No RMA rate or MTBF data is publicly available. [1]

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

NF ACOUS pursues a mixed design philosophy spanning professional monitoring (NM20, NM25 marketed for stage and studio), musical enjoyment (NA20 product copy emphasizing genre adaptability and emotional expression), and gaming (collaboration series). No published measurement target, ABX methodology, or frequency response reference was found in any official communication. Third-party FR data confirms that the NM20 exhibits approximately 16 dB of treble elevation at 5 kHz, which is inconsistent with the flat monitoring response implied by the professional marketing. The product line shows a clear generational progression toward expanded capability: the NM2-to-NM20 transition added a beryllium-plated MC2L-100P driver with dual magnetic circuit and Clutter Trap acoustic structure; the NM25 represents a further tier upgrade; the NH20 is the brand's first full-size monitor headphone; an FCC filing for a TWS product signals ongoing development. The NE4 Evolution (2021) swappable electronic crossover faceplate is a structurally innovative approach — using hardware crossover PCBs rather than near-universal nozzle filters — with three independently documented FR signatures. Cost allocation is partially performance-directed (driver material choices, acoustic patent development) and partially materials-oriented (aviation-grade CNC aluminum and 280-core Litz cable descriptions in Music Series marketing). The lineup contains no products with implausible audibility claims, no vacuum tube equipment, and no R2R DAC products; all offerings function as dedicated wired IEMs and monitor headphones for professional and critical listening scenarios. [1]

## Advice

NF ACOUS produces technically engineered IEMs with confirmed acoustic patents and a documented engineering history, but all current IEMs in the lineup show consistent treble elevation — approximately 9.5 to 16.2 dB above the 1 kHz reference at 5 kHz across multiple independent FR databases — representing substantial deviation from neutral reference targets. Buyers requiring accurate flat monitoring reproduction will find better FR conformity in alternatives such as the Moondrop Chu II (22.99 USD), which also offers a better-specified THD figure. The NM25 (199.00 USD) and NM20 (118.99 USD) are priced at approximately 8.7x and 5.2x the Moondrop Chu II respectively without offering better measured performance. The NH20 (199.00 USD) faces the Sennheiser HD 280 Pro (85.00 USD), which offers better-documented THD (0.1% vs < 1%) and isolation (32 dB vs not documented) at less than half the price. Those specifically seeking the NE4 Evolution's unique swappable crossover faceplate architecture, or professional custom IEM fabrication (NF3–NF8 Pro from 398.99 USD), will find genuine technical differentiation with no direct equivalent at comparable price points; these represent the segments where NF ACOUS offers distinct value.
