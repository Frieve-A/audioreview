## Overview

ivipQ is a Chinese ("ChiFi") audio brand whose core business is high-end Hi-Fi headphone/IEM upgrade cables, organized into A (mid-range), P (professional), and S (flagship) series, with a secondary line of passive dynamic-driver in-ear monitors (ivipQ-100, IQ-14 "Shanhai") [1]. The company self-describes as a "headset cord factory" and reaches buyers through its official store, AliExpress, and Amazon. Marketing emphasizes high-purity conductor materials (single-crystal silver, OCC copper, graphene/palladium plating) and tier branding. All products are passive, with no founding date, financials, patents, or technical papers publicly documented [1][2].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

ivipQ's identity centers on high-priced passive upgrade cables (flagship S-series approximately 310–405 USD) marketed for audible improvements — enhanced soundstage, clarity, and detail — attributed to exotic conductor materials. For a passive cable operating within normal resistance and capacitance bounds, such audible benefits have no ABX or blind-test support, and none is published. The only audibility-relevant electrical parameter disclosed is a DC resistance of 0.34 Ω for a single model, with no capacitance figure and no independent verification [1]. Because this dominant line commands premium pricing while claiming audibility for elements that do not change measurable sound, it sits at the floor. The secondary IEM line carries only a manufacturer-stated frequency-response range of 20–20,000 Hz with no deviation tolerance and no THD figure [2], leaving it largely uncharacterized; no independent third-party measurements exist for any ivipQ product. With the cable business dominating the company and offering no measurable audible benefit, overall scientific validity is very low.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

ivipQ's products are passive upgrade cables and passive dynamic-driver IEMs built from commodity ChiFi materials — single-crystal silver, OCC copper, graphene/palladium plating — joined by conventional Litz braiding [1]. No patents, technical papers, named proprietary technologies, or industry-first implementations were found, and the company describes itself as a cable-assembly "factory" [2]. These materials and braiding methods are mature and now common, widely replicated by competing budget brands, so new entrants can trivially match them and no competitor would seek to license anything ivipQ does. Every product is passive, with no digital, DSP, software, cloud, or AI integration. From a neutral baseline, deductions apply for mature/now-common technology, undesirable (non-licensable) technology, trivially easy catch-up with no lasting differentiation, and analog/mechanical-only construction, with no offsetting evidence of patents or accumulated know-how. This is assessed independently of any sound-quality result.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.3} $$

This site evaluates based solely on functionality and measured performance values, without considering driver types or configurations. Company cost-performance is the weighted average of four representatives spanning both product lines. All comparisons are provisional because the review target has no third-party measurements; results may be revised when independent data appears.

**ivipQ A02 (mid-range cable) — 57 USD** vs **NICEHCK C8-1 8-core cable — 17 USD** [4]. Both provide identical user-facing functions: detachable connector interface (MMCX / 0.78mm 2-pin / QDC), interchangeable 2.5 / 3.5 / 4.4mm terminations including balanced, and multi-core conductor. On the only audio-relevant cable metric, DC resistance is approximately 0.34 Ω (manufacturer) for the ivipQ line versus the comparable low-resistance 8-core class of the C8-1; capacitance is unpublished on both sides, so the two are equivalent within manufacturer-spec scope. CP = 17 USD ÷ 57 USD = 0.296.

**ivipQ S01 (flagship cable) — 405 USD** vs **NICEHCK C8-1 8-core cable — 17 USD** [4]. Functions are identical (same detachable interfaces, terminations, and multi-core build), and DC resistance is again in the same low-resistance class (~0.34 Ω manufacturer vs comparable 8-core), with capacitance unpublished on both sides. CP = 17 USD ÷ 405 USD = 0.042.

**ivipQ IQ-14 "Shanhai" (IEM) — 66 USD** vs **Truthear Gate — 17 USD, plus a 10 USD 4.4mm balanced 2-pin cable to normalize termination = 27 USD** [3]. Both deliver passive wired playback, detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cabling, and 4.4mm balanced output. Frequency response: the IQ-14 states a 20–20,000 Hz range (manufacturer, no deviation figure), while the Gate measures 20–20,000 Hz with near-target frequency response from independent testing [3]; THD is unpublished for the IQ-14 versus measured low distortion for the Gate [3] — equivalent-or-better in the comparator's favor. CP = 27 USD ÷ 66 USD = 0.409.

**ivipQ-100 (IEM) — 59 USD** vs **Truthear Gate — 17 USD** [3]. Both provide passive wired playback, detachable 0.78mm 2-pin cabling, and single-ended termination. Frequency response: the ivipQ-100 states a 20–20,000 Hz range (manufacturer, no deviation figure) versus the Gate's measured 20–20,000 Hz with near-target frequency response [3]; THD is unpublished for the ivipQ-100 versus measured low distortion for the Gate [3]. CP = 17 USD ÷ 59 USD = 0.288.

Weights reflect each representative's importance across both lines: the mid cable carries the broadest sales relevance (0.30), the flagship cable and the flagship triple-driver IEM each anchor their line (0.25 each), and the single-driver IEM rounds out the lineup (0.20).

Weighted CP = (0.296 × 0.30) + (0.042 × 0.25) + (0.409 × 0.25) + (0.288 × 0.20) = 0.0887 + 0.0105 + 0.1023 + 0.0576 = 0.259 → 0.3

Functionally equivalent alternatives exist at a fraction of ivipQ's prices across both lines, so company cost-performance is low.

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.4} $$

Manufacturer-stated warranties are short: one year for IEMs and three months for cables, with accessories uncovered [5]. This places the line in the one-year-or-less band, drawing a downward adjustment. Support is manufacturer-direct, with daily service hours and a global shipping and RMA process plus an online after-sales center, but there is no localized regional service infrastructure outside China and cross-border RMA shipping is customer-paid, so availability is adequate rather than exceptional and earns no bonus. Support is manufacturer-provided rather than third-party-only, so no penalty applies there. No statistical failure-rate, RMA, or MTBF data exists, and no product-specific defects or recalls are documented, so reliability is treated as unknown with no adjustment. Parts-supply duration and out-of-warranty repair costs are undocumented. Firmware is not applicable to these passive products. Construction is electronically simple, but cables are an inherently wear-prone item with no ivipQ-specific evidence either way, so construction is treated as neutral.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

ivipQ's design philosophy is materials- and craft-oriented rather than measurement-based: differentiation rests on exotic conductor purity, plating, and core counts, accompanied by claims of audibly enhanced soundstage, clarity, detail, and high-resolution sound that have no measured support and run contrary to the established engineering position that an in-spec passive cable is audibly transparent [1]. The company publishes no frequency-response, THD, or capacitance data and no controlled-listening evidence. Premium flagship pricing (up to roughly 405 USD per cable) is allocated to exotic materials and braiding labor that do not change audibility-relevant electrical parameters beyond what a competent low-resistance cable already achieves, and the exotic platings, 8-core builds, and triple-driver IEMs represent quantity and materials investment without demonstrated audible benefit. Development is lateral — lineup expansion with no measurable generational improvement — and the overall approach is conservative within a low-barrier category. Multiple negative factors accumulate (subjective rather than scientific approach, costs unrelated to measurable function, audibility claims for inaudible elements, no measurable model progression, meaningless quantity investment, and a conservative attitude). Because the products still provide functional audio value, the score rests at the floor rather than zero. This is evaluated independently of any measured-performance result and of data-disclosure transparency.

## Advice

ivipQ's value proposition rests on exotic-conductor cables sold for audible sound improvements that are not supported by any measurement or blind-test evidence; for a passive cable within normal electrical bounds, an ordinary low-resistance cable performs the same audible job for far less. If you simply need a specific connector or termination, an inexpensive well-made cable such as the NICEHCK C8-1 (about 17 USD) covers the same functions [4]. For the IEM line, functionally equivalent and independently measured alternatives — the Truthear Gate (about 17 USD) — deliver near-target frequency response and low distortion at roughly a quarter to a third of the price [3]. Buyers seeking measurable performance per dollar are better served elsewhere; consider ivipQ only if a particular customization or connector option is otherwise unavailable.
