## Overview

GK AudioLab (brand name: GK / Groove Keeper) is a Chinese wired IEM manufacturer that became independent from KZ (Knowledge Zenith) in late 2025. The company offers passive wired IEMs in the 19–50 USD range, including the flagship Kunten (50 USD), the entry-level Streak (19.9 USD), and several mid-range models (G1 PRO, G2, G3, G5, X1). GK AudioLab positions its products around Harman frequency response targeting, publishing specific numerical specifications on its product pages. [1]

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

No independent third-party measurements are available for any GK AudioLab product as of the review date. Evaluation relies entirely on manufacturer-published specifications.

**GK Kunten (50 USD):** The manufacturer specifies a frequency response deviation of ±1.8dB from the Harman target and THD below 0.3% at 94dB SPL [1]. The claimed Harman deviation indicates good frequency response accuracy for an IEM if verified. The claimed THD represents acceptable distortion performance for this product category. Both figures are entirely unverified by any independent measurement source, and no measurement graphs are published.

**GK Streak (19.9 USD):** No audio quality specifications — no frequency response deviation from any target curve, no distortion figures — are published by the manufacturer [2]. The product is described only as "Harman tuned" without supporting numeric data, and defaults to a neutral score.

Both representative products score 0.5 individually, yielding a company-level Scientific Validity of 0.5.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.2} $$

GK AudioLab is a newly independent brand operating in the established budget passive IEM segment. No proprietary patents or novel driver technologies have been documented for any product. The Kunten employs a standard 10mm dynamic driver marketed with the label "super-linear," a term carrying no documented technical specification or independent substantiation beyond marketing copy [1]. The Streak pairs a 10mm dynamic driver with a micro-planar tweeter — a hybrid configuration already widely available across the budget Chinese IEM market and not unique to GK AudioLab [2].

All products are passive and analog-only, with no DSP, software integration, AI, or cloud functionality. No technology present in either product would be sought for adoption by competitors, as functionally equivalent implementations are available from multiple manufacturers at similar or lower cost. GK AudioLab has not demonstrated proprietary engineering capability as an independent entity.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

GK AudioLab's stated design approach centers on Harman frequency response targeting, a measurement-derived benchmark, indicating a scientifically grounded tuning direction in principle. The company publishes specific numerical performance claims for the Kunten — ±1.8dB Harman deviation and THD below 0.3% at 94dBSPL [1] — suggesting a metrics-aware design orientation.

However, these claims have not been independently verified, and no measurement graphs are published. A measurement-first culture would typically support publicly accessible FR plots and third-party measurement accessibility. No strong negative factors are identified: no vacuum tube or R2R philosophies, no occult audio claims, no claims of audible effects for non-audible elements, and IEMs have clear justification as dedicated audio equipment. Budget pricing in the 19–50 USD range appropriately directs costs to the physical product. The absence of confirmed independent verification and limited public documentation of design philosophy prevent positive adjustment. A neutral score reflects the balance of unconfirmed positive signals against the absence of established measurement-first practice.

## Advice

The GK Streak (19.9 USD) is the lowest-priced product among identified options with confirmed Harman-targeted performance — no cheaper IEM with equivalent measured performance was found. For buyers seeking an entry-level wired IEM with a Harman-targeting specification, the Streak presents a practical option pending independent measurement confirmation.

The GK Kunten (50 USD) faces direct competition. The Truthear GATE is available at 21.99 USD with independently verified performance from Audio Science Review [4] demonstrating distortion described as "basically distortionless" and close Harman frequency response adherence. GK AudioLab's specifications for the Kunten remain unverified, and no third-party data has substantiated its 50 USD price premium relative to confirmed alternatives. Buyers who prioritize independently confirmed measured performance should evaluate the GATE or other products with published third-party measurement data before considering the Kunten.
