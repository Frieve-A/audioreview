## Overview

OpenAudio Technology is a specialized manufacturer focusing on multi-room streaming amplifiers and immersive audio technology. The company has established a significant presence in the cinema industry, serving major chains including AMC, CGV, KCS, Cinemark, Cineplex, and Vue Cinemas. With 121 patents across digital cinema standards (54), immersive audio (34), and multi-room streaming amplifiers (33), OpenAudio Technology has positioned itself as a technology leader. Their proprietary HOLOSOUND immersive audio technology is positioned alongside industry standards like Dolby ATMOS and DTS:X. The company's primary consumer product line includes the HOLO-WHAS series multi-room streaming amplifiers, offering 8-zone capability with unique features such as multi-instance AirPlay 2 support where some models can be expanded beyond typical concurrent limits.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

OpenAudio Technology demonstrates strong technological achievement through extensive patent portfolio and proprietary innovations. The company holds 121 patents across three key areas: 54 for digital cinema standards, 34 for immersive audio standards (HOLOSOUND), and 33 for multi-room streaming amplifiers. HOLOSOUND is positioned alongside Dolby ATMOS and DTS:X as an immersive audio technology, and OpenAudio states ownership of this technology rather than explicitly describing it as fully in-house development. Multi-instance AirPlay 2 support is a notable integration feature, with typical concurrent limits on base models and expanded instance counts on higher-tier models. Advanced features include 31-band EQ with high-order filters, flexible routing configurations (1-to-N, M-to-1, M-to-N), full WebAPI implementation, and comprehensive smart home integration with Control4 and Home Assistant. Industry recognition through major cinema chain adoption validates the technology's sophistication and competitive advantage. While the patent portfolio and cinema industry presence are impressive, established players like Russound, Control4, and Crestron maintain broader ecosystem integration and longer track records in the residential market.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{1.0} $$

OpenAudio Technology's HOLO-WHAS at 1,196.10 USD provides strong value in the 8-zone multi-room amplifier market. The system delivers 960W total output (60WÃ—16 channels, 8 zones) with wireless input support for Qobuz Connect, AirPlay 2, Spotify Connect, DLNA, and Bluetooth, alongside USB Audio Capture as the primary wired input. HDMI ARC, optical/SPDIF, and RCA line inputs are reserved for higher-tier HOLO-WHAS Ultra and Plus models rather than the base HOLO-WHAS. AirPlay 2 concurrency is typically up to four streams on the base unit, with expansion to eight depending on configuration, while higher-tier models can initialize eight instances. General-purpose equipment alternatives were evaluated including PC-based streaming systems with zone amplifiers, smartphone + external DAC combinations with multi-channel amplifiers, and individual streaming devices per zone, but these solutions require significantly more complex setup and lack integrated control systems. The closest dedicated competitor is the Dayton Audio DAX88 [3] at 1,399 USD, which provides 8-zone capability (6 amplified + 2 preamp zones) with 75W per channel and Wi-Fi streaming support, but offers only 6 fully amplified zones versus 8. Premium solutions include Russound SMZ8 [4] at 3,494.99 USD providing 6 amplified zones with 50W per channel, and NAD CI 980 [5] at 1,299 USD MSRP offering basic 8-channel amplification without streaming capabilities. The HOLO-WHAS offers strong functionality and amplification for its price, establishing strong cost-performance.
