## Overview

Luxury & Precision (L&P), established in 2014 by Mr. Wan (formerly of Colorfly), specializes in premium portable audio: USB DAC/AMP dongles (W series), discrete R2R digital audio players (P6/P6 Pro, LP6, LP7), a modular DAP (E7), and a Korg Nutube-based portable amplifier (EA4). The company developed a proprietary non-Android LP OS for its DAP line and introduced its first in-house DAC chip, the LP5108, in 2023. As of June 2026, the official manufacturer website at luxuryprecision.net provides product pages, firmware downloads, and contact information [6].

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

Four representative product segments are evaluated across the portfolio.

**W2 (299 USD):** Third-party measurements using an APx555 B-Series analyzer (GoldenSound) confirm SNR 128 dB and dynamic range 127 dB (AES17) on the balanced output [2], indicating excellent measured performance across both metrics.

**W4 (449 USD):** No credible independent lab measurements are available as of this review. Manufacturer specifications claim SNR 131/134 dB (SE/BAL) and THD+N 0.0003%/0.00013% at 32Ω, which would represent excellent performance if confirmed. Without independent verification, these claims cannot be treated as confirmed performance [1].

**P6 Pro (3,899 USD):** No independent measurements of the P6 Pro have been published. Third-party testing (APx555 B-Series) of the predecessor P6 records SINAD approximately 90 dB and dynamic range approximately 120 dB [3]. The P6 Pro manufacturer claims SNR ≥125 dB (BAL), DR >121 dB (BAL), THD+N <0.003%, and frequency response ±0.8 dB (20Hz–20kHz), all unverified. These predecessor measurements serve as proxy evidence for the R2R DAP line.

**WP2 cable (303–379 USD):** Claims audible quality improvement over standard USB-C cables with no published resistance or capacitance data and no blind-test evidence. No scientifically expected audible benefit is present.

Across the portfolio, confirmed excellent dongle measurements, unverified flagship claims, predecessor R2R data, and the WP2's lack of measurable benefit together support a mid-to-high overall rating.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.7} $$

L&P demonstrates substantive in-house product design capability — no OEM/ODM relationships for third-party brands have been identified, with all products designed exclusively for the L&P brand. Genuine accumulated know-how is evident in discrete R2R assembly with 1/10,000-tolerance precision resistor matching, Intel FPGA programming for audio signal chains, and maintenance of the LP OS firmware across multiple product generations.

No patents have been identified. The LP5108 chip (2023) is presented as L&P's first proprietary silicon; at least one independent reviewer characterizes it as a custom delta-sigma implementation, and no datasheet or technical whitepaper has been published, preventing confident assessment of its novelty. The E7's interchangeable DAC board system (2022) represents a platform approach without a direct industry equivalent. The discrete R2R architecture that distinguished the P6 at its 2019 launch has since been independently replicated by multiple competitors and no longer constitutes a technical differentiator as of 2026. The LP7's progression from 300+ to 500+ precision resistors represents incremental iteration on an established design path.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

Company cost-performance is evaluated across three representative products, weighted by product line significance: W2 (0.25), W4 (0.35, flagship dongle), and P6 Pro (0.40, flagship DAP).

**W2 (299 USD)**

The TRN Black Pearl [4] (36.99 USD) provides USB-C input, 3.5mm SE and 4.4mm BAL headphone outputs, PCM 384kHz/32-bit, native DSD256, and 10-band parametric EQ — equivalent-or-better user-facing functions versus the W2.

Performance comparison (W2 vs TRN Black Pearl):
- SNR: W2 128 dB BAL (GoldenSound APx555) vs TRN Black Pearl 130 dB BAL (ASR) — equivalent-or-better
- Dynamic Range: W2 127 dB AES17 BAL (GoldenSound) vs TRN Black Pearl approximately 120–123 dB without DRE / 132.7 dB with DRE (ASR) — equivalent
- Frequency Response: W2 within −0.1 to +0.0 dB (SE, 40Hz–15kHz) vs TRN Black Pearl flat (ASR confirmed) — equivalent

CP = 36.99 USD ÷ 299 USD = 0.1237

**W4 (449 USD)** [Provisional — no independent lab measurements available for the W4]

The TRN Black Pearl plus a 15 USD USB-to-SPDIF adapter (total 51.99 USD) closes the W4's SPDIF output function gap; both provide SE/BAL headphone outputs, PCM 384kHz/32-bit, DSD256, and EQ functionality. The W4's OLED display is a convenience interface feature not affecting audio output.

Performance comparison (W4 manufacturer claims vs TRN Black Pearl ASR measurements):
- SNR: W4 134 dB BAL (manufacturer claim, unverified) vs TRN Black Pearl 130 dB BAL (ASR) — provisionally equivalent (within ±5%)
- THD+N: W4 0.00013% at 32Ω BAL (manufacturer claim, unverified) vs TRN Black Pearl 0.000316% at 2Vrms BAL (ASR) — provisionally equivalent

CP = 51.99 USD ÷ 449 USD = 0.1158

**P6 Pro (3,899 USD)** [Provisional — no independent measurements for P6 Pro; manufacturer specs compared]

The Shanling M3 Plus [5] (469 USD) provides standalone portable playback on Android 13, 4.4mm BAL and 3.5mm SE outputs, Bluetooth 5.0 with LDAC/aptX HD, 64 GB internal storage plus microSD expansion, PCM 768kHz/32-bit, DSD512, and a 4.7-inch 1,280×720 display — matching or exceeding all user-facing functions of the P6 Pro. Android 13 provides full streaming service access versus the LP OS's limited streaming compatibility.

Performance comparison (manufacturer claims, both products):
- SNR: P6 Pro ≥125 dB BAL vs Shanling M3 Plus 130 dB BAL — better
- Dynamic Range: P6 Pro >121 dB BAL vs Shanling M3 Plus 130 dB BAL — better
- THD+N: P6 Pro <0.003% vs Shanling M3 Plus 0.0007% — better
- Frequency Response: P6 Pro ±0.8 dB (20Hz–20kHz) vs Shanling M3 Plus −0.2 dB (20Hz–40kHz) — better

CP = 469 USD ÷ 3,899 USD = 0.1203

**Weighted Average:**

Weighted CP = (36.99/299 × 0.25) + (51.99/449 × 0.35) + (469/3899 × 0.40) = (0.1237 × 0.25) + (0.1158 × 0.35) + (0.1203 × 0.40) = 0.0309 + 0.0405 + 0.0481 = 0.1196 → 0.1

## Reliability & Support

$$ \Large \text{0.5} $$

The standard warranty is 1 year per authorized retailer documentation — below the 2-year industry average. International retail support routes primarily through authorized dealers (MusicTeck, HiFiGo, Hifonix, Jaben), while the official website provides firmware downloads and manufacturer contact channels [6].

Active firmware support partially offsets this: the E7 received updates from V1.0.4 (May 2024) through V1.1.6 (2025) with separate firmware streams for each interchangeable DAC board; the W4, W2 Ultra, LP6, and P6 series all have recent documented releases maintained at MusicTeck [1].

Documented quality concerns include W4 firmware brick risk during updates (recoverable via rescue driver) and USB cable compatibility issues due to high startup current demands; E7 initial firmware file-recognition failures (resolved in V1.0.4); EA4 initial production tube shock absorption issues subsequently revised in later runs. Battery replacement in the EA4 requires manufacturer RMA due to precision cell-matching requirements. No RMA rate or MTBF data are publicly available.

## Rationality of Design Philosophy

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

L&P's brand identity and flagship pricing are defined by the discrete R2R DAP lineup and the Nutube-based EA4 amplifier. Five substantive concerns define the evaluation.

**Scientific approach:** The LP6, LP7, P6/P6 Pro, and EA4, priced from 2,899 USD to approximately 6,000 USD, use discrete R2R DAC or Korg Nutube architectures marketed with "natural," "organic," and "analog-like" sound claims unsupported by ABX or blind-test evidence. The predecessor P6's third-party measured SINAD of approximately 90 dB contrasts with delta-sigma products achieving 110–120 dB SINAD at substantially lower cost; solid-state alternatives have delivered objectively superior measured distortion performance throughout the period these products have been sold.

**Non-functional cost allocation:** The LP7's approximately 6,000 USD price is built around 500+ hand-matched precision resistors, brass/leather enclosures, and artisan assembly. The P6 Pro (3,899 USD) and EA4 (approximately 3,375 USD) similarly direct their premiums toward materials and analog complexity rather than measured output performance. The WP2 USB-C cable (303–379 USD) carries a premium with no published supporting performance data.

**Unsubstantiated audibility claims:** R2R "natural sound" is marketed without ABX evidence; the P6 Pro is described as "equivalent to 16 PCM1704K chips" while the predecessor P6 measures approximately 90 dB SINAD against the PCM1704K's rated approximately 112 dB; the WP2 claims digital audio quality improvement over standard cables with no resistance, capacitance, or blind-test data.

**Lack of dedicated equipment justification:** LP OS restricts streaming service access across the P6/LP6/LP7 DAP line. A current Android smartphone combined with L&P's own W4 dongle (449 USD) provides equal or better streaming functionality and audio output at substantially lower total cost than any of these standalone DAPs. The provisional comparison confirms the Shanling M3 Plus at 469 USD exceeds P6 Pro manufacturer-stated specifications in every measured metric while running full Android 13.

**Meaningless quantity investment:** The LP7's progression from 300+ to 500+ precision resistors does not correspond to proportional measured performance improvement relative to delta-sigma alternatives; the PCM1704K component-count framing does not reliably predict audio performance given the P6's approximately 90 dB SINAD outcome.

The W4's DSP/EQ firmware and the E7's modular board architecture represent more rational approaches, but they do not define the company's flagship positioning.

## Advice

The W2 (299 USD) delivers independently confirmed excellent measured performance; prospective buyers should note that the TRN Black Pearl at 36.99 USD achieves a confirmed SNR of 130 dB balanced with 10-band parametric EQ at approximately one-eighth the cost. The W4 (449 USD) adds an OLED display, hardware EQ presets, and SPDIF output but awaits independent performance verification. Buyers evaluating the P6 Pro, LP6, or LP7 should consider that the predecessor P6 measured approximately 90 dB SINAD under third-party testing — substantially below the performance routinely achieved by current delta-sigma alternatives at a small fraction of the asking price — and that the P6 Pro's substantially improved manufacturer claims remain unconfirmed by any independent lab. LP OS's streaming service limitations place the standalone DAP lineup at a functional disadvantage relative to Android-based alternatives including the Shanling M3 Plus at 469 USD, which provisionally exceeds P6 Pro published specifications in every measured metric. The WP2 USB-C cable carries no technical basis for its 303–379 USD premium.
