## Overview

IsoAcoustics is a Canadian audio isolation company founded in 2012 by Dave Morrison, specializing in vibration isolation products for professional and consumer audio markets. Morrison's 20+ years of experience in radio and television studio construction at the Canadian Broadcasting Corporation forms the technical foundation for the company's patented isolation technology. Based in Markham, Ontario, IsoAcoustics has achieved global distribution in over 60 countries, offering product lines including the GAIA speaker isolation series, OREA component isolation feet, Aperta adjustable stands, and ISO-PUCK professional isolators.

## Scientific Validity

$$ \Large \text{0.8} $$

IsoAcoustics products demonstrate scientifically measurable improvements through credible third-party testing at the National Research Council of Canada. Laser vibrometer testing confirms significant vibration transmission reduction compared to traditional spike coupling methods [1]. Independent testing documented a 17dB reduction in intermodulation distortion when comparing rigid coupling to GAIA III feet [2]. Frequency response measurements show no coloration across the full 20Hz-20kHz spectrum, confirming transparent performance without unwanted artifacts [1]. The measurement approach validates performance above the transparent level for isolation products, with quantified evidence supporting manufacturer claims of vibration control and energy management without compromising audio signal integrity.

## Technology Level

$$ \Large \text{0.9} $$

IsoAcoustics demonstrates sophisticated mechanical engineering through patented three-part isolation systems utilizing synthetic elastomer materials with specific durometer characteristics. The technology translates proven studio construction principles to consumer applications, providing technical differentiation requiring significant expertise to replicate. Multiple patents protect the isolation designs, establishing competitive advantage lasting several years. The company's approach represents innovative application of broadcast facility expertise to consumer audio markets, with sophisticated understanding of vibration physics and isolation engineering. While purely mechanical without digital integration, this approach appropriately serves the isolation function with clear innovation in solving specific engineering challenges of vibration isolation while maintaining stability and adjustability.

## Cost-Performance

$$ \Large \text{0.1} $$

While GAIA I employs a three-part mechanical system and TenderFoot uses silicone-based elastomer compounds, both achieve the fundamental isolation objective of vibration control without coloration.
